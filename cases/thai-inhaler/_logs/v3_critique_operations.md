# V3 Critique — Operations / Execution-Feasibility Lens

*Critique sub-agent viewpoint: fractional COO who has watched 50+ founders try to execute plans like this.*

---

## 1. Top Concern

**Phase 0 as written is a 4-workstream parallel sprint requiring an ops-mature founder, but the decisions log commits to a "solo or 2-person founder, no prior FDA-CPG experience." That gap is the single biggest execution-feasibility issue.** The roadmap (`07_90_day_roadmap.md`) treats Week 2 as if PO placement, over-sleeve sourcing, FDA counsel intake, and 18 fake-PDP creative variants are independent tasks a single human runs in parallel. They're not — they all sit on the same calendar, the same inbox, the same brain. A first-time CPG operator running this alone will hit a hard bandwidth ceiling somewhere around week 3 and the validation signal will be polluted by execution drift, not by demand truth. The plan is intellectually correct and operationally optimistic by roughly 2x.

---

## 2. Specific Findings

**a) Supplier-equity negotiation is wildly under-budgeted in time.**
Where: `04_deliverable_v3.md` §1 ("Email a candidate Thai supplier this week. Day-60 = signed LOI"). Why: cross-border equity deals with a Thai SME — through a language barrier, with no pre-existing relationship, with cap-table dilution as the offer — are not 60-day deals. Realistic founder time-cost: 40–80 hours of intro-hunting, translation, calls scheduled across a 12-hour time difference, plus US + Thai counsel review of the LOI structure before send. A solo founder also running Phase 0 will not get there in 60 days. Fix: Make Day-60 = "first qualified intro + verbal interest," Day-120 = LOI, Day-180 = executed agreement. Move Plan B activation trigger from day 60 to day 90.

**b) Amazon listing-from-scratch is treated as a Week 3 deliverable, not a 4-week project.**
Where: `07_90_day_roadmap.md` Week 3 ("PDPs live $14/$24/$36 Amazon+Shopify"). Why: A new Amazon Beauty listing for a non-Approved-brand-registered seller takes brand registry application (2–6 weeks for first-time TM holders), Beauty category gating, FBA shipment plan, ASIN parent/child setup, and A+ approval cycles. The plan implies the founder is already brand-registered — but the decisions log says "no IP filed." Founder will hit a multi-week wall they didn't budget for. Fix: Phase 0 should test on Shopify + paid traffic first; treat Amazon as a Phase 1 project. This also cuts Week 3 load roughly in half.

**c) FDA counsel intake assumes 1–2 hours of attorney time = 1–2 hours of founder time.**
Where: `02_phase0_validation.md` Test 3, `04_deliverable_v3.md` §4. Why: Attorney engagement at Greenberg Traurig or HPM has a 2–4 week intake (conflicts check, engagement letter, retainer wire, scoping call) before the substantive 1–2 hours starts. Founder time-cost is 8–12 hours of admin spread over a month, not "$2k buys it." If counsel intake starts week 2, the audit signal lands week 5–6 — past when copy revisions feed back into Phase 0. Fix: Send counsel-intake email simultaneously with the supplier email, week 1. Don't gate it on PO placement.

**d) Creator seeding ops are budgeted as money but not as labor.**
Where: `04_deliverable_v3.md` §4 ("Gift 30 creators"), `02_phase0_validation.md` Test 4. Why: 30 personalized handwritten cards + language card + supplier story + DM conversation + tracking pickup rate is 30–45 hours of founder labor, not a line item. And micro-creator response rates run 30–50% on cold DMs — to get 30 *gifted-and-shipped*, you DM 60–90. There is no way a solo founder runs this in parallel with PO logistics, fake-PDP creative, and supplier negotiation. Fix: Hire a 10-hour-a-week VA from week 1 ($600–1,000 from the $50k reserve); make creator outreach their primary owner.

**e) Reading and acting on Phase 0 results is unstaffed.**
Where: `07_90_day_roadmap.md` Week 6 ("Decision gate: Go/conditional/no-go"). Why: Synthesizing 4 parallel test outputs (private-label sales, fake-PDP elasticity, FDA audit, creator pickup) into a 4-signal AND decision is 20–40 hours of analysis the founder will do *while exhausted from weeks 1–5*. The plan offers no template, no dashboard, no pre-built decision worksheet. Fix: Build the decision-gate scoring sheet *in week 1* before the data arrives. Schedule a 2-day "analysis sprint" in week 6 with no other commitments. Pre-book a Codex/advisor review of the call.

---

## 3. What's Actually Right

- **The "send the email this week" framing is operationally correct.** Long-pole tasks with high external-latency (supplier outreach, counsel intake) belong on Day 1, before the founder has shipped anything else. The deliverable nails this.
- **The kill-switch list is execution-grade.** Pre-committed kill-switches with numerical triggers (`<1.0%`, `>8%`, day-60 LOI miss) are exactly how you protect a tired solo founder from sunk-cost reasoning at week 6. This is rare in plans this size.

---

## 4. Verdict

**Conditional pass.** The strategy is sound. The 6-week Phase 0 timeline is feasible only if (1) Amazon is dropped from Phase 0 and moved to Phase 1, (2) a part-time VA absorbs creator-seeding ops, (3) Day-60 LOI milestone is relaxed to "qualified intro + verbal interest," and (4) the founder pre-builds the decision-gate scoring sheet in week 1. Without those four changes, the plan asks one human to do two humans' work and the validation signal will be noise from the execution drift, not truth from the market. Ship the email — but ship the realistic schedule too.
