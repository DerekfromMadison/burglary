# v3 Critique — Marketing Operator Viewpoint

**Reviewer.** Senior marketing operator, CMO seat at a $20–100M indie wellness brand. Lens: would I sign this plan and put my team on it Monday?

---

## 1. Top concern

**The plan has no validated creative angle and no audience hypothesis — it has a wedge thesis dressed as a media plan.** Read §4 and §5 of the deliverable end-to-end and you can't answer the question every operator asks first: *what is the hook in the scroll?* "1-second daytime nervous-system reset / focus glimmer" is a positioning sentence, not an ad. The four-link bet (§01_strategic_wedge) treats "vocabulary holds" as a consumer-trend assumption, not as a creative deliverable. The Phase 0 "Fake-PDP creative — 18 variants" line in `07_90_day_roadmap` is the entire creative strategy. That's not a strategy; that's a media buy with no message. In a category where Apothékary, Saje, Vicks, and Plant Therapy are all variations on "calm in a stick," the message *is* the moat for the first 12 months — not the supplier-equity story (which is 2026 narrative work, not 2026 paid-acquisition fuel). Without 3 named hook hypotheses tied to 3 named audience segments before money goes live, you're A/B testing your way to a $14k Amazon spend on a positioning you haven't proven anyone repeats back to you.

---

## 2. Specific findings

**a) CAC assumptions are below current category reality.** §05_first_50k_allocation projects Amazon CAC $13–18 and creator CAC $18–30; §04_deliverable_v3 §4 sets blended CAC <$20 as strong-go. Current 2026 benchmarks: Amazon Sponsored Products CPC is $1.18–1.25, vitamin/supplement keywords already sit at $1.30+, Meta beauty/health CAC is ~$34, and DTC blended CAC for indie wellness runs $45–70. To hit <$20 blended on a $14 SKU you need either (i) a creator-organic hit you cannot underwrite or (ii) a subscription LTV you haven't yet validated. **Fix.** Re-run the gate at CAC <$32 strong-go / $32–45 conditional / >$45 kill, and explicitly underwrite the math against a $24 2-pack subscription LTV — single-unit at $14 will not close at honest CAC.

**b) Channel sequencing doesn't compound — it sprays.** §05 fires Amazon ($14k), creators ($9k), Erewhon ($6k), boutiques ($2.5k), Meta ($5k), brand shoot ($4k), PR ($2.5k) inside a single 90-day window (§07). Each channel has a different feedback loop (Amazon = 21–45d to read PPC, Erewhon = 60–90d to read sell-through, PR = 90–180d to land). On $50k there is no channel where you spend enough to get a clean read, and nothing handed off downstream — Amazon reviews don't fuel Erewhon pitch, Erewhon doesn't fuel PR, PR doesn't fuel creator inbound. **Fix.** Sequence: weeks 1–4 creator seeding + organic founder content (free/$3k); weeks 4–10 Amazon-only paid push using validated hooks; weeks 10–14 PR + Erewhon using Amazon review velocity as social proof. One channel feeding the next, not seven channels in parallel.

**c) Creator strategy is a budget line, not a strategy.** §05 line 3 is "50 micro × $180, 5 macro gifted, 1–2 paid × $1,500" with KPI "30+ UGC, 5+ >50K views." Paying micros $180 each in nervous-system + Thai-American is a 2024 playbook and the cohort is now picked-over and sponsorship-fatigued. Hard-exclude list (Young Living/doTERRA/Hong Thai) is correct but not sufficient. Missing entirely: a **whitelisted creative** plan (paid spend behind organic creator posts via Meta Partnership Ads), which is the only creator-CAC structure that currently clears in beauty/wellness. **Fix.** Replace 50 × $180 with 8–12 paid-whitelist deals at $400–800 each + media spend behind them; that's where creator CAC actually lands at $25–35.

**d) Founder narrative is asserted, not produced.** §04_deliverable_v3 §5 says "Founder narrative on day one. Named Thai supplier with photo, public producer-pricing disclosure, founder origin essay." Per `decisions.md`, the committed founder profile is **non-Thai US-based or Thai-American without an existing supplier relationship.** The founder origin essay therefore has nothing to disclose at week 1 — there is no origin. The whole equity-supplier playbook (§01, §03_founder_bifurcation) is *what makes the founder narrative real*, and §07 doesn't sign LOI until day 60 and doesn't execute until day 120. The narrative is therefore impossible at Phase 1 launch (week 7) under the current timeline. **Fix.** Either delay paid Phase 1 to month 5 (post-supplier-execution) or admit Phase 1 leads on a *product-led* hook (sensory-ritual, not heritage) and reserve heritage narrative for month 5+ relaunch. Pretending otherwise will read as performative-Asian to the exact creators you need.

**e) PR allocation is magical thinking.** §05 line 8: $2.5k for 80 mailers targeting Cut, Goop, Cup of Jo, Allure, Well+Good, Strategist with KPI "2–4 Q1 placements; Strategist is highest-leverage." Strategist alone receives ~200 unsolicited wellness-product pitches/week. Without a publicist ($5k/mo retainer is explicitly excluded as a "sub-$250k sink") an unknown founder mailing 80 cold packages lands ~0–1 placement, not 2–4. **Fix.** Cut PR to $1k (10 hand-picked sends, founder-to-editor warm-intro path only via paid PR sourcing platform like Qwoted) and reallocate $1.5k into creator whitelist budget.

---

## 3. What's actually right

- **Phase 0 cheap-validation gate before custom inventory is correct and most plans skip it.** The 4-of-4 metric AND-gate (§4 of v3, recalibrated to above-median) is the single most important discipline in this document. Real CMOs lose this argument with founders constantly; codifying it is operator gold.
- **The "no chain retail Y1, no Sephora, no podcast" exclusions** in §05 ("Absent — Intentional") are operationally honest. Most $50k plans waste $15–25k chasing one of those three. Ruling them out in writing is the difference between a plan and a wishlist.

---

## 4. Verdict

**Conditional — do not execute as written.** Strategically intact, operationally underspecified. The validation discipline is best-in-class; the creative-and-channel layer is generic. Before press-go: produce 3 named hook-by-audience hypotheses (not 18 PDP variants), re-baseline CAC gates to 2026 reality (~$32 strong-go, not $20), serialize the channel stack instead of parallelizing it, swap micro-payment creator spray for 8–12 paid-whitelist deals, and either delay Phase 1 by 8 weeks or explicitly de-couple the Phase 1 launch hook from the founder-heritage narrative until the supplier deal is signed. Approve Phase 0 as-is; rewrite Phase 1 before a dollar moves.
