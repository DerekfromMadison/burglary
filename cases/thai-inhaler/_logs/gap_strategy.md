# Strategic Coherence Gap Analysis — thai-inhaler v5 + sub-docs 11-14, 21, 23

**Lens**: Does the plan hang together as a strategy? Are segment, SKU, messaging, price, and channels mutually reinforcing, or pointing different directions?

---

## 1. Top 3 strategic gaps

### Gap 1: The differentiation cores are non-overlapping across docs — there is no single wedge.

The v5 sentence sells *"$12 single tube, paper sleeve, OEM blank-label, no scent variants"* as the wedge. The differentiation doc (`14_differentiation.md` §4) names the moat as a three-leg stack: **named Thai supplier with 15–25% cap-table equity + JTBD ownership + multi-format starter kit ($36)**. The SKU doc (`12_beachhead_sku.md`) explicitly forbids the multi-format starter kit ("No 2-pack. No starter set. No subscription"). The messaging doc (`13_core_messaging.md` §5) hard-anchors the Wong family in Chiang Rai, "since the 70s, 4× FOB, named on the label, equity not just royalty" — but the v5 sourcing strategy is explicitly **OEM blank-label, stock formula, no supplier exclusivity**, with supplier-equity demoted to "Y2 work." The customer-facing pitch is leaning on assets the operational plan is explicitly not building.
**Fix**: Either (a) collapse the differentiation to what v5 actually ships (format + price + JTBD framing), strip the Wong-family claims from `13_core_messaging.md` until a real supplier is on the cap table — or (b) keep the supplier narrative and pull supplier-equity work into the 90-day plan. Right now the brand is writing checks the supply chain doesn't cash.

### Gap 2: Price ladder fights with itself — the docs disagree by 100% on the unit economics.

`12_beachhead_sku.md` says **$12 single, no bundle**. `14_differentiation.md` says the moat includes a **$36 starter set**. `21_speed_to_100.md` plans for **$14 single / $24 2-pack / $36 starter** as the launch SKU stack. `23_funnel_competitor.md` says "default the $24 2-pack on the buy-button" with AOV target $22 and a $36 free-shipping threshold. The v5 cover doc lists $12 — but the AOV ≥$22 condition in the paid-ads gate (v5 weeks 8–12) is **mathematically impossible at a $12 single SKU with no bundle**. The Sean Ellis test fires at "Day 21 post-purchase" assuming a 2-pack/reorder rhythm that the SKU doc forbids.
**Fix**: Pick a price architecture and propagate it. If $12 single is truly the launch (per `12_beachhead_sku.md`), then drop AOV $22 from the paid-ads gate, drop the "$36 starter set" leg from the differentiation moat, and restate the day-21 reorder logic as a 30–45-day repeat instead of a same-cohort upsell.

### Gap 3: The IRL beachhead and the digital beachhead are different segments wearing the same name.

`11_beachhead_segment.md` defines the segment as **post-Pilates / post-reformer women 28–42** with the JTBD = *post-class parasympathetic-crash reset*. `13_core_messaging.md` defines the primary persona as **Maya, the Nervous-System Regulator**, with the JTBD = *3pm Slack-thread reset / between-meeting moment*. `14_differentiation.md` JTBD = *"the focus dip between meetings"*. `23_funnel_competitor.md` segment = "32-year-old wellness customer, 28-year-old male productivity-stack guy, 45-year-old yoga teacher equally." These are four different people with four different triggers in four different physical contexts (studio lobby, desk, meeting hallway, sample table). The Pilates lobby and the 3pm Slack thread are not the same buyer at the same moment, and a 60-second TikTok cannot serve both without diluting the wedge.
**Fix**: Pick one — *post-class reset* OR *between-meeting reset* — and force every channel into it for 90 days. The IRL playbook strongly implies post-class; the messaging and funnel docs imply desk/meeting. If you keep both, you are running two beachheads with one $50k budget, and neither will compound.

## 2. Contradictions surfaced

- **Supplier-equity contradiction**: `13_core_messaging.md` instructs founder to publish a LinkedIn post stating "We're building it with the Wong family in Chiang Rai... 4× FOB, named on the label, equity not just royalty." `12_beachhead_sku.md` instructs founder to launch on **OEM blank-label, stock formula, no supplier modification, no exclusivity**. The published founder post is therefore false at launch.
- **Bundle contradiction**: `14_differentiation.md` §4 names the **multi-format starter set ($36) as one of three legs of the 12-month moat**. `12_beachhead_sku.md` says **no starter set, no bundle, no subscription** for first 90 days. One of these is wrong.
- **Discovery-channel contradiction**: `11_beachhead_segment.md` claims "TikTok-only attempts are precisely where saturated-aromatherapy brands die" and pushes IRL hand-sell as the cheapest credible distribution. The v5 channel allocation puts **$3,200 on TikTok creators vs. $4,000 IRL** — nearly equal — and `23_funnel_competitor.md` makes "TikTok organic + Reddit" the primary discovery channel with IRL as a secondary surface. The segment doc says TikTok is where they die; the funnel doc says it's where they live.
- **Sean Ellis trigger contradiction**: v5 says fire at "Day 21 of every customer cohort." `21_speed_to_100.md` says fire when "customer has used the product for ≥14 days AND completed ≥1 reorder OR finished the 2-pack." With a $12 single-SKU launch and no 2-pack, the reorder gate cannot trigger by day 21 — the survey will fire at the wrong moment.
- **Customer count contradiction**: v5 = "100 by Day 90." `21_speed_to_100.md` headline = "100 by Day 60, stretch 120 by Day 90." Same project, two different north stars.

## 3. Unstated assumptions (ranked by how-fatal-if-wrong)

1. **(MOST FATAL)** That a $12 single tube can sustain the entire customer-acquisition stack. If true CAC is $48 blended (per v5) and contribution at $12 DTC is $8.85, the customer must reorder ~5.5× before LTV clears CAC. With no bundle, no subscription, and a habit-formation curve that hasn't been validated, breakeven is 6+ months out and the $50k budget runs dry first. **If wrong: the entire bootstrap envelope is upside-down.**
2. That the JTBD ("1-second focus reset") is a real recurring moment a customer will pay $12 to serve, not a writer's construct. Zero docs cite a *single* qualitative interview confirming this is the actual trigger — the JTBD is theory, not finding. **If wrong: every TikTok script and PDP headline misses.**
3. That a non-Thai solo founder can credibly publish "Wong family in Chiang Rai, 4× FOB, equity on cap table" without (a) the supplier signed and (b) the founder-bifurcation work resolved. The messaging doc treats this as a launch asset; the operational docs treat it as Y2 work. **If wrong: first viral post triggers an authenticity callout that becomes the brand story.**
4. That the Pilates studio owner will let a stranger sample inhalers to her members for free, repeatedly. The IRL plan assumes 3 of 6 yes; no doc cites a single warm intro, prior outreach, or completed conversation. **If wrong: the channel that's supposed to absorb 15+ customers and feed Sean Ellis returns zero.**
5. That Saje, Apothékary, and Vicks won't notice and counter-launch in 90 days. The differentiation doc claims a 12-month moat; the only enforcement mechanism is supplier-equity exclusivity, which is Y2. **If wrong: the moat is 90 days, not 12 months, and the brand is in a knife fight before PMF.**

## 4. Missing strategic moves

1. **No retention strategy beyond Sean Ellis.** Every doc treats retention as a measurement problem (day-30 repeat rate ≥15%), not a *design* problem. There is no email lifecycle plan, no community-asset-as-moat, no ritual-design hook (e.g., a daily-use cue, a refill mechanic, a cohort program), no reason this becomes a *habit* instead of a one-time curiosity buy. For a $12 sensory novelty, the gravitational pull is single-purchase.
2. **No community-as-moat thinking.** The moat doc (`14`) lists supplier equity + JTBD ownership + bundle. None of these is *owned audience*. Apothékary's actual moat is its 1700+ Whole Foods footprint and a half-million-strong newsletter — neither is in this plan. There's no founder-list-as-asset strategy beyond "LinkedIn is the moat" which is one writer's content cadence, not a defensible audience graph.
3. **No second-act / scent-extension strategy.** Single SKU is correct for Wave 1, but there is no roadmap for SKU 2 / SKU 3 / scent variants other than "ship if velocity clears 50 units/wk." Without an extension thesis, the brand is a one-product company by accident, not by design — and the LTV math fails.

---

## 5. Verdict — does the strategy cohere?

**Mostly no.** The five docs read like five smart consultants who didn't sit in the same room. Each doc internally is sharp, well-sourced, and well-reasoned — but the seams between them are ragged in ways that will cost the founder real money in the first 60 days. The biggest tells: (i) the price ladder disagrees by 100% across docs, (ii) the segment is named identically in two docs but described as four different humans across the funnel, (iii) the differentiation moat is a three-leg stack where two of the legs (supplier-equity, multi-format kit) are operationally explicitly *not* in the launch plan, and (iv) the Sean Ellis trigger and the launch SKU don't fit the same timeline. The wedge as written ("Vicks format, Apothékary intent, $12 to try") is genuinely good — but the supporting infrastructure is contradicting itself before the first unit ships. The fix is not more docs; it is one founder-led 2-hour session that picks ONE price architecture, ONE segment-context-trigger triple, and ONE moat that matches what the supply chain is actually building, then propagates those three picks through every doc until they say the same thing. Until that happens, every TikTok script, PDP headline, and creator brief is going to inherit the inconsistency and dilute the launch.
