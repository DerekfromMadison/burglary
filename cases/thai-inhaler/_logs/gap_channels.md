## 1. Channels MISSING from v5 (with rationale)

The v5 mix names 11 line items but is structurally **TikTok + Reddit + Amazon + LinkedIn + sampling**. Several discovery surfaces — many of them load-bearing for sensory-wellness brands — are absent.

1. **Instagram (organic + Reels + Stories)** — 22 explicitly *excludes* IG. This is wrong for Persona B (mom 28–42 in Williamsburg / 78704 / West LA) and the post-Pilates aesthetic cohort, who live on Reels carousels, story-shares, and Stories-driven product-saves. IG-Stories is also where studio partners (yoga instructors, reformer teachers) repost gifted product. Missing IG = forfeiting the studio-creator share-graph that 20_inperson generates. Reels can be the same vertical asset as TikTok at near-zero incremental cost.

2. **YouTube Shorts** — zero mention. Shorts is the under-leveraged 3rd vertical surface; the same TikTok asset carries with minimal edits, and the YT algorithm favors small accounts more aggressively than TikTok post-USDS. Founder-POV content compounds in YT search, which is the "ya dom" / "Thai inhaler" SEO surface 17 ignores.

3. **Pinterest** — completely absent. Pinterest is the dominant discovery engine for wellness, ritual, "desk aesthetic," "what's in my bag," "girl dinner," "morning routine," "post-Pilates" search behavior. Persona A and B literally pin these boards. Pins compound for 12–18 months (vs. TikTok's 72-hour decay) and drive saves directly into commerce intent. For a $12 sensory product with strong visual ritual asset, Pinterest is a structural miss.

4. **Email + SMS lifecycle (beyond a single Klaviyo welcome flow)** — 18_paid_ads name-drops Klaviyo welcome + cart-abandon, but there is no lifecycle architecture, no win-back, no replenishment, no VIP, no segmentation by city/persona. The 90-day plan delivers ~100 customers and then... no flow. This is a retention crater (see §5).

5. **Referral program** — totally missing. A $12 sensory product where the value prop literally includes "let a friend smell this" should have referral baked into the IRL keychain mini AND the post-purchase flow. Give-$5/Get-$5 mechanics are the lowest-CAC engine for wellness brands at this price point.

6. **Affiliate networks** — no ShareASale / Impact / Skimlinks / LTK strategy. Wirecutter / Strategist / Cup-of-Jo affiliate-link inclusions are the *mechanism* by which Tier-1 PR converts to revenue; 24_pr_earned ignores this layer. ASMR / fragrance Substack creators monetize via Skimlinks — without the brand on those networks, coverage doesn't pay.

7. **Subscription-box partnerships** — no FabFitFun, Birchbox-tier, BuzzFeed Goodful, TheraBox, Causebox/Alltrue mention. A 1,500-MOQ landed at $1.85/unit makes subscription-box seeding *trivially cheap* and puts product in 15k–100k curated mailboxes. This is the cheapest "sampling at scale" channel that exists.

8. **Corporate gifting / B2B sales** — zero. A $12 pocket object with strong unboxing is *purpose-built* for HR-team gifts, post-conference welcome bags, employee wellness budgets, founder-conference goodie bags. Caraway and Notebook Therapy both bootstrapped via corporate gifting Q4 spikes. Missing.

9. **Spa / clinic / acupuncturist distribution** — 16 mentions r/acupuncture creators in seed list, but there's no wholesale-to-clinic channel. Acupuncture clinics, naturopath offices, and nervous-system-coded therapy practices are point-of-trust retail surfaces where a $14 inhaler sells *because* the practitioner recommends it. Notebook to study: Shoreline Wild Salmon / Jiva Botanicals.

10. **Hotel amenity / mini-bar / boutique-hotel turn-down** — absent. The middle-seat-airplane and post-check-in rituals are literally Persona A's TikTok format. Standard hotel (Ace, Public, Pendry-tier) and wellness retreats (Miraval, Canyon Ranch) buy curated amenity stock at scale. Y2 play but should be on the roadmap.

11. **Gym / boutique-fitness partnership (beyond walking into studios)** — 20 covers indie studios but ignores the *systemic* play: Equinox, Barry's, SoulCycle, Solidcore, [solidcore]. These chains run formal vendor-partnership programs (Equinox's Beauty Bar, SoulCycle's pop-ups). Higher friction but each door = 200–800 high-LTV impressions/week.

12. **Podcast guest spots — separated from PR** — 22 has a "10-podcast ladder," 24 has "10 podcasts to pitch," but neither distinguishes between *guest-on-show* (founder voice) and *paid host-read* placement. Mid-roll host-reads on adjacent podcasts (Almost 30, The Cut, Pulling The Thread, Embodied) are a measurable acquisition channel separate from earned PR. Cost: $25–150 CPM, often barter-able with product.

13. **Event sponsorships** — 20 mentions Wanderlust + Mindbodygreen Revitalize as one-shot booth plays but doesn't treat them as a SPONSORSHIP channel (logo on goodie bag, included in welcome kit, panel placement). Different mechanic from "pay for booth, hand out samples."

14. **Livestream commerce — TikTok Shop Live, Instagram Live, Amazon Live** — 15 explicitly defers TikTok Shop affiliate to month 3+ but says nothing about *founder-hosted* livestream. Olipop, Jones Road, OUAI all use livestream commerce as a 5–15% revenue stream by year 2. Founder doing a 30-min "scent test" live with side-by-side Hong Thai is exactly the format the algorithm rewards in 2026.

15. **Threads / Bluesky / X organic** — 22 explicitly excludes all three. Defensible for X, but Threads' wellness/CPG-founder community is now the cheapest organic-discovery surface for this exact persona, and ignoring it forfeits the share-graph that flows from Substack readers.

16. **WhatsApp / community-DM / private-group channels** — 16 mentions Lex Fridman Discord but not the broader private-graph play: Geneva groups, Slack communities (Indie CPG, Startup CPG, Female Startup Club), Substack chat tabs. These convert at 5–10× public posts because trust is pre-built.

## 2. Channels UNDER-WEIGHTED in v5 (with re-allocation)

The 90-day allocation puts $5,000 into "Meta + TikTok paid" (10% of Y1 budget) and $3,200 into TikTok creators. The under-weights:

- **IRL sampling at $4,000 (8% of Y1)** is *under-weighted* given 20_inperson explicitly states this is the highest-ROI channel for sensory products. Should be **$7,000–9,000** with a second city added by month 4.
- **Amazon at $1,500 in 90 days** is fine, but the months-4–12 Amazon allocation of $4,000 is too low. With Vine + PPC + Brand Tailored Promotions + Subscribe & Save attached, Amazon should carry **$8,000–10,000** of the Y1 spend by month 12.
- **PR/earned at $300 (samples)** is materially under-weighted. The PR sample-kit budget alone for 30 writers + 10 podcast hosts is **$900–1,500**. Press kit production + Muck Rack subscription + 1 freelance pitcher (3 months retainer) = **$3,000–4,000** total.
- **SEO at $0 + $400 for one writer** — fine for the founder-writes phase, but pillar #1 needs link-building support. Allocate **$1,500–2,500** for guest-post outreach + Substack writer gifts (the link-building section of 17 implies but doesn't budget this).
- **Paid retargeting** — 18 acknowledges retargeting is "the only paid layer structurally profitable on order one" but doesn't carve a separate retargeting budget. Should be **$1,500/month always-on** by month 4 separate from prospecting.

**Proposed re-allocation of the existing $50k Y1**:

- Amazon: $10,000 (was $5,500)
- PR/earned: $3,500 (was $300)

- Pinterest/Instagram organic: $1,500 (was $0)
- Subscription-box seeding: $2,500 (was $0)
- Affiliate network setup + commissions: $1,500 (was $0)
- Reduce reserve from $11,400 → $5,400 to fund the above.

## 3. The single biggest miss — **Email/SMS lifecycle + Referral**

If I had to pick **one channel** to add that would 2× early traction: **a real lifecycle engine (Klaviyo flows + Postscript SMS + a referral mechanic baked into the IRL keychain mini and post-purchase email)**.

Reasons:
- v5 acquires ~100 customers in 90 days. Without lifecycle, those 100 customers convert to ~15 repeat orders organically. With proper lifecycle (welcome series → review request → 30-day replenishment → 60-day win-back → referral prompt), repeat rate doubles to ~30%. The Sean Ellis 40% test depends on a re-engagement mechanism the current plan lacks.
- Referral is effectively zero-CAC. A sensory product where "let a friend smell this" is the literal value prop has the highest natural referral coefficient in CPG. Magic Mind's referral loop drove 18% of Y1 revenue.
- SMS, specifically, has 3–4× the open rate of email for IRL-acquired (per 20_inperson) — which is exactly the segment the 90-day plan is building. The plan captures phone numbers but does nothing systematic with them.

A second-place answer would be **Pinterest**, on slow-compound grounds (12–18 months but free, evergreen, and buyer-intent-rich). But lifecycle is the higher-confidence 2× because it operates on customers you already paid CAC for.

## 4. Channel-mix architecture — should there be a ratio rule?

Yes. v5 has an allocation table but no architectural principle. Recommended: **60 / 30 / 10 trial / scaling / brand**, applied to *post-month-3* spend (the first 90 days are appropriately weighted to trial and infrastructure).

- **60% trial-generators** — IRL sampling, gifted creators, subscription-box seeding, founder-LinkedIn-driven free traffic, Reddit, SEO compound. These are the channels that produce the *first-purchase moment* at low to zero CAC.
- **30% scaling-amplifiers** — Meta/TikTok paid, Amazon PPC, retargeting, podcast host-reads, affiliate networks. These are turned on *after* the trial channels prove organic conversion.
- **10% brand / earned / moat** — PR, Substack writer relationships, founder long-form, supplier-story video. Doesn't move volume in the quarter; sets the table for year 2.

The current v5 mix tilts roughly 50/40/10, with the 40% scaling layer turning on too early relative to organic signal. The 60/30/10 architecture would defer ~$3,000 of Meta paid to retargeting + lifecycle.

## 5. Lifecycle / retention specifics MISSING

Concrete absent moves:

- **Welcome flow beyond a $5-off email**: 5-email Klaviyo series — (1) welcome + supplier story video, (2) "how to use it" 1-second-ritual GIF, (3) the FDA-language explainer (founder transparency POV), (4) UGC mosaic, (5) referral CTA. Currently zero of these specified.
- **Post-purchase day 5/12/30/60 SMS**: 20 specifies a 3-message sequence for IRL-acquired but nothing for online-acquired buyers.
- **Replenishment trigger at day 45**: 18 mentions in passing; no copy, no offer, no segmentation logic.
- **Review-request flow** (separate from Amazon's "Request a Review" button): Shopify-side automated email at day 14 + photo-review reward. Missing.
- **Referral mechanic**: zero. Should be ReferralCandy or Friendbuy + give-$5/get-$5 integrated into welcome flow + the IRL keychain mini's QR landing page. The 20_inperson keychain is currently a 20%-off discount engine — it should be a referral engine.
- **Subscription/Subscribe & Save attach flow**: 19 mentions S&S as a target metric (8% by day 90) but no flow drives the customer toward subscription post-purchase. Should be a day-21 "you've used it ~30 times — automate it for $9.60/refill" trigger.
- **VIP / repeat-customer segment**: customers buying 2+ orders should get an early-access SKU drop email and a private-Discord invite. Builds the founder-channel community 22 talks about but doesn't operationalize for actual buyers.
- **Win-back at 90 days + 180 days**: standard but absent. Two emails, $5 credit + one new scent if/when SKU2 ships.
- **Birthday / anniversary trigger**: small but consistent revenue lift. Ignored.
- **Quiz funnel** (Octane AI / Typeform → Klaviyo segmentation): "what's your 1-second reset moment?" → segments by use occasion → personalizes the welcome flow. This is the highest-conversion email-list-build mechanic for wellness brands in 2026; v5 has no list-build engine besides the 20% IRL popup.

## 6. Verdict — completeness rating

**6/10.**

Reasoning: v5 is *deep* in the channels it covers — TikTok, Reddit, Amazon, LinkedIn, IRL sampling all have genuinely strong sub-playbooks. But it leans heavily on a narrow stack of acquisition surfaces and almost completely ignores **retention infrastructure** (lifecycle, referral, subscription mechanics) and **second-tier discovery surfaces** (Instagram, Pinterest, YouTube Shorts, subscription-boxes, corporate gifting, livestream). For a sensory product where the LTV unlock lives in repeat purchase and word-of-mouth, this is a structural gap, not a polish gap.

Upgrade to **8/10** by adding (a) the email/SMS/referral lifecycle stack with concrete flows, (b) Pinterest + Instagram Reels as zero-cost asset multipliers from existing TikTok content, (c) subscription-box seeding as a $2,500 one-time sampling shot, and (d) an affiliate network registration as a prerequisite for PR conversion.

Upgrade to **9/10** by adding the Y2 channels (corporate gifting, hotel amenity, gym chain wholesale) to a roadmap with explicit gating triggers, and by separating "guest-on-podcast" (free) from "host-read sponsorship" (paid) as distinct channels with distinct budgets.

The plan is well-built for getting to 100 customers. It's not yet built for getting from 100 to 1,000, which is where these gaps bite.
