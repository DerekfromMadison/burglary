# V3 Critique — Logistics / Supply-Chain Lens

Reviewer viewpoint: 12+ years importing Asian wellness/CPG into US Amazon FBA and specialty. The brief is strategically literate but logistically thin. The cost stack, the regulatory classification, and the supplier-equity sequencing all break in ways the deliverable does not surface.

## 1. Top concern — The product is almost certainly a Category I OTC drug, not a cosmetic. Every cost, channel, and timeline assumption inherits this error.

A nasal inhaler containing **menthol + camphor delivered via inhalation route** sits squarely inside the FDA's *Cold, Cough, Allergy, Bronchodilator, and Antiasthmatic OTC Final Monograph* (21 CFR 341) as a topical/steam-inhalant nasal decongestant — Category I, with required Drug Facts panel, NDC labeling, FEI-registered manufacturer, and CGMP under 21 CFR 210/211. Poy-Sian is sold in Thailand under a *drug* (or "herbal product," per the 2019 Herbal Product Act) registration, not a cosmetic notification — that's why you'll never find it on a US Sephora shelf. The deliverable's MoCRA / cosmetic-counsel framing (`04_details/04_fda_compliance.md`, the $2k FDA audit in §4) is mis-scoped: MoCRA applies to cosmetics; this is a drug. Fix: before the supplier email goes out, get 2 hours of OTC-monograph counsel (Hyman Phelps, Kleinfeld Kaplan, Arnall Golden Gregory) to confirm path. If it is a drug, your $5k Phase 0 budget is gone before the first ad runs — NDC labeler code, Drug Listing in eDRLS, FEI for the Thai factory, Establishment Registration are non-trivial. Reframing as a "cosmetic aromatherapy stick" by removing camphor is the only path to keep cosmetic economics — and that is a *formulation* decision the supplier must agree to before MOQ is locked.

## 2. Specific findings

**Finding 1 — The 19% reciprocal tariff figure is stale; the regime changed twice in Q1 2026.** Where: implicit in any landed-cost math (`05_first_50k_allocation.md` $14k/9k inventory line). Why: SCOTUS struck down all IEEPA reciprocal tariffs on Feb 20, 2026 (6-3); effective Feb 24 they were terminated. Section 122 replaced them at 10% then 15% global, with a hard 150-day Trade Act sunset around July 24, 2026 unless Congress acts. Stacking: 15% Section 122 + base HTS 3304.99 column-1 (cosmetic) at 0% *or* the relevant drug HTS chapter 30 line + MPF (0.3464%, $32–$634 cap) + HMF (0.125%). If the inhaler is reclassified as an OTC drug (HTS 3004.90 area, MFN-free), the tariff picture gets *better*, not worse — but only if you've registered as a drug. Fix: stop quoting "19%." Build the model with three scenarios — pre-July sunset (15%), post-sunset Congressional outcome unknown, and drug-classified (0% MFN + Section 122 if still in force). Add a tariff line item to the kill-switch table.

**Finding 2 — The $0.50–$1.50 FOB unit cost is roughly right for goods but ignores the small-order surcharge stack.** Where: implicit assumption in `02_phase0_validation.md` ($1.20/unit Poy-Sian wholesale) and `05_first_50k_allocation.md` ($14k inventory for 9k units = $1.56/unit landed, which is cheaper than reality). Why: Alibaba/Thai By Nature wholesale at $1–1.20 only holds at FCL/multi-pallet quantities. At 500 units (Phase 0) the unit FOB after sample fees, MOQ premiums, and OEM tooling for the over-sleeve is closer to $2.50–4.00. KMP Innovation's published OEM range is 200–5,000 bottles for *bottle-format* products; menthol-stick OEM tooling (cap, plug, label registration) typically adds $800–2,500 setup. Real-world MOQ for a *custom-formulated* (not relabeled) inhaler from Gold Mints or comparable GMP factory is 3,000–10,000 units, not 500. Fix: split "private-label relabel of finished Poy-Sian" (achievable at 500 units, but you cannot change formulation) from "true OEM custom inhaler" (10k+ MOQ, 90-120 day lead time, $0.50–0.90 unit at scale). Phase 0 can only do the former. The supplier-equity ask in §1 implies the latter and is a different conversation.

**Finding 3 — Ocean freight + customs at Phase 0 volumes is air, not sea.** Where: not modeled anywhere in `05_first_50k_allocation.md`. Why: 500 inhalers at ~3 cm³ packaged = ~0.015 CBM. LCL minimum charge is 1 CBM regardless. 2026 Bangkok-LAX LCL all-in: $40–180/CBM ocean × 1 CBM minimum + origin CFS $15–40 + destination CFS $15–40 + docs $50–100 + customs clearance $150–250 + ISF $25 + bond $50–150 = **$350–800 landed for the box**, plus 7-day port wait + 3-5 day deconsolidation. For 500 units that's $0.70–1.60 per unit in freight alone — doubling the COGS the deliverable assumes. Air courier (DHL/FedEx) at this volume runs $4–6/kg, ~$60–90 total, **faster and cheaper for Phase 0**. Fix: Phase 0 uses air courier. Phase 1 at 9k units (~0.27 CBM) is still LCL-minimum-bound; only at 30k+ units does sea become rational. Add a logistics line: $200 Phase 0 air, $1,500 Phase 1 LCL.

**Finding 4 — Amazon FBA prep is the killer the deliverable doesn't mention.** Where: `05_first_50k_allocation.md` §2 assumes Amazon launch but doesn't budget prep. Why: Effective January 1, 2026, Amazon ended US-based FBA prep and unit labeling. Every unit must arrive fully prepped — FNSKU on each tube, poly-bag with suffocation warning (24-pt font, opening ≥5"), expiration-date labeling (mandatory for cosmetics/topicals), bundle wrapping for the 2-pack and 3-pack SKUs. Defect fees rose ~1,600% in 2025. A tube-format product cannot be loose-packed. Realistic prep cost via a 3PL like AGS, Stallion, or PrepVia: **$0.45–0.85/unit** at 500-unit volumes, $0.25–0.40 at 9k. For 9k units that is $2,250–3,600 not in the budget. Fix: add an FBA-prep 3PL line ($3,500 reserve) and bake $0.40/unit into landed-cost math. Also: Amazon Beauty requires ingredient-list compliance and (for OTC drugs) NDC + Drug Facts review — additional listing risk if Finding 1 isn't resolved first.

**Finding 5 — Thai-side export permit is undocumented and is the supplier-equity LOI's hidden critical path.** Where: `06_risks_and_kill_switches.md` Risk 2 ("supplier/tariff escalation") and §1 supplier-equity timeline (60-day LOI, 120-day agreement). Why: Thailand's 2019 Herbal Product Act requires the *manufacturer* to hold an active herbal-product license, the *exporter* to be a licensed exporter under Thai FDA, and the formula to be on file (5-year validity). For a co-developed/custom formulation under a US co-founder equity arrangement, the formula re-registration alone is 90–180 days at Thai FDA, plus Free Sale Certificate (~30 days) needed by US Customs and any specialty retailer. Day-60 LOI is realistic, but day-120 "executed agreement with shippable product" assumes an existing licensed formula. Fix: split the supplier ask into (a) relabel of existing licensed Poy-Sian/Hong Thai formula (fast, no Thai-FDA work) and (b) custom co-formulation (slow, 6-9 month Thai-FDA path before any US compliance work starts). Phase 0 must use (a). Phase 1 supplier-equity term sheet should commit to (b) with a 12-month formula-readiness milestone, not 120 days.

## 3. What's actually right

- **Phase 0 cheap-validation gating before custom inventory** is the correct sequencing. A founder who commits 10k-MOQ tooling money before validating $14 Poy-Sian conversion has bought a warehouse problem. This part of the deliverable is exactly right.
- **Channel sequencing — Amazon FBA → one Erewhon door → no chain retail Y1** matches the realistic logistics envelope. Whole Foods regional, Sprouts national, and CVS each require an EDI/3PL/pallet-label compliance program ($25–60k setup + $4–8k/mo) that a $50k bootstrap simply cannot fund. Saying no to chain retail in Y1 is the right call and saves the founder from a six-figure logistics build-out they don't have the volume to amortize.

## 4. Verdict

**Conditional fix-then-proceed.** The strategic logic survives, but the logistics layer needs a rebuild: confirm OTC-drug-vs-cosmetic classification before any other action (this changes everything downstream); replace the stale 19% tariff with the current Section 122 / SCOTUS landscape; add real numbers for FBA prep, LCL minimums, and Thai-FDA formula registration; and split the supplier-equity ask into "relabel" (Phase 0) vs "custom formula" (Phase 1+) tracks with honest 6-12 month timelines for the latter. None of these kills the deal. All of them are missing in v3, and any one of them eats the $50k envelope if hit cold. Add a "Logistics Reality" appendix and re-pin the kill switches.
