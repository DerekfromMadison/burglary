# v3 Critique — Brand Designer / Creative Director Lens

**Reviewer POV.** Sensory-brand creative direction (Aēsop, Le Labo, Boy Smells, Vitruvi, Ranger Station orbit). Reading v3 as someone who would have to actually *make* this brand exist on a shelf, in a hand, in a feed.

---

## 1. Top Concern

**The plan is operationally rigorous and brand-blind.** At a $24+ AOV in a category where Apothékary just raised $8M from Shiseido on the strength of a *visual system* (Forner Studios + Timia Lewis — ingredient-collage panels, custom jar architecture, East/West arch logo), v3 contains zero specification of what this brand actually looks, smells, sounds, or feels like. The word "packaging" appears mostly as a cost line ("custom over-sleeve, $5k"). There is no art-direction brief, no name, no typographic stance, no material decision (glass vs. anodized aluminum vs. compostable kraft), no color territory, no scent-narrative architecture, no founder-voice register. At Aēsop / Le Labo / Vitruvi price points, **the bottle is the brand and the brand is the moat** — and v3 is treating the bottle as a $5k commodity. The Phase 0 thresholds (>2.5% conversion at $14) are being asked to validate a product whose visual / tactile / olfactory craft has not been designed yet. You cannot A/B test a wedge before you've designed the wedge.

---

## 2. Specific Findings

### Finding 1 — No name, no naming territory, no voice (`04_deliverable_v3.md` §4, `01_strategic_wedge.md`)
**Where.** The deliverable refers to "the product," "the wedge," "Thai herbal inhaler" — never a brand name, never even a naming *territory* (single-word botanical à la Aēsop, place-name à la Le Labo, abstract-noun à la Boy Smells, founder-name à la Apothékary). No voice register specified — clinical? folk? poetic? deadpan?
**Why it matters.** Naming sets the entire visual vector. "Bangkok 7am" is a different brand than "Krathin" is a different brand than "Glimmer Co." is a different brand than "Hong Sawan." Each implies a different package, photographer, retail door, creator pool. You cannot brief a designer or test creative without it.
**Fix.** Add a Phase 0.5 (Week 1, ~$2k): name shortlist (5 candidates), USPTO knockout, .com sweep, Class 003 clearability. Designate naming territory before any custom over-sleeve goes to print.

### Finding 2 — "Custom over-sleeve" is a brand-killing artifact at $24 (`04_deliverable_v3.md` §4, Phase 0 line 1)
**Where.** "500 Poy-Sian units + custom over-sleeve" is the proposed test vehicle.
**Why it matters.** A sleeve over a Poy-Sian inhaler is *visibly* a sleeve over a Poy-Sian inhaler. Premium-wellness shoppers — the exact $24-AOV cohort the plan is targeting — are pattern-trained by Aēsop's amber glass, Le Labo's hand-typed apothecary labels, Vitruvi's matte ceramic geometry. A printed sleeve over a yellow plastic stick reads as either dropshipper, parody, or AliExpress relabel. The conversion test will under-read the wedge because the *artifact is wrong*, not because the wedge is wrong. You will kill a real wedge with fake packaging.
**Fix.** Either (a) add $4–8k for a *minimum-viable* form-factor — anodized aluminum tube, magnetic cap, screen-printed glyph (4–6 week turn from a contract decorator), or (b) frame Phase 0 explicitly as "demand signal under handicap" and de-rate the kill threshold accordingly (e.g. 0.8% at $14 = conditional, not kill).

### Finding 3 — Aesthetic territory is unchosen, and the three options are not interchangeable (`01_strategic_wedge.md` §The Wedge)
**Where.** "Sensory-allusion compliant language. Thai-heritage anchored." That's it.
**Why it matters.** Thai herbal aesthetics fork into three non-overlapping creative lanes:
- **Luxury-modernist** (Aēsop / Le Labo orbit): muted palette, architectural typography, Thai language used as abstract mark, single-color print, monk-stone tactility. Plays at Erewhon, Nordstrom, Goop. CAC-friendly to the white affluent core.
- **Folk-craft** (Faherty / Madewell-adjacent): hand-illustrated botanicals, warm cream + tamarind + jade palette, story-forward, market-stall referent. Plays Thai-American diaspora hard, plays white affluent as "discovery." Closer to current Apothékary's ingredient-collage move.
- **Apothecary-clinical** (Future Society / Selsun-meets-Aēsop): white + black + medicinal sans, lot numbers visible, dosage iconography, INCI on front. Reads "this works" in a category fighting for plausibility.
Picking the wrong one in a Phase 0 sleeve test mis-validates a real wedge. They are not three visual flavors; they are three different brands.
**Fix.** Force a creative-territory commitment in week 1 with a one-page positioning brief ("we are X; we are not Y; mood references: A, B, C"). Recommended: **luxury-modernist with one folk-craft accent** (e.g. monogram Thai glyph as the only ornament). It is the lane Apothékary did *not* take, where Aēsop has no SKU, and where the $24 ladder is supportable. Folk-craft puts you in Hong Thai's price tier; clinical kicks the FDA hornet.

### Finding 4 — The scent itself is undescribed (`04_deliverable_v3.md` entire doc, `04_details/04_fda_compliance.md` §Five Forever-Safe Phrases)
**Where.** "An uplifting Thai herbal aroma." That's the entire scent narrative in v3.
**Why it matters.** The FDA-compliance doc (correctly) tells you to *describe the smell, not the feeling* — and then no one describes the smell. At Aēsop's price point the scent brief is novelistic: top notes / heart / dry-down / temperature / season / room. Le Labo names the city the perfumer was thinking about. Boy Smells writes one-line poems per SKU. The Phase 0 ad creative test cannot generate three converting variants if the scent is "Thai herbal aroma." Camphor-forward? Borneol-bright? Kaffir lime + galangal + black cardamom? Cool-eucalyptus or warm-clove? **Scent vocabulary is the only legal premium lever you have**, per your own compliance doc — and v3 does not exercise it.
**Fix.** Add a 1-page scent narrative to Phase 0 inputs: 7–9 ingredient notes named, top/heart/base structure (yes, even for inhalers — the unboxing-to-90-second experience has an arc), one olfactory reference brand for the perfumer to work against, one written paragraph ("morning monsoon, palace garden, cold marble") that goes verbatim to the copywriter and the photographer.

### Finding 5 — Voice in the LinkedIn post is consultant; voice for the brand is unwritten (`06_linkedin_v3.md`, all)
**Where.** The LinkedIn voice is fine *for the consultant*. There is no founder-voice or brand-voice sample anywhere in v3.
**Why it matters.** "Founder origin essay" is named as a launch deliverable (`04_deliverable_v3.md` §5) but no register is specified. Apothékary's founder voice is calm-clinical-personal. Boy Smells is queer-cosmopolitan-deadpan. Le Labo is provocateur-curator. Without a voice prototype, the founder essay becomes generic-wellness-Substack on launch day, which collapses the entire heritage-narrative moat the strategy depends on.
**Fix.** Add a 200-word voice sample to `04_details/` — one PDP paragraph, one IG caption, one cold-email line — written in the chosen voice. This is a 30-minute artifact that disambiguates everything downstream.

---

## 3. What's Actually Right

- **The FDA verb-object rule is a genuine creative gift, not just a legal constraint.** Forcing scent-and-ritual language *over* benefit-and-body language pushes the brand toward Aēsop / Le Labo's actual playbook — describe the object, not the outcome — which is exactly the right register for the price point. v3 has stumbled into the right voice constraint via legal necessity. Lean in harder.
- **"Heritage moat > format moat"** (`01_strategic_wedge.md` §Strategic Implication) is correct creative-direction instinct. Supplier-named, photographed, producer-pricing-disclosed is the Le Labo "we put the perfumer's name on the label" move, applied to a Thai grower. It's a real story. The plan correctly identifies it; it just doesn't dress it.

---

## 4. Verdict

**Conditional reject as a brand artifact; conditional approve as an ops plan.** v3 is a competent operator's playbook and a designer's empty room. Phase 0 will run, will produce numbers, and the numbers will be unreadable because the artifact under test is undesigned. Before pressing send on the supplier email, spend one week and ~$3–5k on (1) name + naming territory, (2) a one-page creative-territory commitment (recommended: luxury-modernist), (3) a 9-note scent brief, (4) a minimum-viable form factor that is not a sleeve over a Poy-Sian, (5) a 200-word voice sample. None of that delays the supplier outreach. All of it determines whether your $5–15k test measures the wedge or measures bad packaging.
