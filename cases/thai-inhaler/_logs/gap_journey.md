# Gap Analysis — Customer Journey Lens (thai-inhaler v5)

**Author**: Customer-journey gap-finder sub-agent
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Lens**: First-person buyer walking the funnel from cold awareness to advocacy. Where does the journey work? Where does it break?

---

## 1. Journey-stage-by-stage gap analysis

### Stage 1 — Awareness (cold first encounter)
**What's planned**: Three discovery surfaces — TikTok organic (founder POV + ASMR creators), r/aromatherapy long-form, and IRL studio sampling. The 5-second elevator line is *"Thai herbal nasal stick — Vicks format, Apothékary intent."*
**Gaps**:
- **No memorable visual hook.** The plan names "paper sleeve" but never specifies a color, motif, or silhouette that will survive a 1.5-second TikTok scroll. Hong Thai's green tube is the entire reason Lisa's 300M-baht arc happened — color *is* the brand. v5 has no equivalent.
- **The 5-second line is for sample-table use, not the For You Page.** TikTok awareness needs a hook that reads in motion (B-roll, gesture, sound). "Vicks format, Apothékary intent" is a positioning sentence for a cofounder Slack, not a creator caption.
- **No sonic identity.** ASMR is mentioned as a content type but no signature sound (the *click* of an unscrew, a specific exhale, a Thai phrase) is committed to. Sensory categories that win own a sound (Coke fizz, Snapple cap pop). The cap unscrew is right there and unclaimed.

### Stage 2 — Consideration (second exposure)
**What's planned**: PDP + founder LinkedIn + side-by-side comparison strip. Target ≥6% ATC, >90s time-on-PDP.
**Gaps**:
- **Second-touch is lottery-driven.** The plan assumes a TikTok viewer will (a) remember the brand name, (b) screenshot, (c) search Amazon or DTC days later. With no committed brand color, no sonic mark, and a still-unnamed brand, the customer drops between touch 1 and touch 2. Maya's persona explicitly screenshots — but screenshots a *green tube*, not a paper sleeve.
- **Founder LinkedIn ≠ buyer's second touch.** Maya is on TikTok and Lemon8. Jen is on Instagram Reels and group chats. LinkedIn reaches recruiters, suppliers, and reporters — useful for the supply-side ladder, not for the buyer's second exposure. The plan conflates "founder credibility channel" with "consumer consideration channel."
- **No comparison content yet exists.** The "Thai inhaler vs Vicks" SEO page is a 90–120 day asset, but the second-exposure window is hours-to-days. A short-form comparison Reel ("$4 Poy-Sian vs $12 ours, here's the difference") is the missing artifact.

### Stage 3 — Trial (sample-to-purchase)
**What's planned**: Tethered counter-sniffer + 1ml take-home keychain mini + QR code → SMS opt-in → 20% off code (14-day expiry). Target 4–8% scan-to-purchase.
**Gaps**:
- **The 24-hour gap between sniff and second-sniff is the whole conversion mechanism — and it is undefended.** The mini hangs on a keyring. There's no committed cue *when* she should re-sniff. "Try it tomorrow at 3pm" is in the playbook but never lands on the sleeve, the SMS, or the QR landing page.
- **QR friction.** The mobile landing page collects phone number for SMS plus optional email plus venue dropdown plus optional referral name. That's four fields between a tethered sniffer and a 20% code. Drop-off rule of thumb: 30%+ per field after the first.
- **No price ladder at trial.** Customer scans, lands on a $12 SKU with a 20% code = $9.60. She's already at the trial price; there's no "add the 2-pack and unlock" mechanism, no $0 first-bottle-pay-shipping option (the trial-pack-style move that 7-Eleven yadom $1 anchoring would justify).

### Stage 4 — First purchase / unboxing
**What's planned**: $12 single, $24 2-pack defaulted, paper sleeve via Sticker Mule, Shopify or Amazon fulfillment.
**Gaps**: This is the largest single hole in v5. There is **no specified unboxing experience**. No insert card, no founder note, no ritual instruction, no scent-introduction sequence, no "what to do in the first 90 seconds" guide. The customer opens an envelope, sees a sleeve, twists a tube, smells it, and decides on the spot whether this is jewelry or a Vicks-with-a-markup. v5 leaves that single most consequential moment to a contract manufacturer's default packaging.

### Stage 5 — Habit formation
**What's planned**: "1-second daytime focus reset" framing. SMS day 3 ("how to use it"), day 10 ("rate it"), day 21 ("subscribe & save").
**Gaps**:
- **No cue.** B.J. Fogg's habit loop is *cue → action → reward*. The plan has the action (inhale) and an implied reward (the bright minute). The cue is missing. "3pm" is named in copy but not engineered into the customer's environment. There is no calendar reminder, no Slack-status hook, no companion-app nudge, no notification, no "stick it on your laptop bezel" sticker.
- **No streak / log mechanism.** Magic Mind, Apothékary, even Liquid Death use streaks/badges/customer counts. Inhaler has no count, no log, no public visible artifact of "I did this 14 days in a row."
- **The product is fungible at habit-formation moment.** If she has it in her purse but a colleague offers Vicks, why hers? Because of a cue she has built. Without one, churn is silent — she doesn't unsubscribe, she just stops opening her bag.

### Stage 6 — Repeat purchase
**What's planned**: 30-day repeat target 15% (later 25–30%). SMS subscribe & save 15% at day 21. Subscription attach 8% by month 6.
**Gaps**:
- **No depletion signal.** A nasal stick lasts 4–8 weeks of daily use depending on cap discipline; the plan never measures or communicates that to the customer. "When do I need a new one?" is an unanswered question. Apothékary's tincture is self-evidently depleting (water-glass volume drops). This product is opaque — the customer only knows it's dying when it stops smelling.
- **Subscription pitched too early.** Day 21 is before most customers have built a habit. Subscribe & save at day 21 reads as "the brand wants the wallet locked before the product earns it." Better: pitch subscription at day 45 *after* a depletion-stage email ("most people are halfway through by now").
- **No SMS depletion-trigger flow.** The plan has time-based SMS (day 3, day 10, day 21). It needs a usage-based one ("you bought this 50 days ago — most refill now").
- **No 2-pack reorder logic.** The default 2-pack means the customer has a backup; she'll reorder later, not sooner. Repeat-purchase math gets worse, not better, with the 2-pack default — yet the plan still targets 15% at 30 days. The targets and the SKU strategy contradict each other.

### Stage 7 — Advocacy
**What's planned**: Referral $5/$5, UGC repost, customer-story long-form.
**Gaps**:
- **No share-trigger.** "Smells stronger than Vicks. Designed like jewelry" is the share-line in the messaging doc — but where does it appear in the customer's life such that she repeats it? Not on the sleeve. Not on the insert (no insert exists). Not on the SMS. The phrase is engineered to spread and then never deployed.
- **Gift mechanic is missing.** Persona C explicitly buys two and gifts as a "soft teach-in." No gift-message field, no dual-shipping, no "buy one, gift one" SKU. The most loyal buyer's natural advocacy behavior has zero product support.
- **No earnable customer artifact.** No badge, no jewelry-grade keychain that says "founding 100," no holdable thing that signals "I was early." Olipop, Liquid Death, Recess all manufactured collectibles for first-100 buyers. This plan has not.

## 2. Top 3 friction points (where buyers drop)

1. **The screenshot-to-search gap (Stage 1 → 2).** TikTok viewer screenshots, never returns. **Fix**: commit to a single visible color (a saturated terracotta or jade that survives compression), put the brand name + URL on the cap (not just the sleeve), and run a "comment 'minute' for the link" CTA on every founder TikTok — Persona A's behavior is to comment-then-DM, not to search.

2. **The QR landing-page form (Stage 3).** Four fields between sniff and code. **Fix**: collapse to one — phone number only, auto-apply 20% code on the next page, capture venue via UTM not user input. Add email later in the SMS flow ("reply with your email for restock alerts").

3. **The day-21 subscription pitch (Stage 5 → 6).** Subscription before habit. **Fix**: replace day-21 subscribe-and-save with a day-21 *check-in* SMS asking "which moment is it for you?" (free-text reply that becomes future creator content), and move the subscribe pitch to day 45 anchored on depletion ("you're halfway through — refill now and save 15%").

## 3. The unboxing experience (currently undefined)

The mailer should do five things in 30 seconds:

1. **A black wax-sealed kraft sleeve** (not glossy — kraft signals craft, wax signals ritual). Sleeve copy: only the wordmark and the line *"open me at 3pm."*
2. **Inside, a single hand-numbered card** ("you are #847 of the first 1,000"), with a 1-sentence founder note in a real handwritten typeface (not a script font). Names the supplier on the back: "Wong family, Chiang Rai, since the 70s."
3. **A 90-second ritual card** — three steps, illustrated: (a) twist, (b) inhale once through each nostril, (c) name the next minute out loud. The phrase "the next minute brighter" lands here, not in an Instagram caption.
4. **A single-use sticker** the size of a laptop-camera cover — same color as the tube — labeled with the time-of-day cue ("3pm"). The customer is invited to put it on her laptop, water bottle, or planner. This is the missing habit cue, manufactured into the unboxing.
5. **A second tube in a tiny envelope** addressed to *"someone who needs this more than you do"*, with a postage-paid return-address sticker if she wants to send it. The gift mechanic — built into the product, not a referral page she'll never visit.

Total cost: ~$1.80–2.40 per unbox (sleeve $0.45, card $0.20, ritual card $0.30, sticker $0.15, envelope + tube $0.70–1.30). Lands at ~15–20% of unit revenue — the unboxing is the single highest-leverage marketing dollar in the entire plan.

## 4. Habit-formation audit

**Current state**: action defined (inhale), reward implied (bright minute), cue absent.

**What it needs**: a cue the customer cannot ignore. Three options, ranked:

1. **Environmental cue (recommended)**: the 3pm sticker from §3 above. She places it on her laptop bezel; every glance at her own keyboard becomes a cue. Zero ongoing brand cost, customer-installed, infinite duration.
2. **Time-bound digital cue**: a free iOS/Android shortcut the brand publishes — "thai-inhaler 3pm reset" — that lives in her notification tray. Ships day 1, costs $0 to maintain.
3. **Social cue**: a daily 3pm Instagram Story from the brand account. "Bright minute check-in." Customer screenshots and replies. Earns repeat exposure and creates a public ritual she can watch others perform.

**Reward layer**: name the minute. The day-21 SMS asks "which minute did this make brighter?" — answers become anonymized customer copy ("the meeting where I almost cried, the nursing-pump break, the airport security line"). The reward is being seen; the brand-side reward is permanent free creative. Apothékary already does this with its "what did you sip on" prompt; it works.

## 5. Repeat-purchase mechanic

**Current state**: time-based SMS at day 3, 10, 21. Subscribe & save 15%. Targets 15% at 30 days.

**Proposed mechanic** (replacing the above):

- **Day 3**: ritual confirmation SMS — "did you find your 3pm yet?" (free-text reply, no link, no offer).
- **Day 14**: photo-moment SMS — "tag us with a photo of where it lives." (UGC harvest, zero discount.)
- **Day 30**: the Sean Ellis test — already in v5, keep it.
- **Day 45 (depletion-anchored)**: "most people are halfway through. refill now, ship before you run out, 15% off the 2-pack." This is the first money-asking SMS.
- **Day 60**: gifting prompt — "the second tube was meant to leave your house. send it free with this code." Activates the dormant gifting mechanic from §3.
- **Day 75 (depletion + churn save)**: out-of-stock simulation — "we're sold out of single tubes for the next 10 days. reserve a 2-pack now to skip the wait." Manufactured scarcity, only deployed if 30-day repeat is below target.

This mechanic targets 25%+ repeat at 60 days (vs. the v5 target of 15% at 30) by trading earlier asks for habit-anchored ones. The math: a customer who has named her ritual and given a photo at day 14 has a sunk-cost relationship with the brand by day 45 that no time-based subscribe-and-save SMS can match.

## 6. Verdict

**Does the journey work?** Stages 1, 2, 3, and 6 are designed but porous. Stages 4, 5, and 7 are conceptually present but operationally undefined.

**The journey breaks at Stage 4 → 5.** The product arrives, gets twisted open, smells nice — and then the customer is alone with it. There is no engineered cue, no ritual instruction, no public artifact, no shareable phrase pre-loaded into her mouth. v5 ships a great product into a vacuum and hopes a habit forms organically. Habits don't form organically; they're scaffolded.

**The two highest-leverage fixes**: (a) commit ~$2 per unit to the unboxing spec in §3 above — that's where the wow, the cue, and the gift mechanic compound, and (b) replace time-based SMS with depletion-and-ritual-anchored SMS per §5. Both are sub-$5k incremental investments inside the existing $50k Y1 budget. Both convert the journey from a string of disconnected touchpoints into a closed loop.

Without these, the plan is a marketing strategy attached to a product. With them, it's a brand worth referring.
