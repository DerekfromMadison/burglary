# Scope — thai-inhaler

## Problem statement
Founder is preparing to launch an aromatherapy brand in the United States built around Thai herbal inhalers — a culturally-rooted product format (commonly tube-style nasal inhalers loaded with menthol, camphor, eucalyptus, borneol, and traditional Thai herbs; "ya dom" in Thai). The deliverable they want is a deep market research document on the US aromatherapy & wellness landscape with the practical purpose of identifying marketing approaches that meaningfully differentiate this brand from incumbent aromatherapy and herbal-wellness products and let it break through a saturated category.

## Stakeholders
- **Decision-maker / buyer**: the founder (LinkedIn requester). Likely solo or small team, bootstrap-to-seed stage.
- **End consumer**: US wellness consumer. Working hypothesis (to validate): 25–55 female-skewing, urban / suburban, already buying aromatherapy / supplements / "clean" wellness products; secondary segment: men 25–45 who treat aromatherapy as a "tool" (focus, sleep, stress) rather than a ritual.
- **Channel partners**: specialty wellness retail (Whole Foods, Erewhon, Sprouts, MOM's, Pharmaca-equivalents), Amazon, DTC website, TikTok Shop, possibly creator-led DTC bundles, and yoga / acupuncture clinic resale.
- **Regulators**: FDA — classification of inhalable / aromatic herbal products varies (cosmetic vs. OTC drug vs. dietary supplement vs. unapproved drug) depending on label claims and form factor. State-level wellness/herbal regs largely permissive but vary on labeling.

## Success criteria
The deliverable is "answered well" if it produces:
1. A clear-eyed map of the US aromatherapy & herbal-wellness market — size, segments, growth, key players, price bands, channel mix, white space.
2. A specific, defensible differentiation hypothesis for a Thai-herbal-inhaler brand — not a list of generic options, a recommended position with rationale and the case against.
3. A concrete go-to-market marketing plan (channels, creative angles, first 90 days) the founder can operationalize.
4. A regulatory red-line summary so claim language doesn't accidentally trigger FDA enforcement.
5. An honest assessment of likely failure modes and the cheapest first test that would invalidate the recommendation.

## Knowledge domains required
- US wellness / aromatherapy market structure & competitive set
- Niche brand differentiation theory + consumer psychology (category entry points, JTBD, sensory-product behavior loops)
- Cultural authenticity in CPG — the "borrowed cultural product" pattern (turmeric, kombucha, kimchi, gua sha, tiger balm, ya dom)
- FDA labeling rules for inhalable / aromatic / herbal products
- DTC + retail distribution economics for indie wellness brands
- Creator economy / TikTok / Instagram dynamics for sensory consumer products
- Thai herbal product context (ya dom is mass-market in Thailand, ~$1 SKUs at any 7-Eleven; transferring it abroad has analogues)

## Risk / sensitivity
- **Cultural authenticity discourse**: a Thai-herbal product launched by a Western-owned brand can attract criticism. The brand's relationship to Thailand (founder ethnicity, sourcing, Thai partner ownership) materially shapes risk. Treat as analyzable; flag where data is missing about the founder.
- **FDA classification**: any claim that the product treats / prevents / mitigates a condition ("clears sinuses," "stops headaches," "anxiety relief") risks reclassification as an unapproved drug. Cosmetic / aromatherapy positioning has narrow allowed-claim language ("structure/function" vs. "disease" claims).
- **Quality & safety**: imported herbal ingredients have inconsistent quality; supply-chain transparency is a competitive lever AND a risk.
- **Trademark / IP**: "ya dom" generic; specific brand names need US clearance.

## Out of scope
- Product formulation R&D (no inhaler redesign).
- Manufacturing / supply chain operations beyond what shapes positioning.
- Detailed financial pro-forma (we surface unit-economics implications but don't build a full model).
- International markets — US only.
- Retail-buyer pitch deck — strategy first; sales materials later.

## Open questions for requester (NOT blocking — flagged, will infer)
- **Founder's connection to Thailand** (ethnicity, supplier relationship, on-the-ground network). Inferred default: founder has authentic Thai connection (Thai-American, lived/worked in Thailand, or close partnership with Thai supplier). Brand strategy bifurcates if this is wrong; we'll flag scenarios.
- **Budget envelope.** Inferred default: bootstrap / pre-seed, sub-$250k for first 12 months; sub-$50k for marketing in first 6 months.
- **Existing assets** (name, logo, prototype, supplier signed). Inferred default: greenfield / very early; supplier identified but not exclusive.
- **Co-founders / team.** Inferred default: solo or 2-person.
- **Retail vs DTC bias.** Inferred default: open; we'll recommend the cheapest beachhead.

These will be flagged in the deliverable; the founder confirming different values lets the recommendation be adjusted without redoing the analysis.
