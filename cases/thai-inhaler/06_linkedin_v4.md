The recommendation that saved a $25–100k mistake came from the red team — not the synthesis.

A LinkedIn founder asked for "deep market research + marketing approaches" for a Thai-herbal-inhaler brand launch.

35 sub-agents. ~2.5M tokens. Single conversation, no shell scripts.

The synthesis (Round B + E) said: validate the wedge cheaply, supplier-equity narrative, premium pricing under MoCRA cosmetic rules. Reasonable.

The red team's second pass (Round F) and the 10-viewpoint critique batch said: actually, your menthol+camphor inhaler is an OTC drug under 21 CFR 341, not a cosmetic — the entire compliance plan is mis-scoped. Your supplier-equity 60-day timeline is impossible against Thai business norms (real timeline: 9–12 months). And you have no brand identity to validate in Phase 0.

All three were structural, all three were caught only AFTER the synthesis committed to specifics. Synthesis would have shipped a recommendation that wasted a $50k Phase 0 budget on an undesigned product with the wrong supplier tier and a wrong regulatory classification.

Two takeaways for anyone running multi-agent analysis:

1. **The red team is the analysis. The synthesis is a hypothesis.** Run the adversarial pass AGAINST the synthesis, not before it. Structural errors only show up after the synthesis commits.

2. **Diverse viewpoints find different errors.** The Asia-startup expert caught the supplier tier; the logistics expert caught the OTC drug classification; the brand designer caught the missing identity. Each was invisible to the others.
