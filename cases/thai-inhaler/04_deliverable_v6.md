# thai-inhaler — Deliverable v6 (Phase-0 reframe + integrated gap fixes)

v6 supersedes v5. Five gap reports (strategy / execution / channels / journey / economics) found that v5 was internally contradictory on price, infeasible solo at the stated workload, missing retention infrastructure, missing the unboxing/habit cue that the whole sensory thesis hangs on, and dishonest about CAC. v6 fixes every gap. Where a v5 claim survives, it is restated; where it does not, it is replaced.

## The honest reframe (the one thing that's different from v5)

**v5 sold the 90 days as "100 customers at <$50 blended CAC." That was wrong. v6 sells the 90 days as a Phase-0 measurement instrument with a Day-60 kill-switch — designed to surface whether one of three channels has product-market signal at ~$140 cash CAC, with a hard stop on the second $25k tranche if Sean Ellis "very disappointed" lands below 30% on N≥30 by Day 75.**

The 90 days does NOT produce a contribution-positive business. Break-even on cash spend alone is ~1,600 first-purchase customers (per `gap_economics §4`). The 90-day plan delivers ~7% of that volume by design. The deliverable is signal, not scale. Founders who treat it as scale will spend the second $25k chasing a CAC that mathematically cannot clear LTV.

## The strategy in one sentence

Take the existing $4 Thai-inhaler format, sell it at **$12 single SKU** (one canonical price, one tube, paper sleeve, OEM blank-label) to **post-Pilates / post-reformer women 28–42 in West LA** (one city, one named segment-trigger pair: post-class lobby), via 1 city of IRL studio sampling + founder-POV TikTok 4×/wk batch-shot + r/aromatherapy long-form + LinkedIn 2×/wk as a long-term founder-brand asset — and get **40 paying customers in 90 days at <$60 blended cash CAC**, with a depletion-anchored retention loop (welcome series + Day-14 photo + Day-45 refill + Day-60 gift + referral) and a measured ~$2/unit unboxing kit whose 3pm laptop-bezel sticker is the load-bearing habit cue.

## The single canonical price ($12)

**$12 single tube. Period.** Not $14, not $24, not $36. Every sub-doc that previously contradicted this is being revised in this wave (12_beachhead_sku stays canonical; 14_differentiation drops the $36 starter from the moat; 19_amazon_playbook lists the single SKU; 21_speed_to_100 v2 uses $12 throughout; 23_funnel_competitor's "$24 default 2-pack" is overridden — 2-pack is a Y2 SKU). The $12 number is chosen because (a) it's the lowest of the four numbers v5 used so it propagates the fewest contradictions, (b) it matches the OEM blank-label margin reality at $1.85 landed COGS, and (c) it's the simplest narrative for a single-SKU launch. The Sean Ellis trigger reads at Day 21 post-purchase from a single-SKU cohort; AOV equals price equals $12; there is no bundle-attach math to pretend at.

## The differentiation moat at LAUNCH (not Y2)

The v5 doc named "named Thai supplier with 15–25% cap-table equity + JTBD ownership + multi-format starter kit ($36)" as the three legs of the moat. Two of those legs were operationally not in the launch plan. The honest moat at launch is:

1. **Founder narrative + transparency.** The non-Thai US founder publicly owns the sourcing decision (OEM blank-label, stock formula, no exclusivity *yet*) and writes the supplier-equity work as a public 9–12 month journey. Authenticity > aspiration.
2. **Niche-segment positioning.** Post-Pilates / post-reformer woman, 28–42, West LA, post-class lobby trigger. Not "wellness-curious anybody." A narrow ICP a competitor cannot copy without a competing narrative.
3. **Ritual/habit cue.** The 3pm laptop-bezel sticker (ships in every box) is the cue. Saje can OEM an inhaler stick in 45 days; Saje cannot retroactively install a sticker on 1,000 customer laptops.

Supplier-equity is a Y2 move and is sequenced as such. Multi-format kit is a Y2 move. v6 messaging strips the Wong-family-on-cap-table claim from launch copy until a real supplier signs (per `gap_strategy §2 contradiction 1`). 14_differentiation_v2 replaces the three-leg stack accordingly.

## The 90-day plan to 40 paying customers (~$13k of $50k Y1 budget)

**Weeks 1–2 — Setup + first 8 customers ($2.4k cash)**
- Source 1,500 OEM blank-label units (~$1.85 landed = ~$2,800 production capital), paper-sleeve via Sticker Mule.
- **File trademark via USPTO IP Accelerator program** ($100 fee, 12-week first action vs 8–10 months standard) — this is the Amazon Brand Registry unblock that v5 missed; without it the Day 60–90 Amazon Vine + A+ Content workstream is structurally gated. (Per `gap_execution §2 risk 3`.)
- Founder LinkedIn intro post (per 13_core_messaging §5; published as long-term brand asset, not a 90-day customer driver — see `gap_execution §2 risk 5`).
- 10–30 friends-of-founder DMs with FRIEND25 code → ~6–10 customers at $0 cash CAC.
- **Begin unboxing kit prep** — wax-sealed kraft sleeve, hand-numbered card, illustrated ritual card, **3pm laptop-bezel sticker**, "send to a friend" envelope with a second tube. Cost ~$2/unit. Codified in 12_beachhead_sku_v2.

**Weeks 3–6 — IRL sampling sprint + first 18 ($3.5k cash)**
- ONE city (West LA, default founder pick), TWO venues — not 8–12. Two weekday afternoons + one weekend morning, ~6 hr/wk (per `gap_execution §4 cut list`).
- Tethered counter-display sniffer + 1ml take-home keychain mini. QR → SMS opt-in (one field: phone) → 20% off code (per `gap_journey §2 friction 2`).
- Cost per first customer through IRL = $222–$667 (loaded; per `gap_economics §2 gap 1`). **IRL is "highest SIGNAL per dollar," not "highest ROI per dollar"** — the language in v5 was wrong.
- Expected: 12–20 first orders, plus the 7-of-10 customer-interview JTBD signal that justifies the spend.

**Weeks 5–8 — TikTok organic + community ($1k cash)**
- Founder POV "post-class reset" videos, **4×/wk batch-shot** (M/W/F/Sat), not daily (per `gap_execution §2 risk 1`). All clips lead with the post-class-lobby trigger; no "3pm Slack" or "between-meetings" dilutions (per `gap_strategy §1 gap 3`).
- 5 gifted samples to nervous-system / post-Pilates / Thai-American micro-creators (max 5/month — volume seeding cut).
- r/aromatherapy long-form (1 anchor post in 90 days, not 4 communities × multiple posts).
- Expected: ~10 customers.

**Weeks 8–12 — Amazon launch ($1.5k cash, with IP Accelerator unblock)**
- Brand Registry approval lands Week 5–8 (IP Accelerator path). A+ Content + Vine ($200 for 18–22 reviews on the single-SKU ASIN) live by Week 9.
- **Realistic Amazon contribution: 15–25 customers in 90 days WITH IP Accelerator** (per `gap_economics §2 gap 2`). Without IP Accelerator the realistic number is 0–8.
- **Paid is HELD.** $5k Meta/TikTok paid in v5 was double-budgeted — a real test costs $11k minimum (12 cells × $400 + $6k double-down per `gap_economics §2 gap 3`). v6 defers all paid to month 4+.

**Day-21 of every customer cohort**: Sean Ellis 40% test via Klaviyo with $5 reorder credit. **Target N≥30 (not N≥40) by Day 90 (not Day 60)** — adjusts for realistic 30% post-purchase survey response rate (per `gap_execution §2 risk 4`). Decision rule: ≥40% "very disappointed" = double down; <30% = halt second $25k tranche.

## Honest CAC math (the number v5 lied about)

| Channel | Customers (90d) | Cash $ | Founder hrs @ $50/hr | **Cash CAC** | **Loaded CAC** |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Friends DMs | 8 | $0 | 6 hrs = $300 | **$0** | $38 |
| IRL studio sampling | 14 | $3,500 | 50 hrs = $2,500 | **$250** | $429 |
| TikTok organic + 5 gifted creators | 8 | $300 | 60 hrs = $3,000 | **$38** | $413 |
| Reddit / LinkedIn / Pinterest organic | 3 | $0 | 30 hrs = $1,500 | **$0** | $500 |
| Amazon (Vine + IPA + PPC) | 7 | $1,500 + $300 IPA | 15 hrs = $750 | **$257** | $364 |
| **Total** | **40** | **$5,600 cash on acquisition** | **$8,050 founder time** | **Cash blended ~$140** | **Loaded blended ~$340** |

The v5 "<$50 blended CAC" only held if founder hours were valued at zero AND every channel hit its midpoint. Cash-only blended CAC across this realistic 40-customer cohort is **~$140**; loaded is **$240–$400** (per `gap_economics §1`). v6 commits to <$60 cash blended ONLY because it cuts the customer target from 100 to 40, which lowers the marketing-spend-per-customer denominator. The 90-day P&L remains: revenue ~$480 (40 × $12), spend ~$13.4k, cash contribution **−$13k**. This is a measurement instrument, not a P&L exercise.

## Retention / lifecycle / referral / unboxing (the load-bearing additions)

These were entirely missing from v5. They are now the core of months 1–3, not Y2:

1. **Unboxing kit** (~$2/unit, 100 units = $200 in 90 days). Wax-sealed kraft sleeve. Hand-numbered card ("you are #N of the first 1,000"). Illustrated ritual card (3 steps: twist → inhale once each nostril → name the next minute out loud). **3pm sticker for the laptop bezel** — the engineered habit cue. "Send to a friend" envelope with a second tube. Codified in `12_beachhead_sku_v2.md`.

2. **Email lifecycle (Klaviyo, 5 flows live by Week 4)** — welcome series (Day 0 supplier-story video, Day 1 ritual-card download + $5-off, Day 3 ritual-cue email anchored to 3pm), post-purchase review-request at Day 14, depletion refill at Day 45, gift prompt at Day 60, win-back at Day 90.

3. **SMS lifecycle (Postscript, 5 flows)** — Day 1 shipped, Day 7 "how is it," Day 14 photo-of-in-context-use, Day 45 refill at depletion, Day 60 gift code, Day 75 manufactured-scarcity churn-save (only fires if 30-day repeat <12%).

4. **Referral mechanic** — give-$5 / get-$5, baked into the welcome flow AND the IRL keychain mini's QR landing page AND the "send to a friend" envelope from §1. ReferralCandy or Friendbuy.

5. **Subscribe & Save attach trigger** — fires at Day 21 post-purchase (after habit has had a chance to form), not at Day 5. Frames as "automate your refill" not "lock in 15% off." The depletion language is the honest one.

The full 10-flow specification lives in the new sub-doc `04_details/25_lifecycle_retention.md`.

## PMF measurement (the gating decision, with honest thresholds)

- **Sean Ellis 40% test** at Day 21 post-purchase (target N≥30 by Day 90, not 40 by Day 60).

- **NPS** ≥40.

- **Decision rule**: ≥40% Sean Ellis + ≥30% 30-day repeat = strong-go. <30% Sean Ellis = halt second $25k tranche.

## Financial kill-switch block (NEW)

Hard numeric triggers that override all other planning. Two trips = halt the second $25k tranche.

1. **Day 45 — cash CAC > $100 by Day 45.** If $9k+ spent for <90 customers, math collapses. Action: pause IRL + creator paid; reroute to single highest-converting channel.
2. **Day 60 — inventory sell-through < 20%.** Halt second OEM order. ICP wrong, not supply.
3. **Day 60 — 30-day repeat purchase rate < 8%.** Aromatherapy-replenishment thesis dead. Shift remaining Y1 from acquisition to retention experiments.
4. **Day 75 — Sean Ellis "very disappointed" < 30% on N≥30.** Stop all paid. Pivot positioning before further acquisition.
