**Why v2:** v1 named a three-leg moat (named Thai supplier with cap-table equity + JTBD ownership + multi-format starter kit). Two of those legs were operationally NOT in the launch plan — the supplier-equity work is a 9–12 month parallel track, and the multi-format starter kit is explicitly forbidden by `12_beachhead_sku_v2.md` for the first 90 days. v1 had the brand writing checks the supply chain wasn't cashing (per `gap_strategy §1 gap 1`). v2 replaces the moat-stack with what the launch actually ships.

## 1. The differentiation in one sentence (unchanged)

**The 1-second daytime focus reset — Thai herbal craft, modern American ritual, in your pocket like a pen.**

(17 words. Format-anchored, JTBD-anchored, heritage-anchored, deliberately silent on body-system claims.)

---

## 2. The 4-vs-comparison (unchanged from v1)

- **vs. Saje** — Saje is a 5-second roll-on for headache and tension; we're a 1-second inhale for the focus dip after class, no greasy temple, no staircase scent, no headache required.
- **vs. Vicks** — Vicks is a drugstore stick for when you have a cold; we're a daytime ritual stick for when you don't, repositioned out of the cold-and-flu aisle entirely.
- **vs. Apothékary** — Apothékary is a tincture you dose under the tongue at home; we're an inhale you do at your desk, on the train, or in the studio lobby — same nervous-system moment, no dropper, no drink.
- **vs. Poy-Sian / Hong Thai** — Poy-Sian is the original $2 Thai stick built for Bangkok 7-Elevens; we are the modern American translation of that craft — same lineage, packaged and explained for someone who isn't already inside it. (The supplier-equity narrative is a Y2 differentiator, not a Day-1 one.)

---

## 3. The trigger (codified — single context, single moment)

The v5 docs described the same buyer in four different physical contexts (post-class lobby, 3pm Slack desk, between-meeting hallway, coffee-shop sample table). v2 picks ONE: **post-Pilates / post-reformer woman 28–42, post-class lobby trigger, in West LA**. Every TikTok script, PDP headline, and creator brief leads with that single trigger for 90 days. The "3pm" sticker (per `12_beachhead_sku_v2.md`) extends the moment from the studio lobby into her workday — it does NOT relocate the trigger to her desk. The studio lobby is where she discovers it; the 3pm cue is where she repeats it.

---

## 4. Defensibility argument — the moat at LAUNCH (revised)

The v1 doc named a three-leg stack: supplier-equity + JTBD + multi-format kit. Legs 1 and 3 are Y2 work. The honest moat at LAUNCH (months 1–6) is a different three-leg stack:

1. **Founder narrative + transparency.** The non-Thai US founder publicly owns the sourcing decision (OEM blank-label, stock formula, no exclusivity *yet*) and writes the supplier-equity work as a public 9–12 month journey. Authenticity is the asset; aspiration is the trap. Saje cannot copy a published founder-journey arc on a product calendar.

2. **Niche-segment positioning.** Post-Pilates / post-reformer woman, 28–42, West LA, post-class-lobby trigger. Not "wellness-curious anybody." A narrow ICP a competitor cannot copy without abandoning their existing brand voice. Saje's "Pocket Farmacy" voice cannot pivot to "post-class lobby reset" without breaking equity. Apothékary's food-as-medicine voice cannot pivot to a non-ingestible. Vicks cannot pivot upmarket without breaking medical-trust pricing. The slot is structurally available because none of the four named brands is engineered for *that* moment.

3. **Ritual / habit cue (the 3pm sticker).** Codified in `12_beachhead_sku_v2.md`. Every customer's laptop bezel becomes a brand-coded inhalation prompt. Saje can OEM an inhaler stick in 45 days; Saje cannot retroactively install stickers on 1,000 customer laptops. The cue is customer-installed, customer-maintained, infinite-duration, and structurally un-copyable post-distribution.

The combination is the moat at LAUNCH. Each of the three is independently weak — founder narrative without a niche dies, niche without a habit cue churns silently, habit cue without authenticity reads as a marketing trick. Together they compound.

### What MOVES INTO the moat at month 6+

1. **Named Thai supplier with cap-table equity (15–25%)** — the v1 leg-1. Lock by month 12. This becomes a moat REINFORCER, not a moat AT LAUNCH. Until the supplier signs, founder copy says "I'm building this with a Thai supplier I'm in conversation with — equity-first, transparent process" and links to a public update log. The first viral founder post must NOT claim "Wong family in Chiang Rai, named on the label, equity not just royalty" — that was v1's contradiction. Drop until real.

2. **Multi-format starter kit** — the v1 leg-3. Ships only when sell-through clears 50 units/wk × 3 weeks at <$35 blended CAC AND repeat purchase clears 22% at day 90. This is a Y2 SKU, not a launch SKU.

3. **Owned audience as moat** — the gap-strategy report flagged this as missing. The founder LinkedIn 2x/wk is a 12-month payoff asset, not a 90-day customer driver. The list-build engine is the email/SMS lifecycle (per `25_lifecycle_retention.md`) plus the IRL keychain QR. By month 12 the owned-audience graph (Klaviyo list + Postscript SMS + Pinterest pinners + LinkedIn followers) becomes a defensible moat layer. Apothékary's actual moat is its 1700+ Whole Foods footprint + 500k+ newsletter — neither comes from product, both come from compounding owned-audience over time.

## 5. "How to talk about it" cheat sheet (revised — supplier line softened)

| Customer says (X) | Founder responds (Y) |
|---|---|
| "Isn't this just a fancy Vicks?" | "Vicks is for when you're sick. This is for when you're not. Different aisle, different job — same pocket-sized format because it's the only format that delivers in 1 second." |
| "How is this different from Saje?" | "Saje is a 5-second roll-on for headache. We're a 1-second inhale for the focus dip — and it's Thai herbal craft I'm transparently building toward supplier-equity on, not an essential-oil blend made in BC." |
| "I already use Apothékary tinctures." | "Keep them. Apothékary is your home routine; we're your between-class routine. Tincture takes a dropper and a kitchen — this takes one breath in the studio lobby." |
| "I can buy Poy-Sian on Amazon for two bucks." | "You can — and you should at least once, to learn the format. Poy-Sian is the 7-Eleven version; ours is built on that craft tradition but designed and explained for someone who isn't already inside it. The 3pm sticker in the box is the part Poy-Sian doesn't ship." |
| "What does it actually do?" | "It's a 1-second sensory reset stick — Thai herbs, menthol, eucalyptus — that gives you a clean breath when you need to come back to a meeting, a screen, or a room. The sticker in the box is to remind you at 3pm." (No body-system verb, FDA-safe, JTBD-anchored, habit-cue named.) |

The X/Y change vs. v1 is the supplier line: v1 said "Thai herbal craft with our supplier on the cap table." v2 says "I'm transparently building toward supplier-equity on" until the partner signs. Specificity > grandiosity.

## 6. Success metric (unchanged threshold; revised question)

**Target: ≥40% of customers who have made one repeat purchase can articulate the differentiation back without prompting in 1 sentence within 3 months of first order.**

Measurement: open-text survey question — *"In your own words, how would you describe this product to a friend who already uses Saje or Vicks?"* Score positive if response contains (a) 1-second / fast / instant / quick AND (b) one of [focus, reset, post-class, after-Pilates, daytime, on-the-go] AND (c) one of [Thai, herbal craft, 3pm, sticker, pocket ritual] OR an explicit Saje/Vicks/Apothékary contrast.
