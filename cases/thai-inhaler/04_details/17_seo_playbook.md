**Sub-agent**: SEO / pillar-content specialist
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Constraints**: bootstrap, solo, greenfield, marketing-first, ≤$50k Y1.

---

## 1. The play in one sentence

SEO is a **long-tail compounding bet** that builds an evergreen content moat over 6–12 months by owning low-volume / low-difficulty corridors competitors ignore — it does **not** generate first-90-days traffic, and any plan that pretends otherwise is selling you something. By month 9 the moat compounds; in months 1–3 it produces zero customers and that is the correct expectation.

## 4. The link-building strategy

No link-buying, no PBNs, no Fiverr outreach blasts. Real signals from real humans. ≤$2k of the $50k budget — most of this is time.

**A. Substack writer outreach (8–12 hours/month).** Per 2026 Substack data (per onlinewritingclub.com, weareroast.com), 40% of new subs come from inside-network Recommendations + Notes. Build a target list of 30 Substacks: nervous-system / somatic / wellness writers (Dr. Aimie Apigian, The Holistic Psychologist's Substack, Wellness Wisdom, etc.) plus Thai-American / AAPI culture writers plus indie-CPG founder Substacks. Outreach: not a pitch, a useful artifact. Send each writer a free Hong Thai Mark II proxy (cost: $4) + a 2-paragraph note offering a guest essay on "the cultural object I almost didn't write about." Aim: 4–6 essays land in Y1, each with 1 contextual backlink.

**B. Podcast guest spots (6–10 hours/month).** Long-tail wellness podcasts with 2K–20K downloads — easy to book, real backlinks from show notes. Targets: Beauty Independent's podcast, Almost 30, The Dream, Bag Ladiez, Outliers, indie founder shows. Pitch angle: "founder of a Thai-heritage brand on the cosmetic-vs-drug language line." Aim: 6–10 episodes Y1. Each episode = 1 dofollow backlink + 1 voice-search corpus contribution (audio search corpus matters for AI assistants per arnoldmarketing.com 2026).

**C. Reddit AMA — exactly one, done right.** r/Aromatherapy (~140K), r/EssentialOils (~190K), r/herbalism (~340K). Pick r/Aromatherapy. Schedule a verified-founder AMA. Show up with photos of the supplier, transparent pricing breakdown (Diaspora Co. precedent), and one giveaway. Reddit threads age well in Google for years — a single decent AMA delivers a 5-year backlink + repeat referral search traffic. Run it after pillar article #1 is live so people have something to read. Budget: $200 in giveaway product.

**D. Niche-community guest posts.** 4–6 guest posts on sites with DR 25–55 in adjacent categories: Thai-American culture blogs (Sawatdee, Khao Sod English, ThaiTable's blog), wellness-and-ritual blogs (The Good Trade, Well+Good's Renegade contributor program, mindbodygreen if it can be earned), small-business journalism (Beauty Independent, BeautyMatter — both publish founder-perspective pieces). Pitch the founder's *story*, not the product.

**E. The Erewhon / specialty-retail SEO bonus.** When the brand lands one Erewhon-tier door (per Phase 1 plan), the retailer's own site links to brand pages. Don't underestimate this — Erewhon's DR is ~70 and a single product-page link from there is worth 50+ random guest posts.

## 5. The metrics

Real numbers, not vanity. Track in Google Search Console (free) + Plausible or Fathom (privacy-friendly, ~$10/mo, won't get blocked by ad-blockers like GA4 does).

- **Month 1–3**: Search Console **impressions**, not clicks. Target: 800–2,000 impressions/mo by month 3 (the brand exists in Google's index, queries are matching).
- **Month 3–6**: **Click-through rate** on top-25 ranked queries. Target: >2.5% average CTR (means titles are working). Pages-indexed in GSC ≥ pages-published.
- **Month 6**: **Time-to-first-100-organic-clicks**. Realistic = 90 days from first publication for the lowest-difficulty pillar (#1 ya dom and #2 Thai herbal inhaler). Realistic for the mid-difficulty pillars (#3, #4) = 6–9 months.
- **Month 9–12**: **First $1k of organic-attributed revenue**. Realistic for an indie wellness brand starting from zero domain authority. If we hit it earlier, great; budget assumes we don't.
- **Leading indicator** (weekly check, 5 minutes): GSC "queries" tab — are new long-tail phrases appearing each week? If yes, the moat is forming. If no, the content isn't being crawled or isn't matching intent.

**What we ignore**: domain authority score (vanity metric, lags by 3 months), bounce rate (GA4 measures it wrong), page speed obsession (good-enough is good-enough; we're not Amazon).

## 6. The DON'T list

- **No programmatic SEO.** Generating 500 city-pages or 1,000 ingredient-pages with templated content gets you penalized hard under the Helpful Content Update + the January 2026 core update. Indie wellness brands have died on this hill. Don't.
- **No AI-spam content.** Writing 5 articles/week with ChatGPT and pressing publish is the fastest way to get the whole site demoted. Use AI for outlines and first drafts — *then hand-edit until it sounds like a human who has held the product in their hands*. Google's 2026 update explicitly rewards "named authors with real expertise" (superframeworks.com).
- **No keyword stuffing.** "Thai herbal inhaler Thai herbal inhaler best Thai herbal inhaler buy Thai herbal inhaler" is 2010 SEO and will get the page filtered.
- **No buying links.** Fiverr "guest post packages," PBNs, link farms — all detected, all penalized.
- **No "aromatherapy" pillar in Y1.** It's the wrong fight at zero domain authority.
- **No medical / drug language anywhere on the site.** Cross-reference every post against the verb-object rule from D_fda_language before publishing. Search-engine traffic that triggers an FDA warning letter is worse than no traffic.
- **No targeting Healthline / WebMD / Cleveland Clinic head-terms.** YMYL territory needs medical-reviewer credentials. We're a cosmetic brand, not a clinic.
