# Wave-1 Founder-as-Primary-Channel Playbook

**Case**: thai-inhaler
**Specialty**: Founder personal brand as the primary acquisition engine for a bootstrap, solo, greenfield consumer-CPG launch.
**Date**: 2026-04-27

---

## 1. The play in one sentence

For the first 100 paying customers, the founder *is* the brand — a public LinkedIn-led practitioner narrative ("I'm building a Thai herbal inhaler brand in public, here's what I'm learning") drives 30–50% of early acquisition, neutralizes the appropriation-risk arc by making the founder's identity and supplier-equity decisions legible *before* any product ships, and seeds the journalist + creator + retail-buyer pipeline that turns into the Erewhon door at month 4.

This is **not** "post some thought-leadership and hope." It is a content engine with weekly outputs, tracked metrics, and a kill-switch at month 6.

## 3. The five content pillars

The founder owns these five themes in public. Every post fits one. If a post doesn't fit, it doesn't ship.

1. **The journey (build-in-public).** "Week 4: I sent 12 cold emails to Thai herbal-inhaler workshops. Got 2 replies. Here's what they said." Concrete numbers, concrete weeks, no vague aspiration. This is the Sahil Bloom hook structure (1–2 line opener, framework, payoff) repurposed for CPG operations rather than personal-finance frameworks. Sahil went 0 → 400k LinkedIn followers in roughly 36 months by stacking these. A solo CPG founder can plausibly do 0 → 8k in 12 months on the same template, which is enough to drive first-100-customer demand.

2. **The product POV — why this exists.** "Aromatherapy in the US is lavender-coded essential oils. The Thai version is a 1-second pharmacy staple. Here's the difference, and why the format matters." Pillar function: makes the product legible to a Western buyer without exoticizing — the Fly By Jing "normalizing not exoticizing" move (Jing Gao framed Sichuan chili crisp as a daily pantry item, not a "discovery").

3. **The cultural research — done with respect.** Named Thai herbalists, named regions, named workshops. Photographs with consent and credit. The Diaspora Co. 4×-FOB pricing disclosure, published as a content artifact, is itself a viral LinkedIn post. *This pillar only works if the founder has secured a Thai supplier-partner with disclosed equity per the decisions.md commitment.* Without that, this pillar is a backlash trigger and must be cut entirely (pivot to D_diff_map Adjacent B per E_final.md).

4. **The marketing meta — the LinkedIn case-study series.** "I asked LinkedIn for help on this brand. 47 strangers wrote me back. Here's the synthesis." This is the *Burglary*-itself content: the act of public consultation becomes the brand's origin myth. Highest-leverage pillar because it has zero competition — no other CPG founder is doing this. Journalists write about novel patterns; this is one.

5. **The practitioner POV — what indie CPG founders learn.** "What I learned in 30 days as an indie CPG founder that nobody on Shark Tank tells you: bundle math, FDA verb-objects, Erewhon's slotting fee." Pillar function: positions the founder as a *peer-teacher* in the indie-CPG community (Amrit Richmond / Indie CPG / Startup CPG audience), which is where the retail-buyer and journalist intros actually originate.

Pillar mix per week: 2× journey, 1× product POV, 1× practitioner. Cultural-research pillar lands when a milestone earns it (supplier signed, photo shoot completed, lab results in). Meta pillar lands monthly.

## 4. The build-in-public tradeoffs

Founders new to building-in-public confuse "transparent" with "naked." They are different. The discipline is *what* to share.

**Share (the process):**
- Customer-discovery interview counts ("23 interviews, 18 mentioned the same thing").
- Phase 0 validation results in aggregate ("conversion at $14 was 1.8%, above the 1.5% gate").
- FDA-compliance learnings as a content artifact (the verb-object rule rendered as a public 1-pager).
- Founder mistakes that don't compound — naming the wrong Instagram filter, the typo on the first batch label.
- Supplier-equity disclosure (named supplier, % revenue share or cap-table equity, photo with consent). This is the single highest-trust artifact a CPG founder can publish.

**Don't share (the moat):**
- Specific suppliers' contact information, factory addresses, or unique sourcing relationships before the supplier-equity contract is executed and counter-signed.
- Exact formulation ratios, essential-oil supplier names below the wholesale tier, packaging vendors. Saje and doTERRA can line-extend an inhaler stick in 30–45 days; don't hand them the recipe.
- Pricing strategy *changes* in advance ("we're raising the bundle price to $36 next month") — leak post-launch only.
- Cap-table specifics beyond the supplier-partner equity stake.
- Customer PII, buyer-meeting transcripts, retail-buyer feedback that names the buyer.
- Failed creator-seeding attempts that name the creator. Burn no bridges in public.

The asymmetry: shared *process* compounds trust. Shared *moat* compounds your competitor's catch-up speed. When in doubt, share the lesson without the artifact ("we learned X" without "here's the SKU and supplier").

## 5. The podcast / speaker pipeline

You don't get on Tim Ferriss in month 1. You earn the way up. Realistic 18-month ladder:

**Tier 1 — Months 1–3 (cold-pitch realistic, indie founder-friendly):**
1. **The Startup CPG Podcast** — top CPG podcast globally; founder-feature format; warm if you're a Startup CPG community member ($199/year, worth it).
2. **Chat CPG (Food + Wellness)** — wellness-CPG focused; founder-story interview format.
3. **Indie CPG / Amrit Richmond's ShelfMade community** — not a podcast but a 100-founder community where the podcast invites originate.
4. **Female Startup Club** (Doone Roisin) — booked the Fly By Jing interview pre-scale; warm to wellness-CPG founders with a story.

**Tier 2 — Months 3–9 (need a launch + early traction proof):**
5. **The Glossy Podcast / Glossy Beauty** — beauty-and-wellness CPG; needs a brand actually shipping.
6. **Modern Retail / Retail Brew Podcast** — needs first retail door (Erewhon counts).
7. **Beauty Independent / Beauty Matter podcasts** — Shizu Okusa-tier wellness brands. Needs $1M+ trajectory or unique angle (the supplier-equity disclosure is the angle).

**Tier 3 — Months 9–18 (need PR moment + revenue inflection):**
8. **My First Million** — Sam Parr / Shaan Puri; needs a hook, ideally a "we did $X in Y months with $50k" frame plus a contrarian POV. The FDA-language verb-object rule is a usable hook.
9. **How I Built This** — Guy Raz; needs ~$5M+ revenue and a clean narrative arc. Year-2 target.
10. **The Tim Ferriss Show** — wellness CPG only lands here with either a $50M+ exit narrative or a sharply contrarian wellness POV. Year 3 at earliest. Don't pitch it before then; cold-pitching too early makes you uninvitable later.

**Operating cadence**: send 3 cold pitches/week. Use the practitioner-POV pillar posts as the pitch artifact ("I wrote this; would your audience care?"). Track in a spreadsheet — pitched / replied / booked / aired / outcome.

## 7. The DON'T list

- **Humble-bragging.** "Just hit 1,000 followers, so grateful." No. Either share the lesson or don't post.
- **Growth-hack jargon.** "10X your CPG launch with these 7 hacks." Disqualifying.
- **AI-generated content** that reads as AI-generated. Run a co-pilot draft, then voice-edit aggressively. The ChatGPT em-dash-and-tricolon cadence is now a known flag; LinkedIn engagement on detectable AI text has collapsed.
- **Fake polish.** Beige-wellness photo grids. Stock photos. The professionally-shot founder portrait. All slot-#2 (per A_pr) signals.
- **Engagement-pod participation.** It compounds short-term, then craters when the algo retunes.
- **"Ancient Thai wisdom" / "healing ritual" copy.** Per A_pr, this is the appropriation-arc trigger. Cut on sight.
- **Posting on weekends in the first 90 days.** LinkedIn algorithm is anemic Saturday-Sunday; save the post for Monday.
- **Replying to wellness-skeptic critics in the same thread.** Listen on X, respond in long-form on Substack a week later if it warrants.
- **Naming competitors.** Even Saje. Especially Apothékary (closest competitor per E_final). Frame all comparisons as category-level.

## 8. One thing this week

Write and ship **one** LinkedIn post by Friday. Topic: *"I asked LinkedIn for business problems three weeks ago. Here's what I learned launching a Thai herbal inhaler brand from the responses."*

Structure:
- **Hook** (line 1): "Three weeks ago I asked LinkedIn for business problems to research. The Thai-herbal-inhaler one ate my brain."
- **Setup** (3–5 lines): What ya dom is, what it costs in Thailand ($1), what comparable products cost in the US ($14+).
- **The 3 things I learned** (5–8 lines, numbered):
  1. The FDA cares about verbs, not vibes (the verb-object rule, in plain language).
  2. The bundle math is structural, not optional ($14 single fails CAC; $24 2-pack works).
  3. The founder identity decides the playbook (and I'm doing the supplier-equity work first, before the brand work).
- **What I'm doing next** (2 lines): Phase 0 validation in the next 6 weeks, $5k budget, kill-switch at conversion <1%.
- **CTA** (1 line): "If you're an indie CPG founder, what did I miss? Reply below."

Practitioner voice. No exclamation points. No "thrilled to share." Post Wednesday at 8:15am Eastern.
