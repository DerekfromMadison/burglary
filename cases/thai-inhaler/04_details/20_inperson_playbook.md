# Wave-1 In-Person Sensory Sampling Playbook — thai-inhaler

**Sub-agent specialty**: IRL discovery layer — coffee shops, yoga studios, co-working, farmers markets, pop-ups, retail try-stations, conferences. Sensory products (you can't smell a TikTok video) convert dramatically better when prospects can touch and inhale before they buy. This is a realistic plan for one solo founder, in one city, with a ≤$50k Y1 marketing envelope.

---

## 1. The play in one sentence

Sensory products convert at 5–10× the rate when sampled in-person versus blind ad-clicks — for a Thai herbal inhaler whose entire value proposition lives in 3 seconds of scent, **IRL sampling is the highest-ROI marketing channel the brand has, provided the founder can physically scale it without burning out**.

The constraint isn't demand or unit economics — it's the founder's calendar. So the playbook below is built around 8–12 hours of in-person work per week, not "build a street team."

---

## 2. The venue priority

Ranked by signal-to-effort. Numbers are working estimates; the brand should track and recalibrate after 30 days.

**Tier 1 — Independent coffee shops (top priority).** The 2:47pm Slack-overload moment from the customer-research memo lives here. People are already alert, on the laptop, stationary, and willing to engage with a small object on the counter. Cost to enter is essentially zero (a conversation with the manager, ~$30–60 in product per shop per month). Most consignment-style retail partnerships at indie shops run a 20% commission on retail value; coffee shops won't usually take cash for a try-station, just the relationship. Expected scan-to-purchase rate: **3–6%** of QR scans convert within 14 days (industry baseline for IRL CPG sampling is 2–4%; sensory products run higher because the product has already done the convincing).

**Tier 2 — Yoga and meditation studios.** Already nervous-system-vocabulary native. Practitioners trust front-desk recommendations more than almost any other retail surface. Yoga Studio Network research shows samples handed out post-class in this environment have unusually high redemption because the post-savasana state is the literal moment the product is for. Cost: free–$50 sample drop per studio; revenue-share 15–20% if shelf placement is offered. Expected scan-to-purchase rate: **5–8%**. Logistical drag: studios are picky about brand fit and want to vet the founder.

**Tier 3 — Co-working spaces (WeWork-tier and indie).** WeWork-scale chains require corporate partnership conversations and slow down the cadence; smaller indie co-works (The Wing's successors, NeueHouse, Industrious, local spots) move faster. Front-desk try-station + a Tuesday/Thursday 3pm "afternoon reset" demo. Cost: ~$0–200/month if any. Expected scan-to-purchase: **3–5%** but volume per visit is higher (50–150 people circulating through a desk).

**Tier 4 — Farmers markets.** NYC Parks farmers markets cost $15/day + $25 permit; LA and SF range $40–125/day depending on market. High traffic, low intent — people are buying produce, not aromatherapy. Useful for: list-building, neighborhood density, weekend founder presence. Expected scan-to-purchase: **1.5–3%**. Don't make this the main play.

**Tier 5 — Pop-ups inside Erewhon-tier specialty retail.** Erewhon doesn't charge slotting fees and runs a CARES vendor demo program — the "Valet Brands" sampling-to-parked-cars channel is uniquely available there. This is the highest-intent foot traffic in the country for the wellness wedge but the bar to get in is high (vetting, spec sheets). Defer until after a Tier 1+2 case study exists. Expected scan-to-purchase: **8–12%** when achieved.

**Tier 6 — Conferences (wellness, productivity, AAPI).** One-shot bursts. Wanderlust, Mindbodygreen Revitalize, Asia Society events, Money 20/20-adjacent founder events. Cost $500–3,000 per booth. Use sparingly — one per quarter, not as a base-load channel.

## 3. The sampling unit

Three options ranked by cost and conversion mechanic:

1. **Try-and-toss desktop sniffer (PRIMARY)**. A larger 5–10ml sealed counter-display tube tethered to the try-station; never leaves the venue, smelled by hundreds of customers. Replenished weekly. Cost: ~$3–5 per unit, lasts 50–100 sniffs. This is the workhorse; everyone in the venue can sample without shipping the brand inventory away.
2. **Take-home keychain mini (CONVERSION DRIVER)**. A 1ml or 1.5ml private-label sample tube on a small chain, branded sleeve, QR code on the back. Cost: ~$1.50–2.50 each at low volume. Hand to anyone who tries the sniffer and seems interested ("take this — try it tomorrow at 3pm and tell me if it works"). The 24-hour delay between try and re-sniff is when conversion happens.
3. **QR-code redemption card** (BACKUP). A printed card with QR + 20% off code. Cheaper but gives up the second-sniff moment, which is where sensory conversion lives. Use only when supplier inventory is short.

Recommend **#1 + #2 paired**, with #3 as fallback. Total cost per "engaged prospect" lands ~$2.50–4.00 — well under any reasonable digital CAC.

## 4. The "founder schlep" 30-day calendar (NYC base case; LA/SF substitute equivalent venues)

Assumes founder dedicates 2 weekday afternoons + 1 weekend morning to in-person work. ~10 hours/week.

**Week 1 — relationship-build, no sales.** Mon/Wed afternoons: visit 6 indie coffee shops in Williamsburg/Greenpoint/LES (specific NYC examples: Devoción, Sey, Hungry Ghost, Konditori, Bluestone Lane Local, Variety) — buy a coffee, talk to the manager, leave a sample, ask for a 10-min meeting next week. Saturday: walk into 3 yoga studios in the same neighborhoods (Sky Ting, Modo Yoga LES, Y7) — speak to the desk lead, leave samples for the instructors.

**Week 2 — first try-stations live.** Convert 3 coffee shop conversations into a 2-week try-station trial with revenue share or no-cost partnership. Drop the sniffer + 50 keychain minis. One yoga studio bought-in: schedule a single post-class sampling for the founder to host (Saturday 11am).

**Week 3 — first co-working + farmers market.** Reach out to 2 indie co-works (NeueHouse Madison, The Yard); offer a Tuesday 3pm "afternoon reset" demo at the front desk. Saturday: vendor a McCarren Park or Union Square Greenmarket booth ($15–40/day NYC).

**Week 4 — measure, double down, ask for referrals.** Visit each Week 1 venue, count scans (manager pulls QR analytics), restock minis, ask the manager: *"who else in the neighborhood would do this with you?"* — then go to those shops next. End-of-month review: which 3 venues converted best? Plan month 2 around them.

Realistic month-1 targets (solo founder, single market):
- Venues active: **8–12** (6 coffee, 2 yoga, 1 co-working, 1 farmers market, 1 pop-up)
- People sampled: **400–800**
- QR scans: **120–240** (30% scan rate of triers)
- Conversions to first purchase: **6–18** (5–8% of scans)
- Subscription / 2-pack attach: **1–4**

These are deliberately modest — sampling is a moat-builder, not a hockey-stick channel month 1.

## 5. The conversion mechanism

QR code on every sample, sniffer, and shelf-talker → mobile-optimized landing page (NOT the home page) with:
- 3-second scent description ("eucalyptus + camphor + something woody, like a Vicks built by a Thai grandmother")
- One-time-use 20%-off code (auto-applied, expires 14 days)
- Phone-number capture for SMS welcome flow (not email — SMS open rates 3–4× email for IRL-acquired)
- "Where you tried it" dropdown — track which venues drive sales (closed-loop attribution)
- Optional: name + email for a referral credit ("send a friend, both get $5")

SMS welcome: 1 message at 24h ("did you try it again at 3pm?"), 1 at day 5 ("ready to keep one in every bag?"), 1 at day 12 with discount expiry reminder. Stop after that.

## 6. The metrics

Track weekly:

| Metric | Target | Calculation |
|---|---|---|
| Try-rate at table | 35–55% | sniff-attempts / foot-traffic past station |
| Scan rate | 25–40% of triers | QR scans / sniffs |
| Scan-to-purchase | 4–8% | first-orders / scans |
| Blended channel CAC | <$18 | (founder hours × $50 + sample COGS + venue fees) / first-orders |
| Per-venue scan velocity | >15 scans/wk by week 4 | scans / venue / week |
| 60-day repeat rate | >25% | second-orders / first-orders at d60 |

Kill any venue under 5 scans/week by week 4. Double down on the top 3.

## 7. The DON'T list

- **Chain coffee** (Starbucks, Blue Bottle corp, Joe Coffee chain locations): no co-marketing surface, corporate brand-rules block sampling.
- **Whole Foods sampling tables**: slotting + demo fees ($500–2,500 per day plus product) are uneconomic at this scale; revisit Y2.
- **Random street sampling / subway / sidewalks**: NYC, LA, SF all require permits and most sidewalk sampling is unlicensed-vendor-illegal; don't get the brand a fine that ends up on the news.
- **Chain pharmacies / CVS / Walgreens**: irrelevant audience and the slotting math is brutal.
- **Biohacker / nootropics expos**: explicitly off-limits per the case decisions — this is exactly the FDA-affiliate-enforcement crowd.
- **Influencer-attended events without product fit**: don't pay $3k for a wellness conference whose attendees don't match the customer-research personas.

## 8. One thing this week

**Today / tomorrow — visit 3 specific local indie coffee shops; ask the manager about a sample-station partnership; test the founder pitch live.** NYC starter list: Devoción Williamsburg, Sey Greenpoint, Hungry Ghost (Bedford Ave). LA starter list: Maru, Document, Civil Coffee. SF starter list: Saint Frank, Andytown, Réveille. Pitch script: *"I'm launching a Thai herbal inhaler — like a $4 7-Eleven Bangkok product, made the right way with named Thai supplier credit. Can I leave a counter-tester for two weeks, no cost, you keep 20% on anything that sells through, I track conversions with this QR code?"* Three conversations. Two will say "send an email." One will say yes. That one shop is week 1 of the entire IRL plan.

The Olipop and Magic Mind founders both built their early credibility through hand-to-hand sampling and gifted-product loops before a single ad dollar scaled — this is the same playbook, applied to a category where physical sampling is even more load-bearing because **scent doesn't survive a screen**.
