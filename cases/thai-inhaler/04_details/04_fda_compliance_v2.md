# FDA Compliance v2 — thai-inhaler

**TL;DR**: A menthol+camphor nasal inhaler is an OTC drug under 21 CFR 341 monograph M012, NOT a cosmetic. v3's "MoCRA cosmetic" framing was contradicted by the molecule list. Path A: drop camphor, reformulate to cosmetic (recommended). Path B: keep camphor, commit to OTC drug compliance (~$25–50k Y1).

## The classification verdict

Camphor at any concentration in a nasal inhaler triggers OTC drug classification under FDA monograph M012 (Cough/Cold/Allergy/Bronchodilator/Antiasthmatic). Vicks Vapor Inhaler, Olbas, and Tiger Balm Inhaler are all registered under this monograph. v3's "cosmetic positioning under MoCRA" was structurally infeasible for the formulation it implied.

## The two paths

### Path A — Reformulate to cosmetic (recommended)

Drop camphor. Keep menthol, eucalyptus, peppermint, borneol, lemongrass, kaffir lime, Thai herbal blend.

**Trade-offs**: loses ~10% of "cooling intensity" the camphor delivers; the remaining sensory profile (menthol + eucalyptus + Thai herbs) still differentiates from drugstore inhalers and Saje roll-ons.

**Compliance steps**:
- 2-hr counsel intake confirming reformulation qualifies under MoCRA cosmetic ($500–800)
- MoCRA facility registration (free; FDA.gov/MoCRA)
- Product listing (free)
- Cosmetic GMP — voluntary but recommended; ~$3k for first inspection
- Labeling per cosmetic standard (no Drug Facts)
- Adverse event records (1-year retention)

**Total compliance cost Y1**: ~$3.5k.

### Path B — Keep camphor, commit to OTC drug

Register product under OTC monograph M012.

**Compliance requirements**:
- NDC labeler code application (~$2k)
- FDA Establishment Registration (FEI) — annual fee
- Drug Facts label format (specific FDA layout)
- CGMP-compliant manufacturer; most Thai OEMs at the recommended tier are NOT CGMP-certified for US drug
- Adverse event reporting (medical-device-equivalent)

**Total compliance cost Y1**: ~$25–50k. Ongoing: ~$10k/year.

**Recommended only if**: founder has $250k+ capital AND a 12-month timeline AND comfortable with FDA inspection risk.

## Verb-object rule (Path A only)

If Path A is chosen, the verb-object rule from v3 still applies: the verb's object determines classification.

| Object of the verb | Classification | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Product / scent / ritual / moment | **Cosmetic-safe** | "Refresh your senses." "An aromatic 1-second reset." "A mid-meeting ritual." |
| Body / system / emotion / condition | **Drug claim** | "Calms the nervous system." "Activates vagal tone." "Eases anxiety." |

Five forever-safe phrases (use freely):
- "An aromatic 1-second reset"
- "Refresh your senses"
- "A mid-meeting ritual"
- "Bright, cooling, mid-day"
- "An uplifting Thai herbal aroma"

Five forever-toxic phrases (use never; trigger drug classification):
- "Calms the nervous system"
- "Eases anxiety"
- "Clears sinuses"
- "Activates vagal tone"
- "Supports focus" (object = body system, even though "focus" sounds cosmetic)

## Where claims appear (all governed by Path A rule)

- Product label
- Website / PDP copy
- Affiliate creator briefs (THIS is where MONQ, Young Living, doTERRA were caught — NOT the product label)
- User-generated reviews republished as marketing
- Social media organic content
- Press release language

The verb-object rule applies in ALL of these. The MONQ June 2022 warning letter, Young Living 2014, doTERRA 2014/2017, and the 2023 FTC distributor case all targeted affiliate / influencer language, not the product label itself. **Affiliate brief filtering is the highest-leverage compliance investment.**

## MoCRA registration (Path A) — concrete steps

1. Register facility (manufacturer or distributor) at FDA.gov/MoCRA. Free.
2. List product (formulation summary + label). Free.
3. Maintain adverse-event records (1-year retention).
4. Optional cosmetic GMP first-inspection (~$3k).

Total Y1: ~$3.5k.

## Trademark / IP (parallel to Path A)

- USPTO 1(b) intent-to-use filing on brand name + logo: ~$300/class + counsel ($800–1500)
- Amazon Brand Registry enrollment (post-USPTO); enables Transparency anti-counterfeit
- Thai supplier-side: NDA + IP-assignment clauses BEFORE supplier visit (see `08_supplier_strategy.md`)

These are pre-Phase-0, not deferred.
