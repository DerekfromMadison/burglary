# Wave 1 — Paid Ads Playbook (Meta + TikTok)

**Case:** thai-inhaler. **Lens:** paid acquisition, post-organic-signal. **Date:** 2026-04-27. **Constraint:** bootstrap, ≤$50k Y1, solo founder, marketing-first.

---

## 1. The play in one sentence

**Paid is the scaler, not the discoverer** — turn it on only after creative + messaging is proven organic, then use it to compress the timeline of what's already working, not to invent what hasn't.

## 2. When to flip the switch — the gating signals

Do **not** spend a paid dollar in months 1–2. The Phase 0 fake-PDP test ($3K, per E_final) is the only allowable spend before signal exists. Real paid scaling unlocks when **all four** trigger, not any one:

- **Organic conversion ≥1.5% cold → buy on Shopify PDP** (per E_final Phase 0 gate). If the PDP can't convert warm-organic traffic at 1.5%, paid traffic — which is colder — won't convert either. You'd be buying losses.
- **AOV ≥$22 sustained over 100+ orders** (proves bundle pull; without it, $89–148 indie-wellness CAC math collapses, per A_cfo).
- **One organic creator clip clears 50K views with ≥4% save rate** (saves are the wellness-purchase-intent signal; likes are vanity). This becomes the paid-ready creative — Spark Ads / boosted-post mechanic uses creator's owned handle, dropping CPM 30–40% vs. brand-handle cold ads.
- **Meta Pixel + GA4 firing 30+ purchase events** in trailing 30 days. Below this, Meta's algorithm cannot exit learning phase; spend goes to inefficient frequency-bombing.

If any one is missing at month 3, do not start paid. Re-invest in creator seeding and Amazon PPC (where intent traffic exists organically). The cheapest CAC error is the one you don't make.

## 3. The creative testing matrix — realistic minimum-viable budgets

The "27-cell test grid at $50/day each" trap is statistical theater on a $50K budget. Math: at TikTok beauty CPM ~$8 and 1.5% CR, $50/day × 14 days = $700 → ~88K impressions → ~7–10 purchases. Standard error on a 1.5% CR with 88K impressions is ±0.08% — fine. But run that across 27 cells and you've spent $18,900 to learn one thing. Wrong shape.

**Right shape — the 3×2×2 sequenced test ($5K total, 21 days):**

- **3 creative concepts (the angle test)** — these are the wedge ideas, not surface variants:
  1. **"The 4pm reset"** — solo founder POV, mid-meeting, 1-second inhale, return to keyboard. Sensory verbs only ("the scent steadies the moment"). No body claims. Tests whether the *use-occasion* hook outpulls heritage.
  2. **"Made in Chiang Mai, named by my grandmother"** (or supplier-equivalent founder narrative per E_final's heritage path) — supplier on-camera, packaging in-hand, 18-second story. Tests whether the *credible-carrier* narrative outpulls function.
  3. **"What's in my bag" tactile ASMR** — top-down, 12-second product reveal, twist-cap ASMR, no voiceover, captioned only. Tests whether the *product-shareability* hook outpulls both.

- **2 hooks per concept (the first-3-seconds test):** (a) text-overlay question ("Why is everyone in Bangkok carrying this?"), (b) cold open with the action (uncap → inhale → exhale, no setup).

- **2 audiences per concept (the targeting test):** (a) broad ages 25–44 US, women + non-binary, no interest stack — let Meta/TikTok algorithm find pattern; (b) **stacked interest layer** — Goop, Cup of Jo, Apothékary, Saje, Erewhon, Magic Mind, magnesium, sleep tonic, breathwork.

That's **12 ads** ($420/ad over 14 days = ~$5K). Per-cell volume: ~52K impressions, ~12 purchases — enough to rank-order winners, not enough to call statistical significance on small differences. That's correct: at $50K Y1, you're not running a pharma trial, you're picking the top quartile.

**Decision rule:** kill any cell with CPA >$60 after $400 spend (Meta learning-phase exit threshold). Double-down on top 2 cells with another $3K each over 14 days. By day 35, you have 2 proven creatives carrying 80%+ of spend.

## 4. The audience strategy — when each works

For an indie pre-traction wellness brand, the audience hierarchy inverts what most agencies preach.

- **Broad (no interest stack):** **Default for cold prospecting once 30+ purchase events exist.** Meta/TikTok algorithms in 2026 outperform interest stacks for brands feeding 50+ daily conversions. Don't broad-prospect from day 1 — you're feeding noise. Broad works *after* the algorithm has signal.
- **Interest-based:** **Best in months 1–3 of paid (creative-testing phase).** Stack 6–10 adjacent-brand + behavior interests (above). Narrow audience size to 1–3M. This isn't where you'll scale; it's where you'll find which creative concept hooks the right buyer.
- **1% Lookalike from purchasers:** **Wait until 200+ purchasers seed the list.** Below 200, the seed is too noisy. Lookalikes from email-subscriber lists (cheaper to build) work earlier but convert weaker.
- **Custom audiences (retargeting):** **Always-on from day 1 of paid.** PDP visitors past 30 days, video viewers >50%, IG profile engagers, cart abandoners. This is where the math actually works — see §5.

## 5. The retargeting layer — where paid actually pays back

Cold paid CAC at indie-wellness CPMs (Meta H&W CPM $20.70, +38% YoY; TikTok beauty CPM $5–10) prices most one-shot orders into a loss. **Retargeting is the only paid layer that's structurally profitable on order one.**

The compounding stack:

- **Pixel retargeting:** PDP visitors 30d, ATC-no-purchase 14d, view-content >75% video. Budget cap $20–35/day total across audiences. CPMs run 40–60% lower than cold; CR runs 3–5×. Expected CPA: $15–25 vs. $60–90 cold.
- **Email + SMS layer:** Klaviyo welcome flow (5 emails over 7 days, $0 incremental cost), abandoned-cart 3-email + 1-SMS sequence, post-purchase replenishment ping at day 45. Email contributes 25–35% of indie-wellness DTC revenue at <$0.005 cost-per-touch — this is the highest-margin "paid" channel even though it's earned. SMS converts 3–5× email but caps at 2 messages/month before opt-outs spike.
- **The compounding effect:** A creator clip drives 1,000 PDP visits. Without retargeting layer: ~15 buyers (1.5% CR). With retargeting + email capture popup + abandoned-cart sequence: ~28 buyers, half from retargeting/email. The paid layer made the organic 87% more efficient. **This is the only honest case for paid in Y1.**

## 6. The KPI thresholds

Anchored to E_final's CAC bands:

- **Blended CAC ≤$32 — strong-go.** Scale spend +50% week-over-week until CAC drifts up.
- **Blended CAC $33–45 — conditional.** Hold flat. Iterate creative weekly. Verify subscription attach rate ≥30% on the 2-pack (the LTV unlock per A_cfo / D_channel — DTC subscription LTV $128 vs. one-shot $25).
- **Blended CAC >$45 — kill or pause.** Either creative is not converting cold, or audience is wrong, or PDP is leaking. Diagnose, don't increase budget.

**MER (marketing-efficiency ratio = total revenue / total ad spend) target: ≥3.0 blended Y1.** This is the realistic indie-wellness floor — top-quartile DTC hits 4–5, but at $50K spend with subscription not yet seeded, 3.0 is the gate. Below 2.5, you're funding the brand from savings, not from customers. Track in **Polar Analytics** ($400/mo, deterministic order-level attribution, BI included — best fit for sub-$1M GMV vs. Triple Whale's $429/mo modeled-pixel approach or Northbeam's $1K/mo enterprise floor) once monthly spend clears $5K. Below $5K/mo spend, in-platform Meta + TikTok dashboards + Shopify UTM are sufficient.

## 7. The DON'T list

- **Don't broad-prospect from day 1.** Algorithms need conversion signal first. Without 30+ daily purchase events, "broad" is "expensive."
- **Don't scale unprofitable creative.** A 4× ROAS creative on $200/day stays 4× at $400/day for ~5 days, then decays as audience saturates. Doubling spend on a flat creative is the #1 cash-burn pattern. Refresh creative weekly; expect 60% of cells to die in <14 days.
- **Don't ignore statistical significance — but don't worship it either.** At $5K test budget you cannot prove a 0.3% CR delta. You can rank-order top quartile. Pick winners, kill bottom half, refresh. Iteration > certainty.
- **Don't treat influencer-whitelisting as creator marketing.** Spark Ads / TikTok whitelisting is a *paid amplifier of an existing organic hit* — it requires the organic to exist first. Paying a creator $1.5K to post + whitelist their handle for ads, before any organic signal, is just expensive paid ads with a face. Whitelist after the post earns organic reach.
- **Don't run paid before the FDA language audit clears.** A scaled non-compliant ad creative is the warning-letter trigger (per E_final's Young Living, doTERRA, MONQ pattern). Compliance review before launch ad is non-negotiable.
- **Don't pay for podcasts, broad influencer drops, or LinkedIn ads in Y1** (per D_channel #3). All three lose on first-touch math.

## 8. One thing this week

Install **Meta Pixel + Conversions API + GA4 + Polar Analytics free tier** on the Shopify PDP. **DO NOT spend a dollar on ads.** Configure the 8 priority pixel events (PageView, ViewContent, AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, Purchase, Lead/email-capture, Subscribe, custom: ScrollDepth-75). Verify with Meta Events Manager Test Events tool that all fire correctly. Set up Klaviyo with a $5-off welcome popup. **The pixel needs 60 days of organic + creator-seeded traffic data before paid spend is allowable.** This week's deliverable is *infrastructure*, not *spend*. If the pixel isn't seasoned by the time paid is appropriate, you'll burn the first $2K teaching the algorithm what a buyer looks like.
