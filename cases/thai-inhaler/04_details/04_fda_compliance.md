# FDA Compliance: The Verb-Object Rule

**TL;DR.** Look at the verb. Look at its object. Verb acts on product/scent/ritual = cosmetic-safe. Verb acts on body/system/emotion/condition = drug claim. This rule alone has killed more indie wellness brands than any competitor.

---

## The Survival Rule

**Look at the verb. Look at its object.**

- Verb acts on **product, scent, moment, ritual** → cosmetic-safe.
- Verb acts on **body, body system, emotion, named condition** → drug claim, regardless of how soft the language sounds.

**Test:** cross out the noun after the verb. If what remains is a body or body-state, it is a drug claim.

Applies everywhere brand-controlled — label, website, paid ads, sponsored content, affiliate posts, customer-service emails. FDA's intended-use rule (21 CFR § 201.128, effective Sep 2021) sweeps in "expressions, the design or composition of the article, or the circumstances surrounding the distribution." Every word your affiliate posts is your word.

Young Living 2014 and FTC v. doTERRA distributors (Mar 2023, $15k each + permanent injunction) were lost in the affiliate channel, not on the label.

## Five Forever-Safe Phrases

1. "An aromatic 1-second reset"
2. "Your morning glimmer"
3. "An uplifting Thai herbal aroma"
4. "A pocket-sized fragrance experience"
5. "Inspired by ya dom, a Thai pocket ritual"

## Five Forever-Toxic Phrases

1. "Calms the nervous system"
2. "Boosts focus" / "improves concentration"
3. "Relieves congestion" / "clears sinuses"
4. "Helps you sleep" / "reduces anxiety"
5. "Therapeutic-grade" / "clinically proven"

## Meta-Rule

When in doubt, **describe the smell, not the feeling**. Olbas Inhaler runs this on Amazon — "aromatic vapors of menthol, peppermint, cajeput and eucalyptol provide an immediately refreshing sensation" — and has held it for years.

"Calm" is the live grenade. Allow only with sensory objects (aroma, ritual, moment), never physiological (nerves, stress, anxiety). "Polyvagal," "vagal tone," "supports the nervous system" — the entire polyvagal vocabulary the wellness creator economy treats as default-safe — are double-trigger drug claims. No structure-function harbor for non-supplements; DSHEA requires ingestion.

## MoCRA Registration

MoCRA (Pub. L. 117-328 Title III Subtitle F, Dec 29 2022):

1. **Register manufacturing facility** via Cosmetic Direct portal. Annual renewal. Free.
2. **List each product** with ingredients, label, intended use.
3. **Designate Responsible Person** (founder or counsel).
4. **Adverse Event records** 3 years (6 for serious). Report serious within 15 business days.
5. **Substantiate safety** — allergen, supplier COAs, microbial testing. Hong Thai Lot 000332 (Dec 2024) is a direct precedent.
6. **Labeling per 21 CFR § 701.3** — INCI, contact, net contents, origin, lot.
7. **Small-business exemption** ($1M revenue) exempts facility registration + listing only — NOT adverse-event reporting, labeling, or claim rule.

Mandatory creator brief: say/don't-say sheet, affiliate TOS auto-clawback, quarterly scan. Zero biohacker creators. Zero nootropic. Zero anyone with prior Young Living/doTERRA/MONQ/Hong Thai/Poy-Sian sponsorship in 12 months.
