# Wave-1 Marketing — PR / Earned Media Playbook

**Specialty**: PR / earned media for a bootstrap, solo, greenfield Thai-herbal-inhaler launch. Honest about timing — PR is post-traction amplification, but the *seeds* are planted at launch.

---

## 1. The play in one sentence

Earned media is a **post-product-market-fit amplifier**, not a launch lever — pre-PMF, you are doing two things only: (a) seeding small niche-publication and Substack relationships that mature into Tier-1 features 9–18 months later, and (b) ensuring the *first credible byline* that ever Googles the brand finds the credible-carrier scaffolding from D_credible_carrier already on the homepage.

---

## 2. The 3 tiers of earned media (and when each becomes addressable)

**Tier 1 — National lifestyle (defer to month 12+).** NYT Style, *The Cut*, *Air Mail* (with the new "Look" beauty-and-wellness vertical run by Linda Wells, ex-*Allure* founding editor), NYT *Wirecutter*, NY Mag *The Strategist*, *Vogue Business*, *Cherry Bombe*, *Eater*. These outlets ignore brands with <$500k LTM revenue or <50k IG followers. Pitching them in month 1 burns your one shot. Build the relationships in month 6–9 *as a source* (offer founder-as-expert quotes on aromatherapy / Thai wellness / FDA cosmetic-claim rules); pitch the brand profile only after a Tier-2 piece exists to send as social proof.

**Tier 2 — Substack + indie editorial (month 3+).** Joanna Goddard's *Big Salad* (Substack #1 fashion/beauty newsletter, run from *Cup of Jo*), *Maintenance Phase* (Aubrey Gordon + Michael Hobbes, wellness-skeptic but exactly the audience that respects normalized non-mystical Thai pharmacy framing), *Conspirituality* (Derek Beres / Matthew Remski / Julian Walker — same audience, same hook), Anjali Prasertong's Substack (food + cultural-appropriation-aware), Soleil Ho's COYOTE Media work, Andrea Nguyen's *Pass the Fish Sauce* recommendation list, *The Florentine*-style indie city/lifestyle Substacks. Tier 2 is reachable cold in month 3 once you have the founder essay + supplier transparency page live.

**Tier 3 — Niche newsletters + podcasts (month 1+).** Aromatherapy/essential-oil reviewer Substacks, Asian-American food/lifestyle podcasts (*Self Evident* — Cathy Erway; *Time To Say Goodbye*; Cold Tea Collective newsletter), productivity/focus newsletters, indie CPG podcasts (*DTC Pod* with Blaine Bolus + Ramon Berrios, *Indie Hackers*, *Indie Bites*). Tier 3 doesn't move volume directly but creates the search-result trail Tier 2 writers find when they Google the brand.

## 3. The pitch hook — the story arc journalists want

**The headline you're writing toward** (per A_pr Section 2a): *"This Thai-American Founder Wants To Replace Your Stress Ball With A 200-Year-Old Pharmacy Staple."* Or for a non-Thai founder with a Thai supplier-co-founder per the decisions.md committed path: *"How Two Founders — One in Brooklyn, One in Chiang Mai — Are Bringing Thailand's $1 Pharmacy Staple to American Desks."*

The hook has four moving parts and you need all four:

1. **Founder narrative** — Thai or Thai-American founder, OR a non-Thai founder who has put the Thai supplier on the cap table at 15–25% with named co-founder credit (per E_final founder-identity bifurcation table).
2. **Cultural-carrier angle** — ya dom is a 100+ year working-pharmacy product, $1 in Bangkok 7-Elevens, used millions of times daily. Not "ancient wisdom." Normalization, not exoticization. (This is the Fly By Jing / Omsom / Diaspora Co. template; D_credible_carrier Move 4.)
3. **The thesis** — "ya dom is bigger than aromatherapy." Aromatherapy is a Western lavender-coded category; ya dom is a different category — a 1-second sensory reset, working-class portable, no diffuser theater, no mystical claims. The brand is *correcting a misperception*, not introducing exotica.
4. **The 1-second-reset POV** — sensory action, not chemistry claim. "What you do between Slack messages." Defensible against FDA verb-object rule (per E_final point 3) because the verb's object is the *moment*, not the body system.

Bundle these into a 250-word founder origin essay that lives on the brand's Substack day-one (per D_credible_carrier Move 5). That essay is the press kit for 18 months.

## 6. The press kit (1-pager, written in week 1)

A single PDF + a `/press` page on the brand site. Contents:

- **Founder bio** — 100 words, Thai connection explicit (or Thai supplier-co-founder named with bio).
- **Story** — 250-word origin essay, dated, first-person. The Jing Gao Medium piece is the template (D_credible_carrier Move 5).
- **Product hi-res** — 5–8 images, including (a) product alone on white, (b) product in hand, (c) product at desk/in bag, (d) supplier or workshop image, (e) named producer with consent.
- **Ya dom 101 explainer** — 200 words: what it is, what it *isn't*, why "Thai aromatherapy" is the wrong analogy, how it differs from Western essential-oil practice.
- **Founder POV essay** — 500 words on the 1-second-reset thesis, FDA-compliant language only (per E_final point 3 verb-object rule).
- **Sourcing transparency page link** — "How We Pay Our Producers" (D_credible_carrier Move 2 + 5–10% revenue share or 4× FOB disclosure).
- **High-res logo, brand colors, founder headshot, supplier headshot.**
- **Two pre-written sample quotes** for journalists to lift.
- **One-line crisis-response paragraph** (per A_pr recommendation 4) — kept private, not on the site, but ready to email within 4 hours.

Cost: $0–800 for the founder; $1.5–3k if outsourced.

## 7. The metrics

- **Earned mentions count** — target: 1 Tier-3 piece by month 3, 2 Tier-2 pieces by month 6, 1 Tier-1 piece by month 12.
- **Backlinks from earned mentions** — track via Ahrefs free / Google Search Console. Target: 10+ DR-50+ backlinks by month 12 (these compound into Amazon + Google search rank).
- **Brand search volume** — Google Trends + Amazon search frequency (via Helium 10 or Jungle Scout). Target: trackable brand-name search volume by month 6.
- **Tier-2 mentions → Tier-1 pitch conversion rate** — when you forward a Tier-2 piece to a Tier-1 writer, do they reply? Below 20% = pitch is wrong. Above 40% = the angle is working.

---

## 8. The DON'T list

- **No paid PR firms at indie-launch budget.** $5–15k/month buys generic outreach to lists you can build yourself in two afternoons. PR firms only earn their keep above $5M ARR when relationships compound. Below that, they relay press releases.
- **No PRWeb / generic press releases.** Zero earned-media value, hurts Google rankings (duplicate content), and signals "we don't have a real story."
- **No ego-bait pitches** ("first Thai-American founder to..." — unless verifiably true and verifiably *first*; otherwise read as marketing).
- **No reverse-order seeding.** Do not pitch white wellness creators or generic mommy-bloggers before Thai-American creators (per D_credible_carrier Move 7). Reverse order locks the brand into appropriation-arc slot #2 permanently.
- **No defending in the first 48 hours of any criticism.** Acknowledge impact within 4 hours, change copy within 24, write a public response within 72 (per A_pr Section 2b Trigger 6).
- **No HARO blasts.** Unfocused, low-yield, signal as "we're a brand, please cover us."

## 9. One thing this week

Two parallel actions, both this week:

**(a) Write the 1-pager press kit** (the founder origin essay is the load-bearing piece). 250 words on heritage connection (or supplier-partnership origin), the named Thai supplier, the producer-pricing model, and the 1-second-reset thesis. Templates: Jing Gao's Medium piece; Sana Javeri Kadri's Diaspora "Big Story" entry. Time: 4 hours.

**(b) Identify and warm-contact 3 specific Substack writers with a personal-essay-by-founder pitch.** From the list in Section 4, the easiest cold-reach trio is **Anjali Prasertong + Andrea Nguyen + Ellie Yang Camp**. The pitch is *not* "please cover my brand"; it is "I'm a founder working on a brand around Thai herbal inhalers; I noticed your piece on X; would you read 250 words on the angle and tell me if it lands?" That email gets replies. The brand pitch lands later, after the relationship exists. Time: 90 minutes.

Both actions cost $0. They are the seeds Tier 1 grows from in month 12.
