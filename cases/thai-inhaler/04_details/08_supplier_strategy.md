# Supplier strategy — thai-inhaler

**TL;DR**: Target OEM blank-label manufacturers (Medicos-tier, $1.50–2.50/unit landed) with master-distributor exclusive licensing as the relationship structure — NOT principal-brand cap-table equity. v3 targeted Poy-Sian / Hong Thai (brand principals with global distribution, wrong tier) and proposed cap-table equity in a pre-rev US LLC (low-status frame in Thai-Chinese family-business culture). Realistic Thai supplier-relationship timeline: 9–12 months. Decouple from Phase 0.

## What changed from v3

| v3 said | v4 says | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cap-table equity 15–25% to Poy-Sian or Hong Thai | OEM blank-label at Medicos-tier | Poy-Sian (Gold Mints / Larbboonsarp family, est. 1934) and Hong Thai (est. 1930s) are brand principals with their own global distribution; they don't need a US channel partner — they ARE the brand |
| 60-day signed LOI / 120-day equity executed | 9–12 month full relationship; Day 60 = qualified intro + verbal interest | Thai business norms (kreng jai, bhunkhun, relationship-first) make 60-day equity transactions impossible; precedents like Diaspora Co.'s farmer relationships took 7+ months |
| Supplier-equity is a Phase 0 gate | Supplier timeline DECOUPLED from Phase 0; use stocked OEM for Phase 0 inventory | Phase 0 cannot wait 9 months |
| Named co-founder credit to supplier | Master-distributor exclusive licensing for US (equity upgrade in Year 2+ optional) | Cap-table equity in pre-rev US LLC reads low-status; master distributor is the standard structure for foreign-market expansion in Thai business culture |

## Tier comparison

| Tier | Examples | Per-unit landed cost | Equity expectation | Brand-recognition value | Right for indie launch? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Brand principal | Poy-Sian (Gold Mints), Hong Thai, Siang Pure, Kwan Loong | $4–7 retail in Thailand | None — they're the brand | Built-in (good for licensing deals later) | NO at indie launch |
| OEM blank-label (Medicos-tier) | ~10–15 Thai contract manufacturers (find via BOI directories, Alibaba, Thai Trade Center) | $1.50–2.50 landed | None — straight contract | None (build your own brand) | **YES** |
| OEM with branded heritage license | Bertram Chemical (parent of Tiger Balm), other regional consolidators | $3–5 landed | License fees | Inherited brand halo | Phase 2 option |

For a bootstrap indie launch, OEM blank-label is the right tier. Brand-principal partnerships are for established brands seeking distribution exclusivity in the other direction (Thai brand → US market, with US partner as channel).

## Realistic Thai timeline

Per `_logs/v3_critique_asia_startup.md`, Thai business norms make rapid equity transactions unrealistic. Map the relationship across these milestones:

| Day | Milestone | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Cold outreach | Via BOI / Alibaba / Thailand Trade Center US-side rep |
| 30 | Sample shipment (~$300–500) | Founder receives 50–100 units |
| 60 | Qualified intro + verbal interest | NOT a signed LOI; in-person or video meeting with senior family member or factory owner, not just procurement contact |
| 90 | First production sample | ~100 units of custom formulation |
| 120 | LOI / pricing terms exchanged | First written commitment |
| 180 | MOU + 500-unit first PO | Production begins |
| 270 | First shipment lands in US | Phase 1 inventory |
| 360 | Exclusive contract executed | If relationship has held |
| 540+ (Y2) | Optional equity / IP-share upgrade | If founder still committed and supplier interested |

Phase 0 (Week 6) does NOT depend on the strategic supplier signing. **For Phase 0**: use a pre-approved Alibaba OEM (any Medicos-tier manufacturer that ships within 30 days). The strategic supplier-relationship build runs in parallel as a 9–12 month effort.

## Cultural-norm guardrails

- **No cap-table equity offer in first 6 months.** Reads as transactional / low-status.
- **Lead with respect.** In-person meeting if possible (use Thailand Trade Center US-side intros, or a Thai-American business broker). Video meet with senior family member, not just procurement. Bring a small gift. Don't lead with terms.
- **Pricing comes after relationship.** Do NOT negotiate price in the first 2 conversations. Talk about the family business, the founder's experience with the product, the US market opportunity.
- **Master-distributor exclusivity for US is the offer.** Standard Thai business-norm structure for foreign-market expansion. Includes: minimum-order commitment, marketing budget commitment, brand-protection clauses.
- **Royalty / revenue share** can be the form of "equity-feeling" without cap-table dilution. A 4–7% royalty on US revenue, escrowed to a Thai supplier-family-aligned holding company, is the cleaner structure.

## Regulatory considerations

- **Thai Foreign Business Act**: caps foreign equity at 49% in most sectors. BOI exemptions exist for certain cosmetics manufacturing — investigate via BOI Investment Promotion Guide before commitments.
- **Thai Herbal Product Act B.E. 2562**: classifies ya dom as traditional medicine in Thailand. Export to US may require Thai FDA Herbal Product Act formula registration — 90–180 days. The OEM you choose should already have export-to-US registration.
- **Hong Thai Thai-FDA raid + recall (Oct 2025)**: supplier compliance history matters. Vet OEMs for Thai-FDA enforcement record before MOQ commitment.
- **US side**: see `04_fda_compliance_v2.md` — camphor inclusion vs. cosmetic Path A.

## IP / security guardrails (parallel)

- USPTO 1(b) trademark filed BEFORE supplier visit
- NDA with Singapore-seat arbitration clause BEFORE sharing formulation details
- IP-assignment clause in any sample contract (the supplier owns the original formula; the brand owns the distinctive customization)
- DO NOT publicly disclose supplier name until exclusive contract is executed (~Day 360)
