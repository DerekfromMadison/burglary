# Wave-1 — TikTok Organic + Creator-Led Playbook

**Case**: thai-inhaler · **Wave**: 1 (marketing strategy sub-agent) · **Date**: 2026-04-27
**Constraints**: bootstrap, solo founder, greenfield, ≤$50k Y1.
**Inputs**: 00_intake, E_final, A_customer, D_creator_fit, decisions.md.

---

## 1. The play in one sentence

TikTok is the **trial engine** for this brand — it produces the sub-$30 impulse-buy moments Personas A and B are already primed for, generates the creator content that becomes the entire paid-media library, and lets a solo founder out-execute Saje by being a person Saje can never be — but TikTok will **not** carry the FDA-language burden, will **not** convert the cultural-authenticity gatekeeper alone (Persona C needs supplier-story content that lives off-platform too), and will **not** survive being treated as a "broadcast 30 videos and pray" channel post-USDS where unverified accounts under 50K eat a 30–50% reach haircut ([Miraflow 2026](https://miraflow.ai/blog/tiktok-ban-2026-where-creators-are-moving)).

## 2. The format mix — 5 specific video formats

### (a) Founder-on-camera POV — "Desk Drawer Reset" (3pm Tuesday genre)
Founder seated at a real desk, real laptop, real Slack notification. Pulls a tube from drawer or pocket. Unscrews. Inhales. Exhales. **Hard cut to scent description as text overlay**: "eucalyptus, camphor, something woody — the smell of a Thai grandmother's medicine cabinet." No claim. No "calm." Just: the moment, the smell, the pause. **First 1.5 seconds = inhaler in hand**. Variant for Persona B: kitchen counter at 5:42pm, kid background noise. Variant for the unvoiced male persona: open Notion, "the fidget that isn't a fidget."

### (b) Founder-on-camera supplier-story documentary — "Where this comes from"
30–60 seconds. Shot in Chiang Rai or Chiang Mai (or via shipped video clips from supplier if founder hasn't traveled yet — explicit "I haven't been there yet, here's what they sent me" is more honest than B-roll). Named Thai herbalist on screen. Their hands, their workspace. Founder voice-over names the equity / revenue-share arrangement publicly. **This is the Persona C unlock + Bon Appétit-blowup insurance in one asset.** Per `D_creator_fit` Pailin-tier collaboration logic — own the supplier-story content, then let creators reference it.

### (c) Founder-on-camera "haul / what's in my bag" — counter-format
Founder shows their *own* bag. Inhaler is one of 6–8 items: a pen, a chapstick, a paperback, the inhaler, AirPods, a $1 Bangkok 7-Eleven Hong Thai inhaler placed next to ours with the caption *"this is the original — we honor it, ours is different, here's why."* Format hijack: most "what's in my bag" videos pretend the brand is the only thing in the world. Acknowledging Hong Thai by name buys cultural credibility cheaper than any paid post — directly per `A_customer.md` Persona C "humility is conversion."

### (d) Creator-led ASMR / sensory unboxing
Gifted to one of the 50K–400K ASMR-wellness creators in the seed list (Celaine-tier, Latte ASMR-tier crossover). 60–90s tactile demo: cap-click, glass-tube sound, scent-description whisper. **No script.** Brief = "describe the smell honestly, hold the tube up to the light, do not say 'calm'." `D_creator_fit` Archetype 4 conversion thesis: ASMR drives saves, not first-purchase — but saves drive month-3 conversion at near-zero CAC.

### (e) Creator-led Thai-American "yai-bag" / heritage cameo
Gifted to 5K–50K Thai-American micro-creators. Format brief: "Show what your yai (or mom, or auntie) carried in her bag growing up. Place ours next to a Hong Thai or Poy-Sian. Tell the truth about the difference." Per `D_creator_fit` Tier-3 thesis — the under-indexed leverage. Engagement 8–12%, gifting cost ~$16/creator, cultural-shield value highest.

## 3. The 30-day content calendar — first 30 videos

Founder posts daily. 30 founder videos in 30 days. Hooks are organized by category to teach the algorithm *what kind of account* this is before it tries to categorize. Per [Social Growth Engineers 2025/2026 organic report](https://www.socialgrowthengineers.com/2025-tiktok-organic-growth-report-lessons-trends-and-the-road-to-2026): polished brand-heavy content underperforms creator-style raw video; daily posting + format consistency wins.

### Days 1–7 — "Origin" (hook type: who am I, why this)
1. Founder pulls inhaler from pocket on camera. 8 seconds. No words. Caption only: *"started a brand around the thing my partner kept stealing from me."*
2. Founder speaks to camera, 30s, naming Hong Thai and Poy-Sian explicitly: *"I am not pretending I invented this."*
3. Founder reads a Persona-C-style blowup tweet ("another white wellness brand 'discovers' yadom") on camera. Responds with: "fair. here is what we're doing differently."
4. Supplier-story V1 — Chiang Rai supplier on screen, named, photographed. (Format b above.)
5. "What's in my bag" with Hong Thai and ours side by side. (Format c.)
6. Founder shows the FDA verb-object rule taped to monitor. Caption: *"why we will never say 'calm' on this account."*
7. Q&A reply to comments from days 1–6. Algorithm rewards reply-stitches.

### Days 8–14 — "Use case" (hook type: when this matters)
8. 3pm desk reset POV. (Format a.)
9. Carpool-line POV (Persona B). Founder in driver's seat, kid background noise.
10. Middle-seat-airplane POV. Filmed on actual flight.
11. Post-acupuncture afterglow POV. *Don't claim acupuncture works — just film the moment.*
12. Sunday-night-dread POV. Couch, blanket, dim light.
13. Pre-meeting POV. Conference room, laptop open.
14. Reply-stitch to top comment from days 8–13.

### Days 15–21 — "Smell" (hook type: olfactory translation)
15. Three scent notes spoken to camera, 12s. *"Eucalyptus, camphor, something woody — sandalwood mixed with mint."*
16. Side-by-side smell test: Hong Thai vs. Poy-Sian vs. ours. Founder reacts honestly, including which one wins on which dimension.
17. "How a Thai herbalist describes this smell" — quote supplier on screen with translation.
18. Reading mean comments about the smell ("smells like vapor rub for sad people"). Founder: "yes."
19. Founder takes one to a friend's house, films friend's reaction.
20. ASMR-style cap-click + scent-pull, 30s, whispered.
21. Reply-stitch.

### Days 22–30 — "Ritual" (hook type: who else uses this and when)
22. Day-in-the-life with 4 specific moments the founder uses one (not a "wellness routine" — just real moments).
23. "5 things in my desk that actually help" — inhaler is #3, not #1. Trend hijack: ADHD-tok.
24. Repurposed creator content — clip from a gifted ASMR post (whitelist permission).
25. Repurposed Thai-American creator content — clip from a yai-bag post.
26. "How Persona C reacted when I sent her an early sample" — real screenshot, real reply.
27. Behind-the-scenes packing day. Real boxes, handwritten cards visible.
28. The supplier on a video call with the founder. Real conversation, light translation.
29. "30 days in, here's what people are actually saying" — UGC mosaic, 60s.
30. Founder direct-to-camera: *"Phase 0 is done. Here's what the data said."* Whether the data is good or bad is the video. Honesty IS the differentiation.

**Hook types deliberately ABSENT**: "POV: you have anxiety," "Things that helped my nervous system heal," "Why your vagus nerve hates you" — all are organic-traffic-magnet hooks **AND all are FDA verb-object trip wires** per `E_final` finding 3 + `D_creator_fit` risk (iv).

### Outreach copy template (DM or email, ≤90 words)

> Hi [name] — [founder name] here, building [brand]. Not asking for a post. I want to send you a small package: an inhaler, a Hong Thai (the Thai original — I want you to compare), a one-page note on what we will and won't say about this product, and the name + photo of the Thai herbalist we work with in [region]. If you like it and post, please tell the truth about how it stacks up. If you don't, no follow-up. Address?

The "Hong Thai included" line is the differentiator — it pre-empts the cultural-authenticity blowup *and* gives the creator content material no other brand provides.

## 5. The metrics to watch — thresholds for "working" vs. "not"

Per [TikTok Shop 2026 brand wins](https://www.inc.com/annabel-burba/4-breakout-strategies-fueling-brand-wins-on-tiktok-shop-in-2026/91302176) + Olipop / Apothékary documented benchmarks. Vanity metrics (likes, follower count) are deprioritized; intent-signal metrics carry the read.

| Metric | "Working" threshold (week 4–8) | "Not working" — pivot signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Saves per video** (founder-organic) | ≥3% of views, sustained across 5+ videos | <1% across 10+ videos = wrong audience or wrong format |
| **Completion rate** (founder POV ≤15s) | ≥65% | <40% = hook isn't landing in first 1.5s |
| **Profile visits / view ratio** | ≥1% | <0.3% = videos work, brand doesn't — name/PDP is the issue |
| **Link-clicks / profile-visit ratio** | ≥8% | <3% = bio link CTA / landing page broken |
| **Gifted creator post-rate** | ≥30% (24–36% per [SeedingOps 2026](https://seedingops.com/blog/ultimate-guide-influencer-product-seeding-2026)) | <15% over 30 creators = brief / package / story is wrong, not the creator pool |
| **Comments asking "where buy"** | Day 1 of any post hitting >25k views | Absent at >100k views = product doesn't translate to buy-intent |
| **First sale attributable to TikTok** (Amazon screenshot search OR direct PDP click) | Day 14–21 | Day 30 with no attributable sale = the funnel breaks somewhere — likely PDP, not TikTok |
| **First 1k views on a single video** | Day 5–14 | Day 30 still not hitting 1k = account is shadow-banned (post-USDS unverified penalty) — verify the account |

The "not working" thresholds are specifically calibrated to avoid the trap of declaring failure too early. Olipop's TikTok account took 18 months to compound; the founder reads a Day-21 result, not a Day-3 result.

## 6. The DON'T list

These are non-negotiable. Per `E_final` findings 2–3, `D_creator_fit` risk (iv), and `A_customer.md` Persona-A churn triggers.

1. **No biohacker / nootropic creators.** Existential risk per `E_final` finding 6. Brand-killing single point of failure.
2. **No drug-claim language anywhere — caption, voiceover, or comment reply.** Banned vocabulary: anxiety, depression, ADHD (as a treatment), insomnia, congestion, headache, migraine, cure, heal, treat, prevent, reduce, regulates, supports, activates, calms.
3. **No polyvagal vocabulary in captions or branded copy.** "Supports the nervous system," "activates parasympathetic / vagal tone," "regulates your vagus nerve" are all double-trigger drug claims per `E_final` finding 3 — the verb's object is a body system. Creators *can* use polyvagal vocabulary in their own organic-voice content; the **brand account cannot**. Founder must enforce this on creator briefs with the language card.
4. **No "Calm. Restore. Awaken." copy.** Dead language. Persona-A bounce trigger per `A_customer.md` § 3.
5. **No "I discovered yadom in Thailand" founder narrative.** Persona-C blowup trigger. Frame founder as operator, supplier as co-founder.
6. **No agency-produced content first 90 days.** Raw founder content out-converts polished brand content per [TikTok 2026 organic report](https://www.socialgrowthengineers.com/2025-tiktok-organic-growth-report-lessons-trends-and-the-road-to-2026). Save the agency budget for spark ads on validated organic winners (month 4+).
7. **No paid-amplifying small-creator content via Spark Ads pre-month-3.** Post-USDS algo penalizes paid-amplified sub-50K creator content. Build organic equity first, then amplify the winners.
8. **No "elevating" yadom positioning.** Don't pretend $4 7-Eleven inhalers are inferior. Per `A_customer.md` Persona C: "Treat the $4 7-Eleven version as canonical, not as something to 'improve.'"
9. **No TikTok Shop affiliate program in month 1.** Wait until month 3+ when 5+ creators have organically posted. Affiliate program with no organic gravity = bottom-of-barrel creators chasing commission, claim-language risk multiplies.
10. **No medical-coded review excerpts in marketing.** A real customer review saying "fixed my anxiety" cannot be screenshotted and reposted. The customer can say it; the brand cannot quote it. `E_final` finding 2 — claim language is applied to the entire surrounding context.

## 7. One thing this week

**Monday–Tuesday**: Record 1 founder POV video (Format a, "Desk Drawer Reset"). 8 seconds. No script. iPhone, no ring light, no caption beyond "started a brand around the thing my partner kept stealing from me." Post Tuesday 7pm ET.

**Wednesday**: Pack and ship 5 gifted samples — 1 nervous-system educator (Tier 2, not Dr. Aimie yet), 2 Thai-American micros (5K–30K), 1 ADHD/focus creator (mid-50K), 1 fragrance/scent-diary critic. Each box: 3-pack starter, handwritten card, language card, supplier-story 1-pager, a Hong Thai for honest comparison. Total cost: ~$80 product + ~$40 shipping = ~$120.

**Thursday–Sunday**: 4 more founder videos (Days 2–5 of the 30-day calendar). Reply to every comment on Tuesday's video by name. Watch the saves number.

That is the entire week. No website redesign, no paid ads, no agency call, no logo iteration. Daily founder content + 5 packages out the door. The compounding starts on Day 7 when the first creator opens the box and the first follow-up DM goes out.
