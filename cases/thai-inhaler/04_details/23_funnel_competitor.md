**Case**: thai-inhaler
**Specialty**: Funnel-stage tactic mapping + action-form competitor positioning
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Constraints**: bootstrap, solo, greenfield, marketing-first, ≤$50k first 6 months

## 1. The play in one sentence

**Discovery is TikTok-organic + Reddit + IRL-sampling, consideration is a single hard-working PDP plus founder-led LinkedIn, trial is a $14 single-stick with a sample-box gate, first-purchase is a $24 2-pack defaulted at checkout, repeat is SMS+subscription on the 2-pack, and advocacy is a referral-credit + UGC-repost loop — each stage owns one channel so the solo founder can actually execute it.**

**The wedge sits at the high-sensory, premium-price quadrant — same shelf-rent as Apothékary, Saje, Vitruvi.** Empty space being defended: **premium ($24+) + active-fast-format (1s nasal) + sensory-allusion language + heritage-anchored Asian rooting.** No US brand currently occupies it. Apothékary owns the language (sublingual, ~3s) but not the format. Saje owns the format-adjacent (roll-on) but not heritage. Hong Thai/Poy-Sian own heritage but not premium-American codes. Vicks owns OTC mass and cannot move up without breaking medical-trust equity.

**The defensible side of the wedge** is heritage-rooting + 1-second format. Saje can copy the format in 90 days but cannot fabricate Thai-heritage. Apothékary can shift heritage angle (Japanese → Thai is a stretch) but reformatting from sublingual to nasal-stick is a 6–9 month dev cycle. The space stays defended for ~6 months minimum if founder-identity work lands.

## 4. The 5-second elevator answer (coffee-shop sample table)

> "It's a Thai herbal nasal stick — Vicks format, Apothékary intent. One second, no caffeine, no screen. $14 to try."

That sentence does five jobs at once: locates the format (Vicks), locates the cultural job (Apothékary's reset/glimmer language), names the heritage (Thai), specifies the speed (one second), and prices the trial low enough that the prospect's wallet opens before the ego argues. It works on a 32-year-old wellness customer, a 28-year-old male productivity-stack guy, and a 45-year-old yoga teacher equally — none have to translate.

## 5. Conversion-rate targets by funnel stage

| Transition | Target | 2026 indie-wellness benchmark | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery → Trial (visitor → signup or first add-to-cart) | **5%** | 3–6% for indie wellness DTC; matches Shopify health/wellness 2.5–4% CVR plus newsletter signup uplift | Solo founder cannot hit this without strong creator pickup. Plan = bootstrap creator gifting, not paid. |
| Trial → First Purchase (add-to-cart → completed checkout) | **30%** | Shopify checkout-CVR median ~30% in beauty/wellness with cart-abandon recovery + free-shipping threshold | Apothékary's $99 free-ship threshold is the model. Replicate at $36. |
| First Purchase → Repeat (30-day) | **15%** | 30–45% is "strong" for established beauty; for new launch month-1 a 15% target is honest, then grow to 25–30% by month 6 | Subscription attach is the lever. SMS day-21 "subscribe & save 15%" does the work. |
| Repeat → Advocacy (referral-attributed sales) | **5%** by mo 6 | Beauty/wellness indie referral programs typically hit 3–8% of revenue when deployed cleanly | Give-$5/get-$5 is the cheapest format; don't over-engineer. |

**Math sanity check** with these targets: 50,000 monthly impressions → 1,000 PDP visits → 50 trials (5%) → 15 purchases (30%) → AOV $22 = $330/mo gross at month 3. At month 6 with 4× the impressions and 15% repeat compounding, the run-rate hits ~$3k/mo. **This is a slow build, not a hockey stick.** Anyone promising the founder $50k MRR in 6 months on $50k bootstrap is selling something.

## 6. The DON'T list

1. **No full-funnel paid ads at launch.** Burning the $50k on Meta + Google before the PDP converts at ≥3% organic is the #1 indie wellness failure mode. Find product-message fit on free traffic first.
2. **No cohort attribution before product-market fit.** Solo founder running first-touch / last-touch / multi-touch attribution at <$10k/mo revenue is theater. UTM tags + Shopify default reports + ask-at-checkout "how did you hear about us" — that's the stack until $20k MRR.
3. **No competitor-name PPC bidding** (no "Saje" or "Apothékary" keyword buys) at <$5k CAC budget. Trademark complaints are filed in 48 hours and ad accounts get flagged. Even if it worked, $30 CPCs on those terms break the unit economics.
4. **No drugstore-aisle distribution** (CVS, Walgreens, Target health-and-wellness) in year 1 — puts $24 product 6 inches from $6.78 Vicks and consumer pattern-match destroys the framing.
5. **No biohacker / nootropic creator seeding** — that's where FDA + FTC enforcement lands (per E_final, MONQ / Young Living / doTERRA precedent).
6. **No "calms anxiety / supports nervous system / activates vagal tone" copy.** Per D_fda_language verb-object rule: drug claim, no safe-harbor, brand-killer at warning-letter scale.

## 7. One thing this week

**Sketch the funnel by hand on one sheet of paper, then walk through the conversion math out loud with realistic 2026 indie-wellness benchmarks.** Force every target to clear two questions: (i) is it benchmark-grounded or wishful? (ii) do I have the channel-execution capacity as a solo operator? If a stage has a 5%+ target and no realistic creator/SEO/IRL plan attached, downgrade it before the launch. Then take the sketch into one IRL conversation — yoga studio owner, coworking community manager, or local coffee shop — and ask: "would you let me sample 50 sticks at your front desk for one week?" If that conversation is too uncomfortable to have, the brand is too uncomfortable to launch.

The reason this is *the* one thing: every other tactical decision (creator brief, PDP copy, ad creative, retail pitch) cascades from the funnel-stage targets. Get the math right on one page; everything else writes itself.
