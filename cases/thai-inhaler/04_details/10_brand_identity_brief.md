# Brand identity brief — Week 1 sprint

**TL;DR**: v3 specified no name, no scent, no visual territory, no voice. Phase 0 cannot validate an undesigned product at $24 AOV — buyers will not pay premium for a sleeved Poy-Sian with no visual or sensory commitment. Week 1 sprint produces visual territory + name + scent brief + 1 mockup. ~$3–5k.

## What gets decided in Week 1

### 1. Visual territory (pick 1; recommend luxury-modernist)

| Territory | Visual signature | Reference brands | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Luxury-modernist** (RECOMMEND) | Apothecary minimalism. Oat / sage / muted gold palette. Sans-serif geometric type. Weighty single-tube packaging. | Aēsop, Le Labo, Apothékary post-rebrand | $24 AOV anchor; premium-without-medical-claim signal |
| Folk-craft heritage | Hand-illustrated Thai botanicals. Terracotta / ochre / indigo. Hand-set serif. Riso-printed paper sleeve. | Diaspora Co, Pinch Spice | If founder is Thai-American with strong heritage narrative |
| Apothecary-clinical | Lab-coded white + amber. Technical typography. Ingredient % visible. | The Ordinary, The Nue Co | If positioning is functional / "ingredients first" |

Recommend luxury-modernist:
- Matches $24 AOV anchor (drugstore Vicks at $4 won't be confused)
- Signals "premium without medical claim" (consistent with cosmetic Path A)
- Differentiates from Saje (mid-tier wellness), Vicks (drugstore), Poy-Sian (foreign-coded)
- Apothékary is the closest competitor here — study their post-rebrand visual system

### 2. Name (5 candidates, USPTO + .com cleared)

Generate via 1-day brand-naming sprint with a freelance brand strategist.

**Avoid**:
- Anything translatable to English ("Inhale," "Reset," "Breathe")
- Anything Thai-language unless founder is Thai (cultural-appropriation risk)
- 2-syllable verbs ("inhale," "uplift") — saturated

**Aim for**:
- 1–2 syllable noun
- Distinct phoneme
- Evocative not literal
- Pronounceable in English without confusion

Prepare 5 candidates. Run through:
- USPTO TESS search (free)
- .com domain availability
- Pronunciation test (5 friends say it back)

Keep #1 + #2; trademark counsel files USPTO 1(b) on top choice, watches #2.

### 3. Scent brief (9-note structure)

The smell IS the PDP — buyers can't read claims, they bounce if they can't imagine the scent in 3 words. The OEM perfumer needs a 1-page brief.

**Structure**:
- **Top notes** (2): the 3-second-encounter impression. E.g., bergamot, white tea
- **Heart notes** (4): the differentiator. E.g., menthol, eucalyptus, lemongrass, kaffir lime — Thai herbs are HERE
- **Base notes** (3): grounding. E.g., borneol, vetiver, light oud

**Brief includes**:
- Target experience descriptor (3 words): e.g., "bright, cooling, mid-day"
- Use-occasion: pre-meeting reset, mid-afternoon shift, post-commute
- Reference scents: Saje Peppermint Halo (similar function, different palette), Le Labo Bergamote 22 (palette tier)
- The 1-second-encounter test: "If a stranger smells this for 1 second, what 3 words come to mind?"

### 4. 1 mockup

Print 1 sleeve mockup of the leading visual territory + leading name + tube hero shot. Goes on the Phase -1 landing page.

**Don't** invest in 5+ packaging variants at this stage — Phase 0 tests positioning, not packaging variants.

## Cost / time

| Item | Cost | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Freelance brand strategist + designer (sprint) | $2–4k | 5 working days |
| USPTO trademark filing (1 class) | $300–800 (with counsel) | 1–2 days |
| Domain + .com | $200 | 1 hour |
| 1 mockup print + digital | $300 | 2 days |
| Scent brief (1-page; founder-written w/ designer review) | $0–500 | 1 day |
| **Total** | **$3–5k** | **1 week** |

## Where this brief slots into Phase 0

- Week 1: this sprint
- Week 2: leading mockup goes onto Phase 0 PDP
- Week 4: Phase 0 results inform whether to refine or pivot the brand identity
- Week 6: decision gate; brand identity is ready for Phase 1 if cleared

## Do NOT do

- Hire a full agency for a 6-week brand-identity engagement at $25–50k. That's pre-PMF over-investment.
- Use stock illustrations or generic CPG templates. Buyers detect template-ness.
- Defer the brand sprint to "after Phase 0 clears" — Phase 0 cannot validate an undesigned product.
- Skip USPTO clearance "to save money." The cost of trademark conflict post-launch is 100× the clearance cost.
