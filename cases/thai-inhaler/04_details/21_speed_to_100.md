# Wave-1: Speed-to-First-100-Customers Plan + PMF Measurement

**Case**: thai-inhaler
**Lens**: Speed-to-PMF — the gating metric for everything else
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Operator profile**: Solo, bootstrap, ≤$50k Y1 marketing, greenfield
**Constraint reminder**: cosmetic-compliant language only (per E_final + decisions.md). Verb-object rule applied to every customer-facing word in this plan.

---

## 1. THE PLAY (one sentence)

**Get to 100 paying customers in ≤60 days at <$50 blended CAC across friends, IRL sampling, organic TikTok, and seeded creators — then run the Sean Ellis 40% test on those 100 to decide PMF before any paid scaling.**

Everything in this doc serves that sentence. If the founder buys $1 of paid Meta before customer #50, this plan has failed. Paid is Phase 2.

## 2. THE 90-DAY CALENDAR

### Week 1 (days 1–7) — Sourcing + landing page + first sampling visit
- **Day 1–2**: Order 200 Poy-Sian + 200 Hong Thai units wholesale (private-label MVP per E_final Phase 0 B). Cost: ~$1,200 incl. Thai customs. Lead time: 10–14 days.

- **Day 4**: Shoot 8 founder-POV iPhone clips (no edits) — "what this is / where it's from / how I use it / 1-second reset moment." Bank for staggered TikTok release.
- **Day 5–6**: First in-person scout. Walk three boutique Pilates / hot yoga studios within 2 miles of the founder's apt. Hand the studio owner a free 4-pack and a "would you let me sample to your members for a week" ask. Get 1 yes = the studio is your week-2 launch venue.
- **Day 7**: Draft the friends-and-family DM (template below). Build a list of 30 personal contacts (text/IG-DM/iMessage), ranked by ICP-fit (women 28–48, wellness-curious, urban). Don't send yet — wait until inventory lands.

### Week 2–3 (days 8–21) — First 10–25 customers via friends + IRL sampling + creator gifting
- **Day 10–14**: Inventory arrives. QC, repackage with custom sleeve. Photograph for PDP.
- **Day 12**: Send the 30 friend DMs with `FRIEND25` code. Expected conversion: 50–70% = **15–20 customers**. CAC: ~$0 (cost = founder time).
- **Day 14–21**: Park at the yes-studio post-class for 30 min, 3× per week. Hand sealed micro-samples + QR card. ~40 samples handed/week × 6% scan-to-buy = **5–7 customers in week 2–3** (per wave1_in_person.md).
- **Day 15**: Ship 25 free units to a hand-picked list: 12 nervous-system / breathwork micro-creators (5K–50K), 8 Thai-American food/lifestyle creators, 5 sensory-ritual / "what's in my bag" creators. Personal note + Calendly link for a 15-min call. **Hard exclusion: biohacker / nootropic creators** (FDA affiliate-channel risk, per E_final §6).
- **Running total at day 21**: ~25 customers. Spend: ~$2,400 (inventory + packaging + shipping).

### Week 4–6 (days 22–42) — First 50 via TikTok organic + community
- **Day 22–42**: Post founder-POV TikTok daily (one of the 8 banked clips, then more). Goal: 1 clip clears 20K views. Post mirror to Instagram Reels. **Do not run paid.**
- **Day 25–42**: Drop in 4 Reddit communities — r/aromatherapy, r/ThaiFood (heritage-context only, no sales pitch), r/migraine (sensory comfort framing, no medical claims), r/EDC (every-day-carry pocket-format angle). Per wave1_reddit_community.md: comment first, post fourth. Two well-loved comments → one tasteful "I make these" post = ~3–8 sales.
- **Day 28–35**: First creator clips go live. Most are 1K–5K views (low). 1–2 clip surprise-pops to 30K+. Repost on brand handle. Track unique discount codes per creator to attribute conversion.
- **Day 30**: Continue IRL sampling at studio #1; add studio #2 in adjacent neighborhood (capacity check — solo founder can sustain 2 studios max).
- **Day 35**: Amazon listing live (private-label SKU). Vine program ($200 for 30 reviews). PPC seed budget $50/day on exact-match "thai inhaler," "nasal inhaler," "ya dom."
- **Running total at day 42**: ~50–60 customers. Spend: ~$5,800.

### Week 7–12 (days 43–90) — Next 50 via Amazon launch + measured paid scaling
- **Day 43–60**: Amazon listing matures. Target 15 reviews ≥4.4 stars by day 60 (E_final Phase 0 gate). Daily Amazon PPC review; kill ACoS >40% keywords. Goal: 25 Amazon orders by day 60.
- **Day 50–60**: Sean Ellis survey fires (see §4). Read first 30 responses by day 60.
- **Day 60–75**: If one creator clip popped (≥50K views, save-rate ≥3%, "where to buy" comments, per wave1_paid_ads.md gate), spin up paid whitelist on Meta Partnership Ads + TikTok Spark behind that exact post. $50/day cap. If no clip popped, hold paid; double creator-seeding budget to $1,500.
- **Day 75–90**: First retail door scout — 1 Erewhon-tier specialty store, founder-led pitch with the Amazon review screenshot + 60-day customer list as proof. (Slot purchase deferred to month 4 per E_final.)
- **Running total at day 90**: ~100–120 customers. Spend: ~$15,000–$18,000.

## 4. THE PMF MEASUREMENT

### Sean Ellis 40% test (the primary instrument)
- **Trigger**: customer has used the product for ≥14 days AND completed ≥1 reorder OR finished the 2-pack. Per Sean Ellis's audience rule (experienced core product, used twice, recently).
- **Survey** (Klaviyo, day-21 post-purchase auto-fire):
  1. **"How would you feel if you could no longer buy [Brand]?" — Very disappointed / Somewhat disappointed / Not disappointed / N/A**
  2. "What type of person do you think would most benefit from [Brand]?"
  3. "What is the main benefit you get from [Brand]?" (cosmetic-compliant phrasing in the question is fine; customer answers may include drug-language and that's their mouth, not yours — do not republish in marketing)
  4. "How can we improve [Brand] for you?"
- **Target N**: ≥40 responses from the first 100 customers (40% response rate is realistic with $5 reorder credit).
- **Decision threshold**: ≥40% "very disappointed" = green light to scale paid. 30–40% = iterate positioning/format. <30% = product-or-positioning broken; pivot.

### Repeat-purchase rate (the behavior instrument)
- **Day-30 repeat**: target ≥15% (per Bsandco DTC benchmark of 18.8% across 156K customers; 50.3% of repeaters return within 30 days).
- **Day-60 repeat**: target ≥25% (CPG consumables benchmark is 20–25% as "strong"; below 20% = retention problem).
- **Day-90 repeat**: target ≥35%. If <25% at day 90, the inhaler is a buy-and-forget novelty, not a habit product.

### NPS (the reference instrument, not the deciding one)
- Fire post-day-30. Target: NPS ≥40. Below 20 = product fundamentally not delighting.

### Qualitative (the truth instrument)
- **10 customer interviews by day 60.** 20 minutes each, $25 Amazon gift card. Recruited from "very disappointed" responders + 3 "not disappointed" for contrast. Founder runs them personally — no agency, no script-reading. Notes only, no recordings.
- One question matters: "Walk me through the last time you used it." The actual JTBD-trigger context. If 7+ describe the same trigger ("Slack-cortisol afternoon," "post-class car," "mid-meeting bathroom break"), the wedge is real. If everyone describes a different context, the brand has no ICP.

### Decision rules (read at day 60, then again at day 90)
| Signal | Action |
|---|---|
| Ellis ≥40% AND day-30 repeat ≥15% AND 7/10 interviews share JTBD | **Double down**: turn on paid whitelist, pursue retail door |
| Ellis 30–40% AND day-30 repeat 10–15% | **Iterate positioning**: narrow ICP, refresh creative, hold paid |
| Ellis <30% OR day-30 repeat <8% | **Pivot product**: bundle-format toolkit (D_diff_map Adjacent B) or non-heritage frame |

## 5. KILL / ITERATE CRITERIA

- **Day 60 customers <50**: pivot positioning — likely "Thai herbal inhaler" frame is failing the cold consumer. Test "1-second focus reset stick" frame for 30 days; re-measure.
- **PMF <30% at day 60**: pivot product. Either bundle-format toolkit (per E_final Phase 0 fallback) or kill the heritage frame. Sunk cost on inventory: <$3k. Acceptable.
- **PMF 30–40%**: iterate. Narrow ICP. Add ritual-card / bundle. Re-survey at day 90.
- **PMF >40% AND day-60 repeat ≥18%**: double down. Move $8k of remaining marketing budget to paid whitelist + first Erewhon door.
- **FDA warning letter to a comparable brand within 90 days**: tighten claim language proactively (per E_final kill-switch). Audit all creator content for the verb-object rule.
- **Founder cannot secure Thai supplier-equity by day 60**: shift positioning to non-heritage "modern American botanical" framing per decisions.md fallback.
