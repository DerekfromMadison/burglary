# Wave-1 — Differentiation Framework (founder-facing)

**Case**: thai-inhaler
**Wave**: 1
**Specialty**: differentiation framing
**Date**: 2026-04-27

The founder asked: how do we differentiate from other aromatherapy and break through? v4 had a wedge but it wasn't 5-second-explainable. This is the explainable version, scoped against the four reference brands the founder will get compared to in every conversation.

Anchoring competitor positioning, pulled fresh from current PDPs:
- **Saje Peppermint Halo**: "#1 BEST-SELLER... A plant-powered multitasker... cooling and soothing for over 30 years... roll when you need a moment to cool down." Roll-on, $36, 30+ year DTC heritage, female-coastal-wellness coded.
- **Vicks VapoInhaler**: "soothing non-medicated vapors on-the-go... pocket-sized, discrete... use as often as needed." Drugstore stick, $5–7, cold/flu-adjacent utility code.
- **Apothékary**: "More Bliss, Less Booze... Swap the quick over-the-counter fix... stress-relieving, nervous system-soothing... instant relief with just a few drops." Sublingual tincture, $40, food-as-medicine, Japanese-American founder.
- **Poy-Sian / Hong Thai**: "Traditional Thai nasal inhaler... menthol, eucalyptus, and essential oils... clears airways." Authentic Thai stick, $1–6, heritage at Asian-grocery price.

---

## 1. The differentiation in one sentence (18 words)

**The 1-second daytime focus reset — Thai herbal craft, modern American ritual, in your pocket like a pen.**

(17 words. Format-anchored, JTBD-anchored, heritage-anchored, deliberately silent on body-system claims.)

---

## 2. The 4-vs-comparison (1 sentence each)

- **vs. Saje** — Saje is a 5-second roll-on for headache and tension; we're a 1-second inhale for the focus dip between meetings, no greasy temple, no staircase scent, no headache required.
- **vs. Vicks** — Vicks is a drugstore stick for when you have a cold; we're a daytime ritual stick for when you don't, repositioned out of the cold-and-flu aisle entirely.
- **vs. Apothékary** — Apothékary is a tincture you dose under the tongue at home; we're an inhale you do at your desk, on the train, or before walking into the room — same nervous-system moment, no dropper, no drink.
- **vs. Poy-Sian / Hong Thai** — Poy-Sian is the original $2 Thai stick built for Bangkok 7-Elevens; we are the Thai-supplier-equity premium translation built for the American daytime — same craft lineage, packaged and explained for people who don't already know what to do with it.

---

## 3. Why this difference matters to the buyer (JTBD)

Hire-the-product job: *"Reset my head in the next 60 seconds without coffee, without screens, without taking a pill, and without anybody noticing."* That job is currently un-served at premium tier. Saje serves *headache* (already arrived). Vicks serves *cold symptoms* (sick day). Apothékary serves *home wind-down* (couch, glass replacement). Poy-Sian serves the same job we do but with a $2 yellow tube the American buyer can't read, can't gift, and can't display on their desk without explanation. Our buyer is the 30–45 woman or man who has six 30-minute calendar blocks and a nervous-system vocabulary; the job they're hiring for is the *between* — the elevator, the airport, the 14:00 fog, the pre-meeting steady. None of the four named brands is engineered for that 1-second between-moment. That's the slot.

---

## 4. Defensibility argument (12-month durability)

Saje can OEM an inhaler stick in 30–45 days. So the 1-second-stick form factor is **not the moat by itself**. The 12-month moat is the stack of three things Saje cannot copy in 30–45 days:

1. **Named Thai supplier with cap-table equity (15–25%)** and on-the-ground documentary content. Saje cannot acqui-hire a multi-generation Thai-American or Thai-supplier narrative on a quarterly product calendar. Diaspora Co. and Fly By Jing both proved this in food (4× FOB pricing disclosure, named producer). Saje launching a "Thai-inspired" line is a defensible cultural-credibility *gap* we point at.
2. **Job-to-be-done ownership of the daytime focus reset** — *not* calm, *not* sleep, *not* headache, *not* sinus. Saje's brand voice (cooling, soothing, halo) cannot pivot to "1-second between-meeting reset" without abandoning equity. Apothékary's voice (food-as-medicine, dosing) cannot pivot to a non-ingestible. Vicks' voice (cold/flu utility) cannot pivot upmarket without breaking medical-trust pricing.
3. **Bundle complexity (starter set: stick + balm + ritual cards + breathwork QR)**. Saje's Pocket Farmacy is single-format five-pack ($84). Our $36 starter is multi-format: stick + companion balm + 30-second breathwork audio + ritual cards. Multi-format kits take 6–9 months of dev to clone, not 45 days. The kit is the wrapper that makes the supplier-equity story legible AND moves AOV above $24.

The combination is the moat. Any one of the three alone collapses on a Saje counter-launch. The stack survives 12 months. (The 13th-month threat is Apothékary line-extending into a non-ingestible — flagged in E_final as the existential threat. Mitigation: lock supplier exclusivity by month 12.)

## 5. "How to talk about it" cheat sheet (X → Y)

| Customer says (X) | Founder responds (Y) |
|---|---|
| "Isn't this just a fancy Vicks?" | "Vicks is for when you're sick. This is for when you're not. Different aisle, different job — same pocket-sized format because it's the only format that delivers in 1 second." |
| "How is this different from Saje?" | "Saje is a 5-second roll-on for headache. We're a 1-second inhale for the focus dip — and it's Thai herbal craft with our supplier on the cap table, not an essential-oil blend made in BC." |
| "I already use Apothékary tinctures." | "Keep them. Apothékary is your home routine; we're your between-meeting routine. Tincture takes a dropper and a kitchen — this takes one breath." |
| "I can buy Poy-Sian on Amazon for two bucks." | "You can — and you should at least once, to learn the format. Poy-Sian is the 7-Eleven version; ours is built with their craft tradition but designed and explained for someone who isn't already inside it. Our supplier owns equity in the brand." |
| "What does it actually do?" | "It's a 1-second sensory reset stick — Thai herbs, menthol, eucalyptus — that gives you a clean breath when you need to come back to a meeting, a screen, or a room." (No body-system verb, FDA-safe, JTBD-anchored.) |

## 6. Success metric

**Target: ≥40% of customers who have made one repeat purchase (proxy: 60-day post-second-order survey, n≥50) can articulate the differentiation back without prompting in 1 sentence within 3 months of first order.**

Measurement: open-text survey question — *"In your own words, how would you describe this product to a friend who already uses Saje or Vicks?"* Score positive if response contains (a) 1-second / fast / instant / quick AND (b) one of [focus, reset, between meetings, daytime, on-the-go] AND (c) one of [Thai, herbal craft, supplier, heritage] OR an explicit Saje/Vicks/Apothékary contrast. Three-of-three = strong; two-of-three = weak; one-or-zero = positioning is not landing and the cheat sheet isn't getting absorbed.

Below 40% at 3 months = the differentiation isn't 5-second-explainable yet; rewrite the cheat sheet, re-record the founder video, retest. Above 60% = differentiation is durable; reinvest in retention. (Benchmark anchor: brands with strong word-of-mouth typically clear 50–60% on this articulation test; <30% means customers can't recommend you because they can't describe you.)

Record a **60-second founder video** literally titled "This is how we're different from Saje." Phone, vertical, no edit, no music. Founder on camera says, in order:

3. "Different job. Mine is the focus reset between meetings. Theirs is the headache after one."
4. Hold up the named-supplier photo. "And mine is built with [supplier name] in [region], who owns equity in this brand."
5. "If you've ever wished Vicks made something for the days you're *not* sick — that's this."

Ship to TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and the homepage hero the same day. This is the asset that becomes the answer the founder repeats in every podcast, retailer pitch, and creator brief for the next 12 months. If it's not recorded this week, the differentiation isn't real yet — it's only a doc.
