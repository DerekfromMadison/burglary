**Why v2:** v1 nailed the single-SKU launch architecture but left the unboxing experience entirely undefined — and the unboxing is the load-bearing intervention the journey-gap report identified as the largest single hole in v5. v2 codifies a ~$2/unit unboxing kit whose 3pm laptop-bezel sticker is the engineered habit cue the rest of the plan now leans on.

## 1. The recommendation (unchanged from v1)

**Launch ONE SKU at $12 retail: a single tube nasal inhaler, OEM blank-label from a Thai factory, wrapped in a custom printed paper sleeve.** No 2-pack, no starter set, no subscription, no scent variants, no custom formulation, no custom mold for the first 90 days.

What v2 ADDS: every unit ships inside a designed unboxing kit ~$2/unit landed (sleeve $0.45, hand-numbered card $0.20, ritual card $0.30, **3pm sticker $0.15**, "send to a friend" envelope + second tube $0.70–1.30). The total unit landed cost rises from $1.85 (formula + tube + paper-sleeve label) to **~$3.85 with kit** — the kit is the marketing dollar with the highest signal-per-dollar in the entire 90-day plan (per `gap_journey §3`).

## 2. Why the unboxing kit changes the math

The v1 spec sold a tube in a printed paper sleeve and trusted the customer to invent her own habit. She won't. B.J. Fogg's habit loop is *cue → action → reward* — v1 had the action (inhale) and an implied reward (the bright minute) and **no engineered cue**. The 3pm sticker is the cue, manufactured into the unboxing and customer-installed onto her laptop bezel. Every glance at her keyboard becomes a brand-coded prompt to inhale; zero ongoing brand cost; infinite duration; cannot be copied by Saje retroactively (Saje cannot install stickers on 1,000 laptops a competitor already has).

The other four kit elements each fix a different gap:

- **Wax-sealed kraft sleeve** — survives the 1.5-second TikTok scroll-fragment test in a way "paper sleeve" does not. Kraft signals craft, wax signals ritual. Color is a saturated terracotta or jade (founder picks, locks).
- **Hand-numbered card** ("you are #847 of the first 1,000") + 1-sentence founder note in a real handwritten typeface — earns the founding-100 collectible status (Olipop, Liquid Death, Recess all manufactured first-100 artifacts; v1 had nothing).
- **Illustrated ritual card** — three steps, named: (a) twist, (b) inhale once through each nostril, (c) name the next minute out loud. The phrase "the next minute brighter" lands HERE, not in an Instagram caption.
- **"Send to a friend" envelope with a second tube + postage-paid return-address sticker** — the gift mechanic, built into the product instead of bolted onto a referral page she'll never visit. Persona C explicitly buys two-and-gifts; v1 had zero product support for this; v2 makes it default.

## 3. The play (sourcing path unchanged from v1; kit added)

1. **OEM blank-label, stock formula** — unchanged. 1,500-unit MOQ. $0.95–$1.15 unit FOB at quantity. Camphor-free option specified in brief.
2. **Paper sleeve over OEM tube** — unchanged. Sticker Mule 50–1,000 sleeve runs.
3. **Unboxing kit components (NEW)**:
   - **Wax-sealed kraft outer sleeve**: kraft cardstock, hand-applied wax seal, $0.45/unit at 1,500 quantity (Sticker Mule custom packaging or local print shop).
   - **Hand-numbered card**: 4×6 cardstock, founder-numbered Sharpie, ~$0.20/unit (founder labor + cardstock).
   - **Illustrated ritual card**: 4×6, full-color print, $0.30/unit.
   - **3pm laptop-bezel sticker**: 0.5"×1", same color as the tube/wordmark, $0.15/unit at 5k quantity.
   - **"Send to a friend" envelope + second tube**: kraft envelope $0.10 + second tube COGS $1.85 + postage-paid return-address sticker $0.10 — kit cost rises here. v6 deliverable accepts the second-tube cost because it doubles as the natural referral coefficient on a sensory product.
4. **Target landed cost per unboxing**: ~$2.00 without second tube (sleeve + numbered card + ritual card + 3pm sticker), ~$3.85 with second tube. Default ships with second tube; founder can A/B half the cohort without to measure referral lift.

## 4. Target gross margin (revised)

At $12 MSRP DTC Shopify with FULL unboxing kit (with second tube):
- $12 − $0.50 payment processing − $1.85 product COGS − $2.00 unboxing kit base − $1.85 second tube − $0.80 shipping subsidy = **$5.00 contribution, ~42% gross margin.**

At $12 MSRP DTC Shopify with kit but NO second tube (control half of cohort):
- $12 − $0.50 − $1.85 − $2.00 − $0.80 = **$6.85 contribution, ~57% gross margin.**

Amazon FBA single SKU at $12: $12 − $0.96 referral − $3.22 FBA − $1.85 COGS − $2.00 unboxing (Amazon allows it as long as it ships inside Amazon's outer carton) = **$3.97 contribution, ~33% gross margin.**

The margin compression is real; the offsetting math is that the unboxing converts repeat-purchase rate from ~15% (v1, time-based SMS, no cue) to a target ~25–30% (v6, depletion-anchored SMS + 3pm sticker cue + gift mechanic). LTV math wins as long as repeat clears 22% — which is what `gap_journey §5` argues the unboxing-plus-depletion-SMS combination delivers.

## 5. MOQ + reorder cycle (with kit)

Initial buy: **1,500 units** at ~$1.85 landed = **~$2,775 inventory cash.**
Sleeves: **1,500 paper sleeves** at ~$400.
Unboxing kit components: 1,500 wax-sealed kraft sleeves + 1,500 numbered cards + 1,500 ritual cards + 1,500 stickers + 1,500 envelopes + 1,500 second tubes = **~$2,800 total kit production capital** (kit COGS amortizes against units shipped, so realistic 90-day spend is 100 units × ~$2 = ~$200 with founder absorbing the second-tube COGS only on units that ship).

Total Wave-1 production capital: ~$3,200 product + ~$200 kit-on-shipped-units = **~$3,400** for the 90-day cohort. Headroom to scale kit production at reorder.
