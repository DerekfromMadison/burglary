## 1. The recommendation

**Launch ONE SKU: a single tube nasal inhaler, OEM blank-label from a Thai factory, wrapped in a custom printed paper sleeve, sold at $12 retail.** No 2-pack. No starter set. No subscription. No scent variants. No custom formulation. No custom mold. One product, one price, one job ‚Äî for the first 90 days of selling.

## 2. Why this is the bet

The v4 framing ‚Äî $14 single / $24 2-pack / $36 starter set ‚Äî is correct CFO arithmetic and wrong launch architecture for a pre-PMF greenfield indie. A pre-PMF brand with no audience, no creator engine, and no shelf placement does not have a "blended AOV problem." It has a **does-anyone-buy-this-thing-once problem.** Three SKUs at launch triples your photography work, triples your listing copy, triples your inventory commitment, splits your conversion data three ways, and trains the founder to optimize a mix instead of optimizing a wedge. The bundle math from the CFO doc only matters once you have a baseline conversion rate at one price point ‚Äî without that baseline you are tuning fictional knobs. Single-SKU launches also force narrative discipline: the founder must answer "what is this for, in one sentence" before they ship a unit, which is the same discipline that makes a TikTok work. The earlier v3 spec ($18, custom "Bangkok 2pm" scent stack, custom kraft sleeve + serialized story-card, 5,000-unit MOQ, ~$25‚Äì28k of the budget burned on launch infrastructure) is also wrong for marketing-first speed: a custom-formulated scent stack invites OEM iteration cycles, COA delays, stability questions, and a 5k unit cash commitment before the founder has a single buyer. Marketing-first means: cheapest viable physical product, biggest possible budget for ads, creators, and listing optimization. The $12 price is deliberately below the $14 v4 single because (a) it sits one dollar above Olbas/Vicks ($8‚Äì11), which is the cheapest credible premium signal, (b) it leaves $5 of psychological room before Poy-Sian/Hong Thai at $4‚Äì5 reads like a bargain, and (c) at $12 with a $1.85 landed COGS the unit still ships ~$6 contribution after Amazon Beauty referral (8% over $10) and FBA fulfillment ‚Äî enough to absorb a $30‚Äì35 organic-blended CAC inside a couple of repeat orders.

## 3. The play

1. **Sourcing path ‚Äî OEM blank-label, stock formula.** Alibaba Thailand suppliers list menthol/herbal nasal inhaler tubes at **$0.55‚Äì$1.40/unit FOB at MOQ 100‚Äì500**, with multiple verified Thai OEM suppliers carrying CE certification and 3-year shelf-life specs (Alibaba "thai-nasal-inhaler" and "thailand-nasal-inhaler-suppliers" showrooms). Order the supplier's stock formula in their stock white or clear tube body. Do not modify the formula. Do not commission custom tooling on the cap. Do not pay for in-tube branding. Founder takes the standard menthol/borneol/eucalyptus/peppermint-camphor stack the factory already runs ‚Äî except specify "no camphor" in the brief if available, since multiple suppliers offer camphor-free SKUs (the camphor drop is a 2-week footnote, not a strategic moat). Custom formulation triggers stability testing, COA cycles, and 8‚Äì12 weeks of delay you cannot afford. The formula is not the differentiator at Wave-1; the sleeve narrative + the founder's content + the Amazon listing copy are.

2. **Packaging philosophy ‚Äî paper sleeve over OEM tube, not custom tube, not custom carton.** Custom-tooled tubes are a 6-month, $15k+ capital trap (mold tooling alone runs $3‚Äì8k, plus 5k-unit MOQ on the new mold). The minimum-viable package is: OEM blank tube + a printed paper band/sleeve. **Sticker Mule paper labels: 50-unit MOQ, $29 promo / $64 standard for 50 labels, 4-day turnaround, free shipping; rotating "50 for $29" deal is reliably available.** A 1.5"√ó4" sleeve at quantity 1,000 lands at roughly $0.25‚Äì0.40/sleeve through Sticker Mule or Inkable; $0.12‚Äì0.18 at 5k via UPrinting. Apply by hand the first run on a kitchen table; this is the single most leveraged hour of founder labor in the launch. Sleeve carries: brand mark, the daytime/desk one-line use claim (compliant verb-object ‚Äî scent acts on the moment, not the body, per the FDA verb-object rule from E_final), the named Thai supplier acknowledgment, ingredient list, and a QR to the PDP. No carton. No insert. No tissue. No magnet closure. The container of the brand is the sleeve typography and the QR-resolved story page, not the unboxing experience.

3. **Target landed COGS ‚Äî $1.85, conservative ceiling $2.20.** CFO model anchored $1.60 on a 10k-unit OEM run; at realistic Wave-1 MOQ (1,000‚Äì2,000 units), unit FOB rises to $0.95‚Äì$1.15 (small-batch markup), plus $0.10 freight/clearance, $0.18 tariff (19% reciprocal Thai), $0.30 paper sleeve + hand-application labor amortized, $0.08 inbound/QC, $0.30 FBA prep poly+carton, $0.05 shrink. **Target landed COGS = $1.85.**

4. **Target gross margin.** At $12 MSRP on Amazon Beauty (8% referral on items >$10): $12 ‚àí $0.96 referral ‚àí $3.22 FBA fulfillment ‚àí $1.85 COGS = **$5.97 contribution, ~50% gross margin.** On DTC Shopify at $12 + shipping pass-through: $12 ‚àí $0.50 payment processing ‚àí $1.85 COGS ‚àí $0.80 shipping subsidy = **$8.85 contribution, ~74% gross margin.** Both clear the 50% retail-survival floor flagged in A_domain ¬ß2.3 and leave headroom for $30‚Äì40 blended CAC on a 1.5‚Äì2√ó purchase customer.

5. **MOQ + reorder cycle.** Initial buy: **1,500 units** at ~$1.85 landed = **~$2,775 inventory cash.** Order 1,500 paper sleeves Sticker Mule at ~$400. Total Wave-1 production capital = **~$3,200** ‚Äî a fraction of the $25k Y1 inventory budget, leaving $20k+ headroom for the first reorder at 3√ó the volume (which is when unit FOB drops meaningfully). Reorder trigger: when on-hand drops to 400 units (~4 weeks of cover at target sell-through). Reorder quantity: 3,000 units once Wave-1 sells through. Lead time Bangkok‚ÜíUS 3PL = 4‚Äì6 weeks ocean, 10 days air-freight emergency. ## 4. Success metric

**Sell-through threshold to add SKU 2 (the 2-pack): 50 units/week sustained for 3 consecutive weeks, organic + paid blended, at ‚â§$35 blended CAC.** That is roughly 200 units/month, ~600 units in 90 days ‚Äî burning through 40% of the 1,500-unit initial buy. Below that velocity, the answer is not "add a bundle to fix AOV"; the answer is "fix the listing, fix the creative, or fix the position." Above that velocity, the 2-pack ships in week 13 as SKU 2; the starter set in month 6 as SKU 3 only if 90-day repeat clears 22% (per CFO model gating). Secondary gates that override raw velocity: Amazon listing conversion ‚â•8% (cold‚Üícart), 30-day return rate ‚â§8%, and at least one organic creator post that drives ‚â•50 attributable orders. If velocity hits but conversion is <4% (i.e., paid traffic but no buyers), the SKU is wrong and bundling won't save it.
