# Phase 0 v2 — thai-inhaler

**TL;DR**: v3 spent $5–15k testing a SKU. v2 spends $1k testing the wedge BEFORE any SKU exists, then $5–10k testing on Shopify-only with cue-anchored creative and above-median thresholds.

## What changed

- v1 tested SKU existence; v2 tests wedge positioning (Phase -1 landing-page) BEFORE inventory
- v1 included Amazon launch; v2 is Shopify-only — Amazon Brand Registry is a multi-week wall
- v1 tested benefit-anchored creative ("morning glimmer"); v2 A/Bs against cue-anchored ("before your 2pm meeting") — habit needs a cue, not just a reward
- v1 thresholds were median (1.5% conversion = indie-wellness median); v2 thresholds are above-median per the wedge thesis
- v1 expected supplier signed by Day 60; v2 decouples supplier timeline (9–12 months) from Phase 0 — uses stocked OEM inventory for the Phase 0 PDP

## The protocol

### Week 0 — Phase -1 Landing-page test ($1k)
- 1-page Carrd or Shopify PDP at $24 anchor with mockup hero shot
- $750 paid Meta + TikTok traffic
- No inventory exists
- Measure: email capture rate; qualitative price-tolerance signal ("would consider" vs. "too expensive")
- **Gates**: ≥8% email capture AND ≥30% price-tolerance signal at $24
- Below either → kill wedge OR pivot to Adjacent Wedge B (bundle toolkit, see `04_details/01_strategic_wedge.md`)

### Week 1 — Brand sprint + OTC counsel ($3–5k)
- Visual territory commit (recommend luxury-modernist; see `10_brand_identity_brief.md`)
- Name shortlist of 5; USPTO + .com clearance check
- 9-note scent brief (top/heart/base) for OEM perfumer
- 1 sleeve mockup
- 2-hour cosmetic/OTC FDA counsel intake (~$500–800): confirm camphor-drop reformulation qualifies as cosmetic
- **USPTO 1(b) filed; Amazon Brand Registry + Transparency enrolled** before any public listing

### Weeks 2–4 — Phase 0 PDP test (Shopify, $5k)
- 2 PDP variants: cue-anchored ("before your 2pm meeting"), benefit-anchored ("morning glimmer")
- $4k paid Meta + TikTok
- $1k creator gifting (5 nervous-system + 5 Thai-American micro-creators) — NOT 50 broad seeds
- **Measure**: conversion rate, AOV, CAC, 30-day return rate, sub opt-in rate, creator pickup, compliance-flag count, cue vs. benefit creative lift

### Weeks 2–6 — OEM sourcing (parallel, ~$1k)
- 5–10 Thai OEM blank-label intros via BOI / Alibaba / Medicos-tier directories
- NOT Poy-Sian / Hong Thai / Siang Pure principal brands
- Target: $1.50–2.50/unit landed; 500-unit MOQ; 60-day production lead

## Decision gate (end Week 6)

**Strong go** (proceed to Phase 1):
- Conversion ≥2.5%, AOV ≥$22, CAC ≤$32
- 30-day return <5%, sub opt-in ≥8%
- Compliance flags: 0
- Cue-anchored creative outperforms benefit-anchored by ≥30% conversion

**Conditional go** (Phase 1 with capped budget OR pivot to Adjacent B):
- Conversion 1.5–2.5%, CAC $32–$45
- Mixed signals on creative variant or retention proxies

**Kill / Adjacent B**:
- Conversion <1% at $24
- CAC >$45
- Compliance flag triggered (e.g. an affiliate creator drug-claim language)
- OTC counsel says cosmetic-path reformulation not viable

## Statistical-power caveat

$5–10k Phase 0 spend is **directional**, not statistically powered. ~1500–2500 cold clicks. If founder wants statistical confidence on the conversion threshold (i.e. 95% CI), Phase 0 budget = $10–15k.

## What Phase 0 does NOT validate

- 90-day repurchase rate (only resolvable post-launch)
- Long-term polyvagal vocabulary stickiness (3–5 year question)
- Whether the brand has 1 retail door's worth of LTV in it (Phase 1 question)

These remain open even on a strong-go and are explicit in `06_risks_and_kill_switches.md`.
