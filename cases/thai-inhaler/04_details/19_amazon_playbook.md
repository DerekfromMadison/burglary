## 2. The listing structure

### Title formula (200-char Beauty cap)

`[Brand] Thai Herbal Nasal Inhaler — Menthol + Camphor + Borneol Aromatherapy Stick for Focus, Travel, On-the-Go | Authentic Thai Ya Dom Formula | Single Stick / 2-Pack / 3-Pack Starter Set`

Front-load: brand → category descriptor (`Thai Herbal Nasal Inhaler`) → ingredient triad (the trigeminal trio that defines the format) → use-occasion (`Focus, Travel, On-the-Go`) → cultural anchor (`Authentic Thai Ya Dom Formula`) → variation. **No claims.** No "clears sinuses," no "relieves stress," no "calms anxiety" — those are FDA drug-claim triggers per E_final / D_fda_language. "Aromatherapy" is the safe-harbor cosmetic verb in Beauty.

### Five bullets (Beauty: 1000 char each, but mobile shows ~250)

1. **THE BANGKOK DESK RITUAL** — Thai office workers, students, and Grab drivers reach for ya dom the way Americans reach for coffee. Single-handed, one-second open, an instant menthol-camphor-borneol lift you can take at your desk, in traffic, before the meeting.
2. **MADE WITH [NAMED THAI SUPPLIER]** — Formulated with [supplier name + region], [generation count] generations into the ya dom craft. Refillable [vessel material] tube; not a 99¢ plastic stick.
3. **THREE NOTES, ONE BREATH** — Menthol (cooling), camphor (sharp), borneol (woody, the Thai signature). Nothing else. No synthetic fragrance, no propellants, no oils on the skin.
4. **DESIGNED FOR DAYTIME, NOT BEDTIME** — Pocket-sized for the 2 PM slump, the airport line, the kids-to-school crunch. Each tube lasts 60–90 days of daily use.
5. **A SCENT, NOT A DRUG** — This is a cosmetic aromatherapy product. Inhale the scent. We make no medical claims. Pair the 2-pack with the [refill] for built-in routine.

Bullet 5 doubles as compliance shield + bundle CTA.

### A+ Content (7 modules — Premium A+ if Brand Registry tier qualifies)

1. **Hero banner**: founder + named Thai supplier on-the-ground photo. Caption: "From [village/region] to your desk."
2. **The 1-second ritual**: 4-frame comic — open / inhale / cap / continue. Counter-positions the 5–10s roll-on category.
3. **Ingredient deck**: menthol/camphor/borneol with sourcing region for each.
4. **Use-occasion grid**: 6 cells — desk, commute, flight, post-workout, study, post-meeting. Photographic, no claim text.
5. **Cross-sell strip**: 2-pack, 3-pack starter, refill subscription.
6. **Comparison chart** (the price-justification module — see §5): the brand vs. Poy-Sian vs. Vicks Vapor vs. Olbas. Columns: refillable, named supplier, ingredient transparency, scent profile, cosmetic positioning.
7. **Founder + supplier story**: 90-sec video, captions on. Cap-table credit to Thai supplier-co-founder if applicable (per E_final founder-bifurcation table).

### Infographics on the main image carousel (7 slots)

1. Hero packshot, white background, full Amazon-spec.
2. Single-handed pocket pull (scale shot vs. iPhone).
3. Annotated tube cutaway (refillable, brass/glass material — not the $3 plastic Poy-Sian shell).
4. Use-occasion lifestyle (desk).
5. Use-occasion lifestyle (commute/airport).
6. Comparison-vs-Poy-Sian visual (without naming Poy-Sian — show the upgrade).
7. 6-second video: the ritual + a tagline ("the inhaler Bangkok keeps in its pocket").

## 3. The keyword strategy

Run Cerebro reverse-ASIN against B01FLJN7I6 (Poy-Sian 6-pack), B00MKVYCX8 (Siang Pure 6-pack), B00014DMG4 (Olbas), and the Tiger Balm Inhaler ASIN. Pull every keyword with ≥100 search volume and ≤500 competing products. The category is small enough that this fits in **2–3 free Cerebro searches** on the Helium 10 free plan (2 daily searches, 20 keywords per search) — confirm with the [Helium 10 7-day Diamond trial](https://www.helium10.com/pricing/) before committing to a paid month.

### Primary (rank target months 3–6)

- **Thai herbal inhaler** — anchor brand-defining term; mid volume, low-medium competition (Hong Thai + Poy-Sian dominate but no brand owns the descriptor).
- **Nasal inhaler aromatherapy** — bigger volume; Olbas + Vicks own this; competitive but on-brand.
- **Menthol inhaler stick** — clean, descriptive, lower competition than "nasal inhaler."

### Secondary (rank target months 1–3 — easier wins)

- **Ya dom** — low search volume, low competition. **Claim this term.** It's the Thai-language tag with near-zero American advertiser interest. Own it cheaply, ride the rising-tide search growth as the Lisa Manoban / SCMP / Strategist coverage spreads.
- **Thai inhaler stick**
- **Aromatherapy inhaler for focus** (compliant — "for focus" describes the product context, not a body claim)
- **Pocket aromatherapy inhaler**
- **Camphor menthol inhaler**

### Long-tail (PPC anchors + content match)

- **Nasal inhaler stress relief** — compliant variant; "nasal inhaler" describes the format, "stress relief" is in the search query, not in our copy.
- **Menthol focus inhaler**
- **Thai aromatherapy gift**
- **Yadom inhaler USA** / **yadom Thai**
- **Travel inhaler menthol**
- **Aromatherapy stick on the go**
- **Bangkok inhaler** / **Thai herbal stick**
- **Refillable nasal inhaler** (the moat keyword — Poy-Sian is plastic-disposable)

### Compliant-variant rules

Bid the long-tail and let Amazon match it; do **not** put "stress relief" or "anxiety" or "headache" in your title, bullets, or backend search terms. Backend (250 bytes): Thai-language synonyms (ยาดม), regional spellings, "ya-dom," "yadom," "ya dom inhaler," "thai pocket inhaler" — exhaust the bytes; don't repeat front-end terms.

## 4. The review velocity strategy

### Vine (the workhorse)

Enroll **30 units at $200** the moment the listing is live and Brand Registry is active. Per [BellaVix 2026](https://www.bellavix.com/amazon-vine-program-costs-in-2026-what-sellers-and-vendors-need-to-know/), tiers are 1–2 free / 3–10 at $75 / 11–30 at $200, billed 7 days after first review publishes; if no review in 90 days, no charge. 30 units claimed → 18–22 reviews on average across days 8–45. **Do not enroll a single tube.** Enroll the **2-pack ASIN** so Vine lands ratings on the bundle SKU you actually want to rank.

Cost: **$200 enrollment + ~30 × $1.60 COGS + FBA = ~$320 all-in for 18–22 verified reviews in 6 weeks.** Cheapest legal review velocity on the platform.

### Early Reviewer Program — dead

[Discontinued April 25, 2021.](https://www.edesk.com/blog/amazon-early-reviewer-program/) Don't budget for it. Don't let an agency upsell you on a "post-ERP equivalent" — most are review-manipulation services that violate ToS and trigger suspension.

### "Request a Review" button

Free, native, one-click per order in Seller Central. **Use it on every order, day 5–7 post-delivery.** Tools like [Helium 10 Follow-Up](https://www.helium10.com/) automate this within ToS. Conversion typically 3–8% of orders → review. On 200 first-90-day units, that's 8–16 reviews on top of Vine.

### Brand Tailored Promotions / Brand Insiders

For Brand Registry-enrolled brands, Amazon's **Brand Tailored Promotions** lets you target high-engagement / repeat / cart-abandoners with custom discount codes. Use for **gifted-via-Insiders** seeding to repeat buyers — request reviews via "Request a Review" only (no separate solicitation; that violates ToS).

### Repeat-purchase incentive

Subscribe & Save on the 2-pack at 5% (Amazon's minimum) → repeat buyers + organic review velocity. This is the LTV lever; reviews are the byproduct.

### Realistic 60-day numbers

| Week | Source | Cumulative reviews |
|---|---|---|
| 0–3 | Vine claims, no reviews yet | 0 |
| 3–6 | Vine begins posting + first organic | 8–14 |
| 6–9 | Vine peak + Request-a-Review on 100+ orders | 22–35 |
| 60-day total | Vine 18–22 + organic 8–15 + Insiders 5–10 | **35–55** |

**50 reviews in 60 days is aggressive. 100 is excellent and rare.** Anyone promising 100+ in 60 days is selling review-manipulation.

## 5. The price-vs-Poy-Sian gap (the 5-second fight)

The brand is at $14 single / $24 2-pack / $36 starter. Poy-Sian is **$3–5/stick** on Amazon today. The PDP must justify the gap **in the first 5 seconds of attention** — meaning in the main image and the first bullet, not on the A+ scroll.

The five-second hierarchy:

1. **Main image**: refillable vessel cutaway (visible difference vs. Poy-Sian's plastic-disposable shell). One frame, one truth: *this is not the same object.*
2. **Title**: "Thai Herbal Nasal Inhaler" + "Authentic Ya Dom" + "Refillable" — anchors heritage, format, and the moat keyword.
3. **First bullet**: "the Bangkok desk ritual" — names the cultural use-case Poy-Sian's listing does not articulate.
4. **First A+ module**: founder + named Thai supplier photo. Provenance Poy-Sian cannot match (Poy-Sian is the supplier; this brand is the curated upgrade with named-region sourcing).
5. **Comparison module**: refillable / named supplier / ingredient transparency / cosmetic-compliant claim language — Poy-Sian's listing has none of these explicit.

If the founder cannot deliver any of (1)–(4), the price gap collapses and the listing dies. **Brand Registry is the gate that unlocks A+ Content** — without it, modules 4 and 5 don't exist and the listing competes nakedly on $14 vs. $4.

## 6. The PPC layer

Per [SalesDuo 2026](https://salesduo.com/blog/cost-of-amazon-advertising/) and [Trellis benchmarks](https://gotrellis.com/resources/blog/amazon-advertising-benchmarks/), Beauty CPC averages $1.50–$3.00 with high-competition keywords ($2.50–$5+). For our keyword set, expect **$1.20–$2.80 CPC** (the Thai-inhaler subcategory has lower competitive density than mainstream beauty).

### 90-day PPC plan ($8K from D_channel allocation)

| Campaign | Type | Targets | Daily budget | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Owned-brand defense** | Sponsored Products, manual exact | brand name, brand+misspellings | $10 | Reserve brand SERP |
| **Category exact** | Sponsored Products, manual exact | "Thai herbal inhaler," "ya dom," "menthol inhaler stick," "nasal inhaler aromatherapy" | $40 | Rank-build on primary terms |
| **Long-tail broad/phrase** | Sponsored Products, broad + phrase | the §3 long-tail list | $25 | Discovery of search-term gold |
| **ASIN targeting (competitor)** | Sponsored Products, product targeting | Poy-Sian B01FLJN7I6, Siang Pure B00MKVYCX8, Hong Thai (when listed), Olbas B00014DMG4, Tiger Balm Inhaler | $20 | Steal consideration traffic |
| **Sponsored Brands video** | once Brand Registry active | category-exact terms | $15 | Above-fold real estate |

**Total daily: ~$110 → ~$3.3K/month → $10K over 90 days** (slightly above the $8K allocation; cap to $90/day if budget-constrained).

ACoS targets:

- Month 1: 60–80% (rank-build phase, accept loss-leading)
- Month 2: 40–55%
- Month 3: 25–40% (TACoS — total ad cost as % of revenue — should trend below 20% by month 3 if organic is compounding)

Pacing rule: **harvest search-term reports weekly**, promote any term with 2+ orders to a manual exact campaign, negative-match any term spending >$15 with zero orders.
