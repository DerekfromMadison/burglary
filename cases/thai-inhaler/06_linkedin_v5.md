The most useful agent was the user.

Posted a LinkedIn business request from a founder. Ran it through 54 sub-agents and ~4M tokens — 10 viewpoint analyses, red team, deep dives, second-pass red team, steelman + reviewer, deliverable + reviewer, 10-viewpoint critique batch, 13 marketing-strategy agents, 5 gap-finders, 1 gap-filler.

What I produced through v5: a polished, internally contradictory plan that priced the product four different ways across four sub-docs, demanded 71–90 hr/wk of solo founder time, claimed "100 customers at <$50 CAC" against actual cash CAC of $140, and assumed Amazon Brand Registry could clear in 90 days when standard USPTO is 8–10 months.

What corrected it: the user pushed back. *"This is trying to identify product market fit. What the f*** are you talking about getting all these patents and identities and trademarks?"*

Three fixes the AI couldn't see on its own:
1. **Lead with marketing strategy, not regulatory compliance.** The founder asked for differentiation; AI led with FDA classification.
2. **The economics didn't pencil and AI smoothed it over.** Honest 90-day P&L is −$18,800 contribution at break-even cohort ~1,600 customers. v6 reframes from "launch plan" to "Phase-0 measurement instrument with Day-75 kill-switch on Sean Ellis <30%."
3. **The single highest-ROI line item was missing entirely.** USPTO IP Accelerator petition, $100 fee, unblocks Amazon Brand Registry inside 90 days. v1 through v5 missed it.

Two takeaways for anyone running multi-agent analysis:

1. **AI viewpoint diversity ≠ AI calibration.** 35 sub-agents producing convergent recommendations can still be uniformly wrong about the framing question. The user is the one who knows what was asked.

2. **Gap-finding is not the same as red-teaming.** Red teams attack the synthesis. Gap-finders find what's missing entirely. v5 needed both, ran in waves, and v6 is a different document because of it.

The LinkedIn series is 1 of 5 cases. DM if you have a real business problem worth ~4M tokens of structured argument.
