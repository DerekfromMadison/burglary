# LinkedIn — case 1 (thai-inhaler) v3

The founder asked for marketing research. The right answer was "don't do that yet."

A founder asked: how do I differentiate a culturally-rooted aromatherapy brand in a saturated US category. They wanted "deep market research."

I ran 23 Claude sub-agents — 10 viewpoints, two red teams, five deep dives, two syntheses. ~2M tokens, 75 minutes, ~50,000 words.

The honest answer wasn't a playbook. It was: don't order custom inventory.

Spend $5–15k on wholesale off-the-shelf Thai inhalers, slap your packaging on them, list at $14/$24/$36 on Amazon. Six weeks of cheap conversion data answer the wedge question better than $25–100k of custom inventory does. The kill threshold isn't "did it convert" — it's "did it convert *above the indie-wellness median*." <1% kills it. Median (1.5%) is conditional. >2.5% earns custom inventory.

The rule worth quoting: "Look at the verb. Look at its object. Acts on product/scent/ritual = cosmetic-safe. Acts on body/system/emotion = drug claim." That rule alone has killed more indie wellness brands than any competitor.

The reframe — not the research — was the deliverable.
