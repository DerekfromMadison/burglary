# thai-inhaler — Deliverable v4

## The Recommendation

**Stop. Reformulate to drop camphor before any other action.** A menthol+camphor nasal inhaler is an OTC drug under 21 CFR 341 monograph M012 (Vicks Vapor Inhaler precedent), NOT a cosmetic. v3's MoCRA-cosmetic framing was contradicted by the molecule list. You have three paths:

1. **Reformulate (drop camphor; keep menthol/eucalyptus/borneol/Thai herbs)** → cosmetic path. Cheap and fast. **Recommended.**
2. **Keep camphor** → OTC drug path: NDC + FEI + CGMP + Drug Facts label. ~$25–50k Y1 compliance. **Skip unless $250k+ capital.**
3. **Drop the inhaler form entirely** → roll-on/balm/spray. Loses the form-factor wedge.

Pick #1.

**Then, in this order:**
1. **File USPTO trademark (1b) + enroll Amazon Brand Registry/Transparency** before any public listing. ~$1.5k.
2. **$1k Phase -1 landing-page test** (7 days, no inventory): paid traffic to a fake PDP at $24 anchor. Email capture <8% or price tolerance <30% → kill the wedge.
3. **Brand identity sprint, Week 1**: visual territory (recommend luxury-modernist), name shortlist (USPTO-cleared), scent brief, 1 mockup. ~$3–5k. Without this, Phase 0 tests bad packaging and mis-reads results.
4. **Phase 0 validation** ($5–10k, 6 weeks) — Shopify-only (drop Amazon — Brand Registry is a multi-week wall), cue-anchored creative ("before your 2pm meeting") A/B against benefit-anchored ("morning glimmer"), recalibrated above-median thresholds.
5. **Supplier strategy revised**: target OEM blank-label (Medicos-tier, $1.50–2.50/unit) NOT Poy-Sian principal-brand. Pursue principal-brand exclusive licensing as a Year-2 move. Realistic Thai supplier timeline: **9–12 months** — decouple from Phase 0.
6. **Honest path-bifurcation**: this is a **bootstrap indie cash-flow business**, not venture-fundable. CPG seed bar (2025–26): $1–3M revenue + meaningful wholesale. Plan for $25–50k angel money on relationship terms; revenue funds growth.

**If you do one thing this week:** call cosmetic/OTC FDA counsel for a 1-hour intake on the camphor-drop reformulation question. ~$500. Everything downstream depends on this.

## The 60-Second Reasoning

The original v3 plan would have shipped a fundamentally mis-scoped compliance posture (cosmetic for a product that's a drug), wrong supplier tier (brand principal not OEM), an impossible Thai partnership timeline (60 days vs. real 9–12 months), and an undesigned product through a $5–15k Phase 0. Each of those errors alone wastes the budget; together they would have killed the brand. Multi-agent red-team caught all four.

The strategic wedge (1-second daytime sensory reset, bundle-led $24+ AOV, premium-positioned vs. drugstore $4 yadom) survives intact. What changed is the validation sequence and the regulatory framing — the wedge gets cheaper, faster, and legally feasible.

- Phase -1 ($1k) tests the wedge BEFORE inventory.
- Reformulation (drop camphor) preserves the cosmetic compliance path.
- OEM tier replaces principal-brand equity ambition — speed and cost win.
- Brand identity moves to Week 1 — Phase 0 can't validate an undesigned product.
