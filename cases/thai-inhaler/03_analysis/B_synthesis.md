# B — Cross-viewpoint synthesis (Round B)

Source: 10 Round-A viewpoint files (`A_cmo.md`, `A_customer.md`, `A_competitor.md`, `A_pr.md`, `A_domain.md`, `A_risk.md`, `A_skeptic.md`, `A_cfo.md`, `A_futurist.md`, `A_data.md`).

## Convergences (where viewpoints agree)

1. **Founder identity is the load-bearing variable.** 7 of 10 lenses (CMO, Customer, Competitor, PR, Domain, Skeptic, CFO indirectly) explicitly flag founder ethnicity / Thai equity partner as the single fact that determines whether half the recommendations apply. The brand's PR slot, premium-pricing legitimacy, and competitive moat all bifurcate on it. Without confirmation, recommendations must be written as conditional pairs.
2. **Authentic Thai inhalers are already on Amazon US at $3–$5.** Poy-Sian Mark II, Siang Pure, Hong Thai, Hanuman, Tiger Balm Inhaler — multiple ASINs cited (Skeptic, Domain, Customer). Any new brand must justify a 2–7× premium with something other than "we are also Thai-herbal."
3. **The dominant white space is "1-second nervous-system reset / focus / glimmer," NOT "calm / sleep / aromatherapy."** Domain, CMO, and Futurist independently land here. The saturated killing fields are "calm" (Saje, doTERRA, Vitruvi own it) and "cold/sinus" (Vicks owns it). Daytime sensory state-shift, the actual job of yadom in Thailand, is open.
4. **Bundle / AOV economics are structural, not optional.** CFO's model: $8 single-tube fails against $30+ realistic CAC. Viable configuration is $14 single / $24 2-pack / $36 starter set with curated samplers. Customer's PDP analysis confirms: the brand-asset spend is for the bundle decision, not single-SKU acquisition.
5. **FDA classification is set by claim language, not molecule.** Risk, Skeptic, and Domain converge: MONQ LLC (June 2022 warning letter), Young Living (2014), doTERRA (2014, 2017), 2023 FTC distributor case all targeted *language*, often in the affiliate / influencer channel. Strict cosmetic positioning under MoCRA is the only feasible compliant path at indie scale; OTC monograph (Vicks Vapor Inhaler precedent) is too expensive for a sub-$250k launch.
6. **TikTok organic is necessary but no longer sufficient.** Post–USDS restructure (Jan 2026), measurable virality compression. Futurist + Customer agree: owned channels (SMS, newsletter, members club) must be built from day 1. Any acquisition plan with TikTok > 40% of CAC is a 2024 plan.
7. **Cultural-authenticity discourse is binary.** PR, Customer, Skeptic agree there is no middle ground. Either the credible-carrier playbook (founder narrative + named Thai supplier + supplier equity) lands, or the brand becomes an appropriation target within the first 90 seconds the press touches it. Diaspora Co. / Fly By Jing / Omsom are the working precedents; Lucky Lee's, Wildling, the spa-water brand are the cautionary ones.
8. **Saje is the only existential threat among incumbents.** Competitor isolates Saje as the brand with permission, retail footprint (250+ Ulta doors), capital, and zero inhaler-stick SKU yet — they can crash-launch a "Peppermint Halo Inhaler" in 90 days and outspend on creators 10:1. Vicks (P&G) and the indie aromatherapy field are weaker threats individually.

## Contradictions (where viewpoints conflict)

1. **Position vs. compliance tension.** CMO recommends "Nervous-System Regulator" positioning. Risk says state / function claims trigger drug classification. **Reconciliation**: sensory-allusion language ("the 1-second reset," "your morning glimmer") that conveys *state experience* without making *health claims*. The exact safe-harbor phrases need a Round D dive.
2. **"Shelf already lost" vs. "form-factor wedge is real."** Skeptic argues Poy-Sian / Siang Pure / Hong Thai own Amazon US for "Thai inhaler" search. CMO + Domain argue the form factor is functionally superior to roll-ons (1s vs. 5–10s ritual; no oily residue; daytime-discreet) for the daytime-state-shift JTBD that no incumbent owns. **Reconciliation**: by segment. Drugstore-replacement segment is lost. Premium-nervous-system-regulator segment is open and well-fit.
3. **Premium vs. authenticity.** Domain insists yadom is a $4 7-Eleven product in Thailand and "elevating" it breaks the thing. Customer says anchor below $24. CFO says $24+ AOV is structural. **Reconciliation**: bundle-led, not single-tube-led. The first SKU communicated is the $24–$36 starter set; the $14 single is the trial entry; refills/subscriptions handle replenishment. Premium per-unit price is avoided; premium per-order AOV is achieved through curation.
4. **Specialty retail vs. DTC-first vs. Amazon-first.** CFO: Whole Foods is cheapest CAC channel (50% off MSRP, foot-traffic free acquisition). Customer: Amazon Prime + one credible specialty retailer are trust-proxy non-negotiables. Competitor: creator → Amazon is the actual discovery loop. **Reconciliation**: Amazon + 1 Erewhon-tier specialty retailer for trust + DTC as brand asset (not revenue engine) — this is consistent across all three views once the role of each channel is parsed correctly.
5. **GLP-1 framing relevance.** Futurist treats GLP-1 normalization (~25M users by 2030) as a category-bifurcating force ("GLP-1 companion" vs. "Faux-zempic naturals"). Other viewpoints don't engage. **Reconciliation needed in Round D / E**: assess whether the brand must take a stance on GLP-1 by 2027–2028.

## Gaps (what no viewpoint covered well)

1. **Confirmed founder identity.** Every viewpoint flagged this; none can resolve it. Action: deliverable must include explicit bifurcation; founder must answer before final go/no-go.
2. **Empirical 90-day repurchase rate for sensory-inhaler CPG.** No public benchmark. CFO + Data flag this; only resolvable post-launch with cohort tracking.
3. **Cold-ad-to-purchase conversion rate at Meta/TikTok.** Unknowable from analysis. Data prescribes a fake-PDP test as the cheapest first signal.
4. **Whether sensory-only (non-functional) positioning moves volume.** The CMO–Risk tension can only be resolved by A/B test. Data's $1000-experiment plan addresses this.
5. **Specific FDA-safe phrasing for the nervous-system-reset position.** Risk lays out the matrix but doesn't enumerate the exact verbal substitutions a brand can deploy. → Round D dive.
6. **Tariff / supply-chain vulnerability.** CFO flagged a 19% reciprocal Thai tariff (~$0.15/unit COGS impact) but didn't dive into mitigation paths (US-side blending, alternate sourcing, retail-shelf-stable shelf life, contract-manufacturer alternatives).

## Highest-stakes findings (ranked)

1. **Strategic wedge: "1-second daytime nervous-system reset / focus glimmer," bundle-led ($24+ AOV) at premium pricing, with FDA-safe sensory-allusion language.** Aligned with Futurist's polyvagal-vocabulary forecast, Domain's correct read of yadom's actual use, CMO's segmentation. Differentiates from Saje (calm/sleep) and Vicks (cold/sinus). **Confidence: high.** Conditional on founder identity (#2) and language survivability (#4 below).
2. **Founder identity determines strategy, not just messaging.** Thai-American or Thai equity partner → credible-carrier playbook (Diaspora Co. / Fly By Jing / Omsom analogues). Neither → reposition off the heritage frame entirely (sensory-only, ingredient-led, or pivot the brand idea). The deliverable cannot finalize without this. **Confidence: very high; unresolved.**
3. **Form-factor wedge is real and Saje cannot credibly counter without contradicting its existing line.** 1-second sensory action vs. 5–10s roll-on; no oily residue; pocket-discreet for the "between-meeting reset" use case Saje doesn't speak to. **Confidence: high.**
4. **FDA compliance is the kill-switch.** One warning letter → Amazon delist + Shopify flag + retailer pull. Cosmetic positioning under MoCRA is the only feasible compliant path at indie scale. The Round D dive on FDA-safe language is non-negotiable for the deliverable. **Confidence: very high.**
5. **Bundle / AOV economics ($24+ blended AOV) is the only configuration that survives realistic CAC.** Engineered replenishment (refills, subscriptions, ritual kits) is structural. Single-tube DTC at $8 is uneconomic. **Confidence: very high.**
6. **Saje is the existential 90-day threat.** Founder must move fast on creator seeding + Erewhon-tier placement + supplier-equity narrative to lock the slot before Saje crash-launches a competitor SKU. **Confidence: medium-high.**
