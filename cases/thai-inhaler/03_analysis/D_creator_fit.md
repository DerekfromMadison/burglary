## 1. Creator archetype taxonomy (10 archetypes)

Ten archetypes relevant to the "1-second nervous-system reset / focus glimmer" positioning ($24+ AOV, FDA-safe sensory-allusion). Leverage concentrates in three. Economics reflect 2026 norms — health/wellness affiliate commission averaged 16.4% on TikTok Shop; wellness gifted ROI ran $7.25/dollar (beauty/skincare $11.40) per GRIN/SeedingOps.

### Archetype 1 — Nervous-system / polyvagal educator
- *Description*: Clinically credentialed creator (psychiatrist, somatic practitioner, trauma therapist) translating polyvagal theory. Vocabulary: co-regulation, vagal tone, glimmers, ventral state.
- *Named examples*: Dr. Aimie Apigian (@draimie, 28K IG, "Biology of Trauma" author); Brittany Piper (somatic coach, Substack-led); Dr. Mariel Buqué (~250K IG); Deb Dana (small audience, enormous citation gravity).
- *Size / engagement*: 25K–500K. 4–8% engagement. Saves and shares dominate.

- *Format*: 60–90s mechanism explainer ("why a cooling sensation engages the trigeminal-vagal reflex"), product cameo as artifact. *Epistemology*, not haul.
- *Economics*: Most won't take paid posts (CE-credit board liability). Gifted + Substack mention. When paid: $400–$1.5K sub-100K, $2K–$6K mid-tier. Affiliate uptake 15–25%. Take as authority capital, not direct attribution.

### Archetype 2 — Thai-American / AAPI heritage lifestyle creator

## 2. Highest-leverage 3 archetypes for thai-inhaler

Picking three under realistic budget. Criteria: (a) reaches price-tolerant Persona A / Segment 1 with consistent ROI, (b) multiplies cultural-authenticity defense, (c) compatible with FDA-safe language.

### Pick 1 — Nervous-system / polyvagal educator (Archetype 1)
Most aligned with the secular bet (Futurist "Glimmer companion" thesis, GWS 2026 neurowellness frame). A Dr. Aimie–tier post *makes the brand defensibly premium*. Without it, the brand competes with $11.99 Plant Therapy on Amazon SEO. With it, the brand prices like Saje and gets the Erewhon meeting.

### Pick 2 — Thai-American / AAPI heritage creator (Archetype 2)
The cultural-authenticity moat. Persona C rejection produces a Bon Appétit-tier blowup. Pailin-tier seeded gifts + supplier-story partnerships = cheapest insurance available. Below her, the leverage is in the 5K–50K Thai-American micro band — chef-aunties, second-gen food bloggers, Thai-mom creators on Lemon8.

### Pick 3 — ADHD / focus / executive-function creator (Archetype 3)
Contested pick. Defense: reaches the unvoiced male persona — lowest-cultural-risk, easiest-to-acquire buyer. Activates the "focus glimmer" half of the wedge. Without ADHD creators the brand collapses to "another calm SKU" against Saje's own ground; ADHD creators *flank* Saje.

**Vs. alternatives**: Mom-wellness anchors price at $14–18 (the death zone `A_cmo.md` flagged) — Phase 2. ASMR drives views, not first-purchase — month 4+. Biohacker is highest-claim-risk — sequence after Series A.

## 3. The seeding strategy

### (a) Targeting list size
Build a **300-creator tier-segmented list** month 0: 50 polyvagal / 60 Thai-AAPI / 50 ADHD / 40 mom / 30 ASMR / 25 acupuncturist / 20 biohacker / 15 fragrance / 10 travel. Source via Modash, Heepsy, Favikon + manual hashtag scrapes (#nervoussystemregulation, #ยาดม, #adhdtok, #glimmers). Filter for >3% engagement, >60% US audience, no doTERRA/Young-Living MLM history, no FDA-flagged content.

### (b) Gifting protocol
Send a **3-pack starter set** in branded box (~$12 COGS gifted, ~$8 fulfillment), with: (1) handwritten card (the 40% post-rate lift per 2026 SeedingOps benchmarks isn't a myth); (2) one-page **language card** — FDA-safe phrases the creator can use and trip wires they can't (the most underused seeding asset in wellness); (3) supplier story (named Thai herbalist, photo, region); (4) no CTA to post (no-expectation gifting outperforms pay-for-post by 1.4× post-rate per Modash 2026).

### (c) Realistic conversion at three list sizes
Per 2026 wellness gifting benchmarks:
- **50-creator seed**: 12–18 organic posts (24–36% post-rate); 1–2 semi-viral; $3K–$8K direct revenue. MVP signal.
- **200-creator seed**: 50–80 posts; 4–8 semi-viral; $20K–$60K. Right size for focused launch.
- **500-creator seed**: 110–180 posts; 8–15 semi-viral; 1–2 viral; $80K–$220K. The "$50K to spend" tier.

The diminishing return between 200 and 500 is offset by *cumulative cultural density* — at 500 mentions across nervous-system / Thai-heritage / ADHD niches in 60 days, the brand becomes "the brand my algorithm is showing me."

### (d) When to escalate to paid
Trigger paid only after two organic seed cycles. Specifically: a creator who organically posts and generates >$500 tracked affiliate → escalate to $1.5K/mo retainer (2 posts/mo). A creator with >100K views → one paid post within 30 days while algorithm still favors them. Never lead with paid — `A_customer.md` flagged obvious paid-influencer content as a Persona A churn trigger.

### (e) Affiliate program structure
Mirror Olipop's documented hybrid: $36 samples + 10% commission, scaling to 15% above $1K/mo, 20% above $5K/mo. Olipop's program drives 12% of total sales at 982% ROI. At $24 AOV, 10% = $2.40/sale — breakeven only with >2× repeat. Tier commission against repeat-cohort, not first-purchase.

## 5. Risks

### (i) Post-USDS TikTok algo (Jan 2026)
USDS restructure introduced federal auditing reducing organic virality for unverified accounts (`A_futurist.md` Force 4). Creator content at <50K loses ~30–50% pre-2026 reach. Mitigation: weight seed list toward 50K–500K mid-creators; diversify to Reels (which absorbed 30–50% of compressed creator effort per Miraflow 2026).

### (ii) FTC #ad enforcement
FTC issued formal actions against 15 influencers/brands in H1 2025 alone (40% YoY); penalties $10K–$100K+; AI scanning for missing #ad tags; wellness explicitly named in 2026 guidance. Mitigation: pre-cleared #ad copy in every brief; branded-content tag mandatory on Instagram; separate disclosures per platform; legal review of every paid post >$500.

### (iii) Creator fatigue with wellness seeding
Polyvagal-niche creator receives ~40 seed boxes/month. Generic seeding ROI decaying. Mitigation: language card + supplier story + handwritten note + Thai-cultural anchor. The brand's gift is *content material*, not a sample.

### (iv) FDA enforcement risk in influencer/affiliate (Young Living / doTERRA precedent)
Existential. 2014 FDA letters (Young Living, doTERRA) for Ebola/cancer/Alzheimer's claims by *distributors*; 2020 FTC letters for COVID claims; 2023 FTC-DOJ wins against doTERRA distributors. Pattern: company doesn't make the claim, the affiliate does, company is held responsible. For an indie Thai-inhaler at sub-$250K capital, one warning letter = Amazon delist + Shopify flag + retailer pull = brand death.

Mitigation: (1) Mandatory creator brief with say/don't-say sheet — *don't say*: anxiety, depression, ADHD, headache, migraine, insomnia, congestion, cure, heal, treat, prevent, reduce; *say*: a moment of calm, my between-meeting reset, the smell that grounds me, my pocket ritual, the cool breath. (2) Affiliate TOS requiring claim-compliance with auto-clawback. (3) Quarterly compliance scan (AspireIQ, Modash compliance dashboard). (4) **No biohacker creator at launch** — highest-risk vocabulary category.
