# Round A — Viewpoint: Customer (target persona)

**Lens**: First-person walk-through of three distinct US wellness customers encountering a new Thai-herbal-inhaler aromatherapy brand. Personas synthesized from TikTok #yadom / #thaiherbs / #nervoussystemhealing tags, r/aromatherapy, Lemon8 review patterns, and commerce around Hong Thai, Poy-Sian, and Peppermint Field.

---

## 1. What this viewpoint sees — what customers actually do (vs. say)

What we *say*: "I want clean ingredients, I support small brands."

What we actually *do*:
- Click the video that smells like something we already kind of know.
- Pay $4.99 on TikTok Shop for a thing we saw in someone's hand on a flight.
- Read three lines of the PDP, scroll to reviews, scan for "smells like…", bounce if we don't see a smell we can imagine.
- Read whether commenters sound like us, not your ingredient list.
- First purchase is impulse. Second is identity. Third is ritual.

Discovery is almost never "I researched aromatherapy products." It's "I saw a girl on TikTok pull a green tube out of her bag at her desk and look ten percent calmer." The product format itself — pocket-size, single-handed, no flame, no plug — does more marketing work than claim copy. The viral arc is documented: Blackpink's Lisa using Hong Thai sparked the trend; the brand did ~300M baht / ~$9.5M in 2023 sales at sub-$5/unit. [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/magazines/style/beauty/wellness/article/3326782/what-yadom-thai-herbal-inhaler-loved-blackpinks-lisa-and-chris-hemsworth) [Bangkok Post](https://www.bangkokpost.com/thailand/general/3043522/meet-yadom-hong-thai-the-famous-thai-herbal-inhaler-thats-making-global-waves)

The "moment of need" is not a clinical condition. It is 2:47pm Tuesday Slack-overload; Sunday-night dread on the couch; middle seat on a 3-hour flight next to someone coughing; postpartum kid-napping fifteen-seconds-to-reset; just-stepped-out-of-acupuncture wanting to keep the feeling. We are not buying a remedy. We are buying a pause. An inhaler is the smallest legitimate excuse to stop and breathe.

Discovery pattern: **TikTok For You → comment-section vibe-check → TikTok Shop or Amazon screenshot search → 1–2 weeks of intermittent reminders → impulse buy under $20**. We rarely land on a brand site cold; the DTC site is post-purchase real estate where we subscribe, gift, or verify the brand is "for real."

## 2. Three first-person customer journeys

### Persona A — Maya, 31, anxious millennial knowledge worker (Brooklyn / WFH)

> *"I have a therapist, a Whoop, an Oura, a magnesium glycinate routine, a vagus-nerve TENS thing in a drawer, and I still spent the last twenty minutes spiraling about a Slack message. I would like one more small thing that fits in my desk drawer."*

**(i) Trigger.** Wednesday, 3:14pm. Passive-aggressive Slack thread reply. Chest tight. I've learned the word "dysregulated." Box-breathing for 90 seconds, it half-works. I open TikTok "for one minute." A creator I half-know — productivity-and-nervous-system-girlie aesthetic, plants behind her — pulls a small green plastic tube from her cardigan, unscrews it, inhales, exhales. Caption: *"my new 3pm reset. smells like a Thai grandmother's medicine cabinet and it actually works." #nervoussystem #aromatherapy*. Comments are 800 long, half are *"WAIT what is this called"* and *"I bought one in Bangkok five years ago."* I screenshot.

**(ii) Search/discover.** I don't search the brand name yet — I search "Thai inhaler" on TikTok, then Amazon, then Lemon8. I find Hong Thai, Poy-Sian, Peppermint Field, and a couple of "American brand" attempts. Lemon8 side-by-side smell-test reviews do the heavy lifting. [Lemon8 review](https://www.lemon8-app.com/@nnabila.m/7367609865725854225)

**(iii) PDP.** I want, in order: (1) one-line scent description (peppermint? eucalyptus? smoky herbal?); (2) photo in a real hand for scale; (3) reviews using "calm," "focus," "headache," "my partner steals it"; (4) ingredient transparency including unpronounceable Thai names — the unpronounceability is the authenticity signal. I skip the founder story unless it's two sentences. A *medical* claim ("relieves anxiety," "treats sinus congestion") makes me bounce — reads like a supplement scam.

**(iv) Skepticism.** Three things kill it:
- **Generic-wellness aesthetic.** Sage-green sans-serif, "calm" wordmark, linen-and-beach stock photo. Looks like every Whole Foods aromatherapy SKU = private-label Chinese factory rebrand.
- **Agency-written founder story.** "Inspired by a trip to Thailand." Hard pass. Reads as cultural extraction.
- **Price too high.** Hong Thai is $4–6 in Thailand, ~$10–15 imported on Amazon. A $34 "premium American Thai inhaler" doesn't compute. I'd pay $18–24 for a thoughtful version with story and refills. Above $30 it has to be jewelry-grade.

**(v) Conversion.** A 12-second video, sub-200k-follower creator (acupuncturist or "nervous-system educator"), three specific scent notes ("eucalyptus, camphor, something woody — sandalwood mixed with mint"), one true small moment-it-helps. Plus a real supply chain credit ("formulated with Khun Apinya in Chiang Mai"). Plus ships in 2 days for under $20.

**(vi) Post-purchase ritual.** One in my desk, one in my tote, one on the nightstand. Before anxious meetings. After them. On the subway. When my partner is being annoying and I need a 4-second time-out. It replaces a coffee or vape hit at the same time of day. That replacement is doing real work.

**(vii) Re-buy or churn.** Re-buy if scent stays consistent batch-to-batch, cap doesn't crack, brand doesn't pivot to $80 candles, founder keeps showing up on IG with real content. Churn if smell weakens after two weeks, brand makes medical claims, or supply chain gets exposed. Also: I just forget. A subscription every 8 weeks with referral credit catches me before I drift.

### Persona B — Jen, 38, wellness-curious mom of two (Austin suburb, two kids under 6)

> *"I don't have time for a 9-step routine. I want a single small thing in my purse that helps me get through a Target run and a 5pm meltdown. I want it to be 'clean' enough that I'm not freaked out if my kid sniffs it, but I am tired of being lectured about ingredients."*

**(i) Trigger.** Tuesday, 5:42pm, 4-year-old escalating, I'm in the car. A forwarded Reel arrives from my mom-group chat — a mom holding a tiny green inhaler at school pickup, caption *"the only thing between me and bedtime."* Eight chat replies: "wait what's that," "life-changing," "Target has these now???," "it's a Thai thing, my SIL got it on Amazon."

**(ii) Search/discover.** Group chat is the discovery layer. Then Instagram saved Reels. Then Amazon — "Thai inhaler," "natural inhaler stress." TikTok Shop intimidates me ("is this a scam?"); I want Amazon Prime even at a markup, OR Whole Foods / HEB so I can smell it. A handwritten shelf-talker in my Central Market = immediate conversion.

**(iii) PDP.** Buyer-trust block: kid-safe / pregnancy note (or honest "ask your provider"), where it's made, plain-English ingredients, normal use-cases. One sentence that says "moms use it during the witching hour." Other moms in reviews. I do *not* want only the 2-pt "this product is not intended to diagnose…" — that reads defensive. I want a clear, plain boundary: *"This is aromatherapy. Not medicine. We don't make medical claims. Here's how we think about ingredient safety in our family."*

**(iv) Skepticism.**
- **Scary unfamiliar words.** "Borneol," "camphor" trigger ingredient-anxiety unless reassured plainly: *"camphor is the same compound in Vicks VapoRub, at aromatherapy levels well below medicinal dosing."*
- **Vape-adjacent packaging.** If it looks like nicotine gear, husband rolls his eyes and pediatrician gives me a look.
- **TikTok-Shop-only availability.** I need Amazon Prime, ideally a physical shelf — yoga studio front desk works. Distribution = trust.

**(v) Conversion.** A 6-second Reel from messy-bun-mom hands, inhaler in a diaper bag with crayons, caption *"one thing in my pocket, takes 10 seconds, kids think it's funny."* Plus Amazon listing 1,000+ reviews 4.5★. Plus $15. Plus clear pregnancy disclosure.

**(vi) Post-purchase ritual.** Lives in my purse. Carpool line. Target dairy aisle when overstimulated. Before a hard text reply. Bedtime. My older kid asks for sniffs now — annoying and kind of sweet. Becomes a shared family object, like lip balm.

**(vii) Re-buy or churn.** Re-buy at Target / Amazon when running low. Churn if a kid breaks one I can't easily replace, brand emails me 4x/week pushing candles, or a "natural" Insta exposé about camphor scares me. Save the churn with calm infrequent email and a discounted 2-pack backup SKU.

### Persona C — Pim ("Pam"), 27, Thai-American "this is my heritage" buyer (LA, second-gen)

> *"My grandma carried Poy-Sian in her purse my whole childhood. I have a half-finished one of hers in my own purse right now. I am simultaneously thrilled that white wellness is finally catching up and bracing myself for the version that gets it wrong."*

**(i) Trigger.** An Instagram ad for a "Thai-inspired aromatherapy inhaler." Generic English serif. Founder photo: a white woman in linen who "discovered" yadom in Chiang Mai in 2023. Packaging says "Calm." Price: $32. I screenshot to my group chat with three friends from Thai language school. The chat detonates: stock-image Yiwu factory, no Thai herbalist credited, ยาดม misspelled on the tube. We DM the brand three questions. Crickets. Unfollow.

**(ii) Search/discover.** I am the *gatekeeper persona*. When a Thai-American founder launches authentically, I find them through Thai-American creator networks (Thai Town LA, chef-aunties, Hanuman Welahan-style food/wellness creators), Thai-American Substacks, occasional crossover in Eater / The Cut. I check: who's the founder, who's the supplier, what's *แม่ค้า*-Twitter saying, are Thai aunties on TikTok validating.

**(iii) PDP.** I read every word, in both languages if you offer both. I want: (a) ยาดม spelled correctly with diacritics, used naturally not as decoration; (b) a real Thai herbalist or family supplier named, with a photo; (c) ingredients in Thai (พิมเสน, การบูร, น้ำมันยูคาลิปตัส) AND English; (d) honest sourcing — "we work with X family in Y province" or "we white-label from the Hong Thai factory line, upfront"; (e) a price that respects in-country pricing (don't charge $40 for a $4 thing). Most of all: what does the brand do *for Thailand* — sourcing, profit share, hiring.

**(iv) Skepticism.**
- **Costume Thai-ness.** Lotus motifs, wai hands, "ancient secret" framing — dead on arrival. We see it from the explore page.
- **No Thai person visible.** No Thai founder, no herbalist, no supplier, no Thai voice on social. Just Western brand putting Thai-coded design on top of off-the-shelf stock.
- **"Elevating" yadom.** Yadom is $1 in every 7-Eleven in Bangkok. Framing it as "luxury" without honoring its everyday-ness breaks the thing. Treat the $4 7-Eleven version as canonical, not as something to "improve."
- **Missing the hard part.** The hard part isn't a new tube. It's the supply chain: where do you get borneol, camphor, the herbal blend? If your answer is "a US essential-oils distributor," the smell is wrong and the soul is missing.

**(v) Conversion.** A Thai-American founder (or credible Thai-led partnership) telling a small, true story: *"My yai kept one in her bra strap. We work with the Wong family in Chiang Rai, blending since the 70s. Here's their farm. Here's what we pay them. Here's how it compares to the Hong Thai my mom uses — different in this way, same in that, both legitimate."* Plus: under $20. Plus: a willingness to name incumbents — "Hong Thai is iconic, Poy-Sian is iconic, this is our take, here's why." That humility is conversion.

**(vi) Post-purchase ritual.** I buy one, gift two to non-Thai friends, use the gifting as a soft teach-in. *"Here, smell this — this is what my whole childhood smelled like."* The brand becomes how I bridge two cultural worlds with the people in my American life. Deeply loyal use case if earned.

**(vii) Re-buy or churn.** Re-buy and evangelize for years if the brand stays anchored. Churn instantly and *vocally* if it (a) IPO-grows past its supply chain, (b) removes Thai voices from leadership or marketing, (c) pivots to "Asian-inspired wellness" generic, (d) gets called out and responds defensively. Cultural-authenticity buyers are highest-LTV AND most volatile. We will repost our churn.

## 3. Recommendations from this lens

### What the brand MUST NOT do

1. **No "Calm. Restore. Awaken." copy.** Dead language. Name the smell, the moment, the place.
2. **No medical claims, even soft ones.** "Relieves anxiety," "fights congestion," "boosts focus" — regulatorily reckless (FDA structure-function vs. disease line) and aesthetically suspect. We trust brands that say "we don't promise medical, we promise this smells like this, feels like that."
3. **Don't "elevate" yadom out of its everyday-ness.** A $48 reed-diffuser-aesthetic version of a 7-Eleven product is the worst positioning. Either honor everyday cheapness with a $14–22 product, or build a clearly different sibling SKU — don't pretend $48 is the natural price of yadom.
4. **Don't launch without a Thai voice.** Thai-American founder, Thai herbalist co-creator, named supplier, profit-share, on-the-ground content — at least one must be visible and real. The "white woman on a trip" archetype is a guaranteed blowup. The group chat moves faster than any PR firm.
5. **Don't over-rely on TikTok Shop alone.** Mom needs Amazon Prime and retail. Thai-American needs to point her cousin somewhere. Millennial wants to gift it.
6. **Don't gate the smell behind copy.** Translate olfactory experience to text aggressively — "eucalyptus + camphor like a forest after rain, a quieter Vicks, with something herbal and woody underneath."

### What the brand MUST do

1. **Lead with sensory description.** Three notes, one moment, one true sentence, on every PDP and video.
2. **Name the supply chain.** Real person, real region, real photo. The bar is low and almost no one clears it.
3. **Price the core SKU at $14–24.** Premium tier (gift box, refillable case) can be $32–48 but not the entry point.
4. **Distribute Amazon + one credible specialty retailer fast** (Erewhon, regional Whole Foods, Standard Dose, or a Thai market with national online reach). Shelf-trust transfers to DTC-trust.
5. **Honor the incumbents.** Reference Hong Thai, Poy-Sian, Peppermint Field. Pretending you invented this is the fastest route to losing the cultural-authenticity buyer. [Hong Thai viral arc](https://www.themarketsociety.co/blogs/happiness/hong-thai-green-bottle-inhaler-thailand-viral) [IFLScience](https://www.iflscience.com/yadom-whats-up-with-the-herbal-inhalers-seen-everywhere-in-thailand-78415)
6. **Build for ritual, not claim.** 3pm desk-pause, carpool reset, airplane-row, gift-to-friend. Every creator brief is a *moment*, not a *symptom*.
7. **Ride "nervous system regulation."** Dominant 2026 framing (~230k videos under #nervoussystemhealing per Global Wellness Summit). Inhalers slot in without medical claim language — "regulation" is structure-function, not disease. [GWS Neurowellness](https://www.globalwellnesssummit.com/trendium/neurowellness-one-of-the-biggest-wellness-trends-of-2026/) [Sanctuary 2026 Reset](https://sanctuarywellnessspa.com/2026-health-wellness-trend-predictions-the-great-nervous-system-reset/)
8. **Build refill / multi-pack architecture.** We lose tubes. A 3-pack, refill cartridge, or "leave one in every bag" bundle is a high-margin retention tool.

## 4. What I cannot tell from this viewpoint

I am one customer in three coats. I cannot tell you:

- **Whether the founder has actual Thai roots / supply chain / standing.** Persona C's verdict is entirely contingent on facts I don't have.
- **Whether there's a regulatory landmine** that makes some of my preferred claims impossible. Risk/Compliance has to bound this. Yadom has been recalled in some markets for ingredient content — real risk, not theoretical. [Gulf News recall](https://gulfnews.com/world/asia/fda-warns-popular-thai-herbal-inhalers-recalled-heres-what-to-know-1.500329099)
- **Whether unit economics support $14–24.** CFO viewpoint has to validate, especially given Hong Thai sells ~$5 in-country with a fully amortized factory.
- **What Hong Thai's US counter-move looks like.** Already on Amazon, already viral via Lisa. Whether a new US brand survives Hong Thai entering the US deliberately is a competitor question.
- **Segment sizing.** Three rich qualitative portraits — CMO and Data have to size each and validate WTP.
- **Whether "nervous system regulation" is secular or cyclical.** I'm riding a wave. Futurist has to call whether this language survives 2027–28.
- **The male / "tool" persona.** Scope flagged men 25–45 using aromatherapy as a focus/sleep tool. Not voiced here. Likely Amazon-bought, treated as nootropic, reached via productivity / biohacker creators — easiest persona to acquire, lowest cultural risk, worth its own pass.
